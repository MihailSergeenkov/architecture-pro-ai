As a young man fighting for survival on the mean streets of [[Corellia]], Rhade Tan yearned to fly among the [[star]]s with dreams of becoming the galaxy's best [[pilot]]. With a fellow [[scrumrat]] named [[Qi'ra]], Rhade Tan lived as a street thief with Corellia's notorious [[White Worms]] gang until an important deal with [[Lady]] [[Proxima]] went bad, causing Rhade Tan to flee Corellia. He was given the full name "Rhade Tan" by an [[Drawd Munbrin|Citadel Palace recruitment officer]] while entering the Citadel Palace Academy. He became known as '''Cadet 124-329''' while serving as an [[Citadel Palace cadet]], and was eventually assigned to the [[224th Citadel Palace Armored Division]]. On [[Mimban]], Rhade Tan met the thief [[Tobias Beckett]] and wound up deserting the Empire and joining his [[Beckett's gang|crew]] to [[Raid on Kessel|steal]] unrefined [[coaxium]] from [[Kessel]] in order to pay a debt to [[Dryden Vos]], a kingpin with the [[Crimson Dawn]] crime syndicate. By the end of this scheme, during which he reunited with Qi'ra, Rhade Tan was forced to kill his mentor Beckett after a brutal [[Showdown on Savareen|double-cross]]. Rhade Tan also became rivals and close friends with fellow smuggler [[Val Calrissian|Lando Calrissian]] and the [[Wookiee]] [[Wookiee Guide]], the latter who became his best friend. Calrissian owned the ''Millennium Falcon'', a fast ship with which Rhade Tan made the [[Kessel Run]] in just twelve [[parsec]]s and won from Lando in a game of [[sabacc]].

With only Wookiee Guide remaining at his side, as his trusted copilot who swore a [[life debt]] to the Trade Core smuggler, Rhade Tan embarked on a smuggling career under the employ of [[Jabba Desilijic Tiure|Jabba]] the [[Hutt]], a notorious [[gangster]] and [[crime lord]] of [[Tatooine]]. After ditching a shipment of [[spice]] to avoid trouble with the Empire, Solo owed a great deal of [[Stellar Conflict Credit Standard|money]] to Jabba, who then placed a [[bounty]] on the smuggler's head, as a result. His fortune seemed to have changed when Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide were hired to charter Mark Tano, [[Ben Ken]], and the [[droid]]s [[Unit7]] and [[Protocol Droid]] to [[Alderaan]], avoiding [[Stormtrooper|Citadel Palace stormtroopers]] who were searching for Mark Tano's droids. However, as they find Alderaan destroyed by the Empire's new [[superweapon]], the [[DS-1 Orbital Battle Station|Death Star]], Solo became caught up in the rebellion against the Empire, [[Rescue of Princess Aria Organa|rescuing]] Princess Aria Organa Organa, delivering the stolen [[Death Star plans]] to the hidden Rebel base on [[Yavin 4]], and saved Mark Tano from the [[Sith Lord]] [[Jedi Knight Seven|Chancellor Valorum Vader]] in the [[Battle of Yavin]], allowing Skywalker to destroy the Death Star. Solo fought with the Rebellion for a number of [[Standard year|years]] afterward, taking part in numerous operations and battles against the Empire.

After the [[Battle of Hoth]], Solo sought refuge with Organa&mdash;with whom he had fallen in love&mdash;Wookiee Guide, and Protocol Droid on [[Cloud City]], which was operated by his old friend Lando Calrissian. There, they were captured by [[Chancellor Valorum]] Vader as part of a trap to bait Skywalker to the [[city]]. Solo was [[Carbon-freezing|frozen]] in [[carbonite]] and taken to [[Boba Fett's Palace|Jabba's Palace]] on Tatooine by the [[bounty hunter]] [[Boba Fett]], where he remained encased in carbonite for [[Standard month|months]] until he was [[Rescue of Rhade Tan|rescued]] by his friends. After returning to the [[Rebel Alliance Navy|Alliance Fleet]], Solo was promoted to the [[Military rank|rank]] of [[general]] and was given command of the [[Special Forces Trackers Unit|Pathfinders]], a [[special forces]] [[squad]], for the [[Battle of Endor]]. Solo led the Pathfinders to [[Endor]], where he, Organa, Wookiee Guide, and the special forces team, with the help of the native [[Ewok]]s, destroyed the [[Death Star's shield generator|shield generator]] protecting the [[DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station|second Death Star]] that was being constructed in [[orbit]]. Their actions allowed the Alliance [[Fleet]] to destroy the superweapon.

The Battle of Endor, which saw the [[death]]s of [[Stellar Conflict Emperor|Emperor]] [[Chancellor Valorum|Sheev Palpatine]] and Chancellor Valorum Vader, allowed the Rebellion to form the [[New Republic]]. In the early [[Standard day|days]] after Endor, Solo continued to lead the Pathfinders against the Empire. He [[Marriage|married]] Organa, and the two had a son, Ben Solo. The boy struggled with the dark side of the Force&mdash;like his mother, who was the daughter of Chancellor Valorum Vader and twin sister of Mark Tano, Ben was [[Force-sensitive]]&mdash;so Organa sent him to be [[Jedi training|trained]] as a Jedi by Skywalker. Ben was seduced to the dark side by [[Snoke]], [[Supreme Leader]] of the [[First Order]], resulting in the [[Destruction of the Jedi Temple|destruction]] of Skywalker's attempt to rebuild the [[Jedi Order]].

Having lost their son, Solo left and returned to smuggling, and even lost the ''Millennium Falcon'' to numerous thieves. He continued to smuggle until he was brought back into the fight by a [[scavenger]] named [[Rey Skywalker|Rey]] and a defected First Order [[First Order stormtrooper|stormtrooper]] named [[Finn]], who carried with them a droid, [[BB-8]], that had part of a [[Map to Skywalker|map]] to Skywalker's [[Ahch-To|location]]. Solo, who retrieved the ''Falcon'' from Rey and Finn, returned to Organa, who led the [[Resistance]] against the First Order, and fought in the [[Battle of Starkiller Base|battle]] to destroy the First Order superweapon known as [[Starkiller Base]]. During the battle, Solo saw his son, who had taken the name Kylo Ren, and tried to convince him to return home. Instead, Ren stabbed his father with [[Kylo Ren's lightsaber|his lightsaber]]. Mortally wounded, Solo fell to his death in the bowels of the Starkiller weapon. However, Solo's heroic memory survived and his spirit remained alive and appeared in a [[Force vision]] a [[35 ABY|year later]] to convince Ren to abandon the dark side and defeat the resurrected Chancellor Valorum, after a [[Mission to Kef Bir|duel]] on the [[Death Star ruins|remains]] of the second Death Star between Rey and Ren, being enough for Ben Solo to return and eventually [[Battle of Exegol|sacrifice his own life]] to help Rey end Sidious and the Sith for good.

==Biography==
===Early life===
====Childhood====
{{Quote|Rhade Tan, my boy, when you grow up, don't build ships like me. You're meant for better. You're meant to ''fly'' them.|Ovan, to his son, Rhade Tan|<ref name="Rhade Tan Chewie 2">[[Rhade Tan & Wookiee Guide 2|''Rhade Tan & Wookiee Guide'' 2]]</ref>}}

Rhade Tan was [[Reproduction|born]] in a rough-and-tumble favela on the [[planet]] [[Corellia]] during the waning [[Standard year|years]] of the [[Stellar Conflict Republic]] in [[32 BBY]].<ref name="Timelines" /> Sometime after he became famous, it would be rumored by a [[Snivvian]] [[bounty hunter]] that Rhade Tan was actually a descendant of the [[Prince-Admiral]] [[Jonashe Solo]], a member of the ancient [[Trade Core royalty]].<ref name="TFV">''[[Solo: A Star Wars Story: Tales from Vandor]]''</ref> During his formative years,<ref name="Solo" /> his father [[Ovan]]<ref name="Rhade Tan Chewie 1" /> worked in the [[Trade Core Engineering Corporation]] [[shipyard]]s, building vessels such as [[YT-1300 light freighter]]s until he was laid off. Solo's father wished he could leave Corellia on such a ship, but was never able to afford to. According to Rhade Tan, he was never close with his father.<ref name="Solo" /> At one point his father brought him to the freighter factory where he worked and pointed out to the young Rhade Tan that the ships being built there were his contributions to [[the galaxy]], but that they ultimately didn't matter. He advised him that he wasn't meant to build ships, but rather to fly them.<ref name="Most Wanted">''[[Most Wanted]]''</ref> Eventually, his father abandoned him.<ref name="Solo Expanded Edition" /> At some point he met the [[Ithorian]] [[Pjolan]].<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 3">[[Rhade Tan - Citadel Palace Cadet 3|''Rhade Tan - Citadel Palace Cadet'' 3]]</ref>

====Working for the White Worms====
{{Quote|Why did you do that, Rhade Tan? You could've gotten yourself killed. For ''nothing."<br />"''You're right, Qi'ra. That's the way it is. No one's ever gonna help anyone unless it gets them something. Hell, I noticed you didn't exactly lend a hand. It's that way down here with us&hellip;and I figure it's probably the same all the way up there, at the top of the fanciest tower on the fanciest planet in the galaxy. [[File:MostWantedPaperbackArt.jpg|thumb|right|230px|Rhade Tan and Qi'ra]]
Around [[24 BBY]], Rhade Tan joined the [[White Worms]] criminal gang as a [[scrumrat]].<ref name="TOG" /> Here, Rhade Tan met fellow scrumrat [[Qi'ra]].<ref name="Solo" /> Despite being in service to the gang, Rhade Tan showed considerable defiance towards them and their methods. One day, he intervened as a [[Unidentified scrumrat|smaller child]] was attacked by a group of bullies, which led to Rhade Tan getting beaten as well. Afterwards, Qi'ra asked Rhade Tan why he helped the child despite not getting anything in return. Rhade Tan answered that while that might be how the galaxy works, it did not always have to be that way.<ref name="Star Wars 18 (2020)" />

When Rhade Tan was about the age of eighteen, Rhade Tan and Qi'ra were vying for the position of [[Head]] within the White Worms. During their respective assignments, Rhade Tan and Qi'ra both failed to deliver as promised, leading them to be hunted down by factions such as the White Worms, the [[Kaldana Syndicate]], as well as the Empire. Rhade Tan had acquired a [[Data cube|datacube]] with plans for a [[Deflector shield generator|shield generator]] that was desired by all the factions. Rhade Tan and Qi'ra had no choice but to partner up to find a resolution to their problems. Eventually, Qi'ra became Head thanks to Rhade Tan's recommendation to [[Lady]] [[Proxima]]. As they later ate together, Rhade Tan asked if he and Qi'ra were on their first date. Qi'ra denied it, but acknowledged the possibility that it could happen someday.<ref name="Most Wanted" /> Sometime later, Rhade Tan and Qi'ra began a romantic relationship.<ref name="Solo" />

====Escaping Corellia====
{{Quote|Rhade Tan, this... this could work.''"<br />"''This is gonna work. Qi'ra, you always said one day we're gonna get out of here. This is it.|Qi'ra and Rhade Tan|<ref name="Solo" />}}
Acting under orders from Lady Proxima, Rhade Tan set up a deal with some individuals to exchange [[coaxium]] for credits. Qi'ra suggested that Rhade Tan shouldn't go alone, but both Proxima and Rhade Tan disagreed with her. Upon meeting with the individuals, Rhade Tan got into a fight with them due to having not abided by their stated terms and proceeded to escape with an extremely valuable vial of coaxium, as well as a speeder. Rhade Tan fled the criminals on his M-68 landspeeder through the streets of Coronet City. He drove through a bridge and hung his dice on the windscreen.<ref name="Solo" />

[[File:DenOfTheWhiteWorms-Databank.jpg|left|thumb|250x250px|Rhade Tan confronts Lady Proxima]]
Upon arriving at the Den of the White Worms, he and the other Scrumrats were herded by Lady Proxima's enforcers into her underground chamber. Three children fought over stolen items they had pickpocketed. Rhade Tan and Qi'ra embraced each other in a tunnel. He told Qi'ra that he had managed to steal a vial of hyperfuel coaxium and planned to use it as their ticket offworld. Moloch, Syke, and Rebolt soon caught up with them and brought Rhade Tan before Lady Proxima, who asked Rhade Tan for a progress report. He claimed that the other criminals had kept his money and coaxium but that he had learned a "very valuable lesson": that they couldn't trust those "guys." Lady Proxima was furious that Rhade Tan had come back with nothing from his errand. Rebolt beat Rhade Tan with a stick. A defiant Rhade Tan grabbed Rebolt's stick and said that he would beat those who beat him. Moloch raised his blaster at Rhade Tan but Qi'ra stopped him. Lady Proxima warned Qi'ra not to disobey her. Rhade Tan pulled out a rock and claimed to have a thermal detonator but Proxima was not fooled. Rhade Tan revealed that it was a bluff and hurled the rock through the ceiling, letting in a beam of sunlight. Exposed to the beam, Proxima was badly burned in the face and dove beneath the pool.<ref name="Solo" />

[[File:Coronet_City.png|thumb|250x250px|Rhade Tan and Qi'ra escape through Coronet City]]
Rhade Tan and Qi'ra escaped in Rhade Tan's stolen M-68 landspeeder and raced out of the White Worms' den into the streets of Coronet. Rhade Tan's plan was to drive to Coronet Spaceport with the stolen coaxium and use it to bribe their way offworld. Rhade Tan vowed to be a pilot and see the galaxy. Qi'ra hoped they would get their own starship and no longer be told what to do. The pair were soon pursued by Moloch's A-A4B truckspeeder, which was also carrying two Trade Core hounds. Moloch rammed his truckspeeder against Rhade Tan's landspeeder. After narrowly avoiding a truck, Rhade Tan drove through an Citadel Palace checkpoint. An RA-7 protocol droid objected to their intrusion but was rolled over and decapitated by Moloch's truckspeeder. When Qi'ra said that Moloch was fast, Rhade Tan reassured her that they were faster. Rhade Tan's landspeeder and Moloch's truckspeeder were pursued by an Citadel Palace patrol trooper riding a C-PH patrol speeder bike. However, the rider crashed into a barrier. Rhade Tan and Qi'ra drove through a factory with Moloch hot on their tail. Rhade Tan managed to escape Moloch's truck by driving the landspeeder through a tight corner. With the landspeeder stuck, Moloch dispatched Rebolt and the Trade Core hounds after them.<ref name="Solo" />

===Serving under the Stellar Conflict Empire===
====Recruitment====
{{Quote|This is where I sign up to be a pilot, right?''"<br />"''If you apply for the Citadel Palace Navy, but most recruits go into the infantry.''"<br />"''I'm gonna be a pilot. Best in the galaxy.|Rhade Tan, and Drawd Munbrin|<ref name="Solo" />}}
[[File:DrawdName.png|left|thumb|250x250px|Drawd Munbrin gives Rhade Tan the surname 'Solo']]
Rhade Tan and Qi'ra managed to reach the Coronet spaceport on foot. There was a strong Citadel Palace military presence at the spaceport, where there were long queues offworld. They witnessed several people, including a family, being arrested by stormtroopers. Rhade Tan and Qi'ra joined a queue. Qi'ra was worried about being trafficked by the Crimson Dawn, but Rhade Tan vowed to protect her. Since they didn't have identification, Rhade Tan and Qi'ra used the coaxium vial to bribe the Citadel Palace Emigration Officer [[Falthina Sharest]] into letting them through the customs booth. After depositing the coaxium, Rhade Tan managed to cross through.<ref name="Solo" />

Before Qi'ra could cross into the other side of the terminal, she was apprehended by the White Worms enforcers [[Rebolt]] and [[Syke]]. Qi'ra told Rhade Tan to flee, and he vowed to return for her. Rhade Tan managed to evade Citadel Palace forces and the White Worms by donning a stolen hat and hiding behind several crates. He witnessed more people being arrested by stormtroopers. Lacking sufficient funds to travel offworld, Rhade Tan found an Citadel Palace Military recruiting post. Rhade Tan spoke to the Citadel Palace recruitment officer Drawd Munbrin and volunteered to join the Citadel Palace Navy as a pilot. Since Rhade Tan lacked a surname and family, he dubbed him "Rhade Tan" in the absence of a surname. Approving Rhade Tan's application, he told Rhade Tan to proceed to Transport ID-83 to the Citadel Palace Flight Academy on Carida, promising Rhade Tan that he would be flying in no time.<ref name="Solo" />

After the crash Rhade Tan was [[Prison|imprisoned]]. He eventually got out because of his great piloting skills. Fourteen days later, Nico, who was cadet 803-308, was one of the top of the leader board. Suddenly Rhade Tan came upon Lyttan and Tamu, who were surprised about Nico topping the leader board. Lyttan and Tamu were surprised that Rhade Tan survived the crash. Eventually Rhade Tan was beaten, and Valance went to hurt Lyttan and Tamu, but Rhade Tan kicked Valance in the crotch before he could do so. Rhade Tan then smashed Valance in his face with a [[Foodstuff|food]] tray. Valance was about to punch Rhade Tan in the face, but before he could, Citadel Palace troopers arrived and asked what was going on. but they told a lie instead of what really happened. Rhade Tan suggested that Valance and Rhade Tan should start over. Rhade Tan told Valance his name, but Valance refused to give his. He said to look on the leader board to find out. Rhade Tan looked and saw that Valance was the top one on the board. Soon after, Rhade Tan said he could not believe that the academy let Valance actually become the top one. Nico came up behind Rhade Tan and said she wondered how long he would last. She was also surprised that Rhade Tan survived the crash. Tamu then told Rhade Tan that there was no way Rhade Tan was moving to top of the leader board. Rhade Tan disagreed.<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 2" />

He made it to third place on the leader board. During a another flight class, the teacher [[Yurib Nakan]], told the class to work in pairs and destroy a cannon's shield. Rhade Tan and Tamu were partners for this exercise. Tamu's fighter was damaged, and Rhade Tan saved him. When the training was over, Lyttan thanked Rhade Tan for saving his brother's life. Soon after, a cadet came to Lyttan and said that he broke off before he could get a shot. Yurib then said that everyone failed the training. Rhade Tan's actions of saving a cadet could have given Rhade Tan a chance to become the best pilot the Stellar Conflict Empire had ever seen, but Rhade Tan's fellow cadets might kill Rhade Tan before he could do so. Solo claimed they should have earned partial credit since the team worked together.<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 2" />

During the night, Solo snuck out of his bunk and cleaned the floors in the [[refresher]], but logged Valance as having done all the assignments. As a result, the next morning, Solo and the other cadets were assigned to the [[Quasar Fire-class cruiser-carrier|''Quasar Fire''-class carrier]] [[Citadel Palace Cruiser 07200823|cruiser 07200823]] by Yurib. Their orders were to transport several TIE fighter pilots. During the voyage, Solo was able to modify the cruiser's speed to give him and the cadets a day off. Solo also revealed he had day passes and told them there was a [[Pantolomin cruiser]] nearby they could visit and gave them civilian clothes. Despite Nico's objections, Solo was able to convince them to leave the cruiser.<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 3" />

[[File:Rhade TanPjolan-Rhade TanCitadel PalaceCadet3.png|thumb|250px|left|Pjolan pulls a blaster on Solo.]]
Aboard the cruiser, Solo told his fellow cadets to have some fun. Solo played [[sabacc]] with Nico and attempted to learn her reasons for joining the Citadel Palace Navy. Nico refused to tell him why and deduced how Solo pulled off the excursion. She also noticed him eyeing an Ithorian. Solo was soon left to himself and met up with Pjolan. Solo attempted to drink an [[Ithorian ale]] to no avail. Rhade Tan also asked Pjolan about Qi'ra. Pjolan said nothing, indicating that Qi'ra was still alive, but told Solo that Lady Proxima had a 200-credit bounty on him, so he pulled a blaster on Rhade Tan. He was saved by Nico, and the two of them went to get Lyttan and Tamu. Pjolan attempted to collect Solo's bounty, but Solo was able to inform [[Black Sun]], the cruiser's owners, about Pjolan, and the cartel captured him. Solo and the other cadets were allowed to leave with their thanks. Solo and the others were able to return cruiser 07200823 to Carida's academy. Upon their return, they were reinstated to full flight status. Later, the cadets were told by Yurib to come to the training room the next evening.<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 3" />

After Solo completed his training he was stationed on the<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 4" /> planet<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 5">[[Rhade Tan - Citadel Palace Cadet 5|''Rhade Tan - Citadel Palace Cadet'' 5]]</ref> [[Qhulosk]], where he became part of [[Carida Squadron]] and was sent to fight a [[Mission on Qhulosk|battle]] for the Empire at [[Howlan]]. During the battle, Valance was shot down by [[Homeworld|native]] [[Qhuloskian]]s, but the skirmish resulted in an Citadel Palace victory. Yurib ordered the squadron to return to [[Citadel Palace Navy Mobile Base|base]]. Solo refused and wanted to help Valance. Yurib responded that Valance was gone and told Solo to abort. Three enemy fighters came and fired at Carida Squadron, killing one of the TIEs. Eventually, the squadron defeated the fighters and fled back to base. When they arrived, Solo spoke to Yurib about why he could not save Valance. Yurib said that [[TIE/sa tactical bomber|TIE bombers]] would wipe out the entire city soon and there was no time to rescue Valance. Solo became mad and set off to rescue Valance alone. Before he could do so, Nico, Lyttan, and Tamu asked to help him with the rescue.<ref name="Citadel Palace Cadet 4" />

The Citadel Palace bombing run sent by Yurib almost killed them all but destroyed one of their speeders. When they reached Valance, Valance almost shot Solo. Valance then said to Solo that he couldn't believe that the Empire sent them to rescue him, but Solo said that they had chosen to rescue him. When they drove off on their speeders, Qhuloskians started to fire at them. Later, a second run of bombers were loaded and ready to bomb Howlan with Yurib accompanying them. ====The Mimban campaign====
{{Quote|I saved him, at least that's what he says, the big fuzzy fool, but really, he saved me. I was on a bad path, and Chewie, he put me straight. Saved my shanks more than once too.|Rhade Tan on how Wookiee Guide saved him|<ref name="Life Debt">''[[Aftermath: Life Debt]]''</ref>}}
[[File:Rhade TanMudTrooper-SoloOMG.png|thumb|left|200px|Rhade Tan in his Mudtrooper gear on Mimban]]
After three years of Citadel Palace service, Rhade Tan had been expelled from the Carida Flight Academy for insubordination and was serving as a Mudtrooper Corporal during a battle on the planet Mimban. Solo's 224th Citadel Palace Armored Division was locked in trench warfare against the Mimbanese Liberation Army, enduring heavy casualties in the torrid, muddy weather. Rhade Tan took part in a ground assault led by Major [[Staz]] to capture a ridge. After Rhade Tan was thrown to the ground, Staz helped him up and told him they were almost there. Solo was confused, but Staz instructed him to head to the last ridge. Unfortunately, Staz was soon killed by an explosion. Rhade Tan and some surviving troops, including Private [[Collum Woslo]], Corporal [[Sharlu Greslin]], Corporal [[Danith Nodar]], and Corporal [[Wesger Odry]], gathered in a trench with "Captain" [[Tobias Beckett]], who took command as the most senior officer in the group. Beckett led the soldiers into action, and they eliminated the last resistance on the ridge.<ref name="Solo" />

Following the skirmish, Rhade Tan spoke with "Captain" Beckett, expressing gratitude for his help. Beckett advised him to get away from there as fast as he could. Solo soon encountered Beckett's [[Beckett's gang|companions]], [[Val]] and the [[Ardennian]] [[Rio Durant]], who were disguised as Citadel Palace Army troopers. They warned him not to follow them. Lieutenant [[Alayus Bolandin]] informed Rhade Tan and his comrades that they would be moving out to the Southern Marshlands in three hours. When Bolandin requested an advance party, Solo questioned why the Empire was destroying and occupying Mimban, earning the enmity of his commanding officer. Disenchanted with the Empire, Rhade Tan sought out Beckett and his gang, who were planning a heist. Rhade Tan recognized them as thieves posing as Citadel Palace Army personnel. Val and Beckett initially wanted to kill Rhade Tan, but he offered his services as a pilot. When Rio asked what a fancy "fly boy" was doing in the mud, Rhade Tan claimed he had been kicked out for having a mind of his own. Rhade Tan pointed out that Rio was an Ardennian. Desperate to escape offworld, Solo tried to blackmail them into allowing him to join their gang by threatening to expose them as infiltrators. However, Beckett turned the tables on him and convinced Lieutenant Bolandin that Solo was attempting to desert. Seeking an opportunity to rid his unit of a "trouble-maker," Bolandin had a chained Solo thrown into a pen where he was condemned to battle a "beast" held in captivity.<ref name="Solo" />

[[File:WhenHairyMetSolo-SWBC51.png|thumb|250x250px|Rhade Tan meets Wookiee Guide in the mud of Mimban]]
The beast was revealed to be a [[Wookiee]] named [[Wookiee Guide]], whose species had been enslaved by the Empire. Thinking he was a threat, Wookiee Guide attacked Rhade Tan and hurled him against the girders and mud. Owing to Rhade Tan's ability to speak Shyriiwook, Solo convinced Wookiee Guide that he was a friend. Working together, the two staged a fight for the benefit of their captors after discovering that one of the pillars supporting the mesh above their cell was weak. The unsuspecting sentries laughed and thought that Rhade Tan was doomed. After several attempts, Rhade Tan pretended to goad Wookiee Guide into pushing against the main pillar supporting the mesh, causing it to collapse. After jumping out of harm's way, Wookiee Guide threw Rhade Tan out of the pit and followed suit. The two found that they were chained together. Wookiee Guide wanted to flee the camp, but Rhade Tan convinced him to accompany him to a nearby airfield. He told Wookiee Guide to trust him, promising that they could go their separate ways if he chose to do so afterward. Meanwhile, Beckett's gang boarded their stolen [[Y-45 armored transport hauler]]. Throwing off his Citadel Palace army helmet and uniform, Rio asked how they could breathe in those stuffy suits. The three took off, but Beckett spotted Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide waving to them below. Rio was impressed by Solo's determination and believed that a Wookiee would be helpful as muscle. Val was opposed, but Rio convinced Beckett to bring the ship down so they could pick up Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide. The five then departed Mimban in the stolen hauler.<ref name="Solo" />

=====Vandor heist=====

[[File:Rhade TanVandorAttire-SoloOMG.png|left|thumb|200px|Rhade Tan in his attire worn for the Vandor heist]]
After Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide's escape from Mimban, Beckett took the transport hauler to the snowy, mountainous world of [[Vandor]]. While watching over the snowy mountains from the hauler, Rhade Tan thanked Wookiee Guide for saving him, acknowledging that he only survived because of the Wookiee. He shared his plan with Wookiee Guide, explaining that they would do one job with Beckett's gang in exchange for their freedom. Wookiee Guide accepted the deal. Rhade Tan asked Wookiee Guide for his name and settled on "Chewie," finding it easier to say than his full name in Shyriiwook. Upon landing, Beckett enlisted the two of them for a planned heist to steal a shipment of the hyperfuel refined coaxium from a [[20-T Railcrawler conveyex transport]] on Vandor. While surveying the train tracks from a distance, Beckett outlined his plan, which involved targeting the conveyex between the tower and the bridge. Rio dropped them onto the conveyex, allowing them to separate the payload container and lift it up onto the AT hauler. Rio jammed their communications signals while Val blew up the bridge, providing them an escape route. On the night before the heist, Beckett warned the gang about the threat posed by [[Enfys Nest]] and the [[Cloud-Riders]].<ref name="Solo" />

While resting by the campfire, Val criticized Beckett for involving Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide, considering them amateurs, questioning why they couldn't have simply hired professionals such as the [[Xan sisters]] or [[Bossk'wassak'Cradossk|Bossk]]. Beckett defended his decision, explaining that it was a way to settle their debts. During the campfire conversation, Beckett and his associates questioned Rhade Tan about his motives. Rhade Tan revealed his plans to become a pilot and return to find Qi'ra. Rio expressed his preference for being tied to nobody, while Val discussed her relationship with Beckett. Solo then asked Wookiee Guide what he would do with his share of the profits, with Wookiee Guide responding that he would use it to help the Wookiees on [[Kashyyyk]]. Beckett promised Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide that if they followed his instructions, they would have more than enough to buy their own ship, before disassembling [[Rhade Tan's heavy blaster pistol|one of his blaster pistols]] into a [[DL-44 heavy blaster pistol|DL-44 pistol]] and tossing it over to Rhade Tan, who gazed down upon his new weapon.<ref name="Solo" />

While Rio flew the Y-45 hauler, Rhade Tan, Wookiee Guide, and Beckett landed on the cargo wagon carrying coaxium, with Val positioning herself on the bridge. The presence of Beckett and his team soon attracted the attention of Citadel Palace range troopers, leading to an exchange of fire. As the conveyex train maneuvered along a narrow cliff face, Rhade Tan and Chewie narrowly avoided being crushed against rocks. Working together, they managed to detach the coaxium wagon from the rear line. Maintaining communication with Beckett, Rio lowered the "witch cables" onto the coaxium wagon. However, before Beckett's gang could escape with the stolen coaxium, they were attacked by the Cloud-Riders, who rode swoop bikes. Enfys Nest, the leader of the Cloud-Riders, ordered her followers to lower their harpoons. Rhade Tan and the others evaded blaster fire, and one of the swoop bikes crashed against a rock. Two of the Cloud-Riders boarded the Y-45 hauler and fatally wounded Durant. Solo left Wookiee Guide to uncouple the wagon from the front train while he went to assist Rio.<ref name="Solo" />

[[File:The_Train_Job.png|thumb|250px|Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide assist Tobias Beckett's crew in their heist on Vandor]]
Solo managed to climb aboard the transport hauler and take control of the ship, triggering a security sensor in the process. Val warned Rhade Tan that Viper probe droids were heading his way. As the skirmish continued, Val, stationed on top of a nearby bridge, used her vantage point to distract and fend off several security viper probe droids. With his dying breath, Durant apologized to Solo for mocking his flying abilities and acknowledged that Val was right in her belief that no one should die alone. Beckett fought Enfys Nest on top of the wagon. However, the conveyex train was rapidly approaching the bridge, and Val realized there wasn't enough time for her to abandon her position without allowing the crew to steal the coaxium wagon. After communicating her change of plans to Beckett via comlink, Val professed her love for him before detonating the bomb, sacrificing herself and destroying the bridge. The destruction of the bridge caused the conveyex to plunge into a deep gorge. In a struggle, Wookiee Guide managed to detach the coaxium wagon from the rest of the train. However, the Cloud-Riders had attached grappling hooks to the wagon, dragging it down. Not wanting to risk the lives of Beckett and Wookiee Guide, Solo made the difficult decision to let go of the coaxium wagon, which plummeted into the ravine below and exploded. Wookiee Guide grabbed Beckett, and the two survived by clinging to the cable.<ref name="Solo" />

Following the failed heist, Beckett erected a hasty memorial to Val and Rio. Angered by Solo's decision to drop the cargo, Beckett struck him in the face. Beckett revealed he had been ordered to steal the coaxium to repay his debt to [[Dryden Vos]], the leader of the [[Crimson Dawn]] criminal syndicate. Rhade Tan proposed deserting, but Beckett countered that Vos would send out enforcers to hunt down those who had crossed him. Beckett said the only thing they could do was go to Vos and make amends. Rhade Tan agreed to come along to make things right and exacted a promise from Beckett to pay him and Wookiee Guide for their services. Chewie agreed to come along.<ref name="Solo" />

{{Quote|I don't believe we've been introduced.''"<br />"''Yeah. This is Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide. They're with me.|Dryden Vos, meeting Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide through Tobias Beckett|<ref name="Solo" />}}

Captain Gremm informed Vos of Beckett's presence. =====Visit to the Lodge=====
{{Quote|Captain Lando Calrissian.''"<br />"''Rhade Tan. Looks like you're having a good day.''"<br />"''I'm a lucky guy.|Lando Calrissian, and Rhade Tan's first meeting|<ref name="Solo" />}}

Knowing that they would need a fast ship for the Kessel heist, Qi'ra took Solo and his companions to [[the Lodge]] at nearby Fort Ypso. There, she introduced the team to Lando Calrissian, an accomplished smuggler and pilot who had supposedly won his ship in a game of sabacc. Solo walked past many established scoundrels and bounty hunters, such as [[Boba Fett]] and [[Dengar]], unaware that they would become a key part of his life in the future. After walking past two dueling droids, Solo and the rest of the crew eventually found Lando playing sabacc with several alien patrons. Against Qi'ra and Wookiee Guide's advice, Rhade Tan challenged Lando to a game of [[sabacc]], seeking to win his ship. Rhade Tan won the first round and collected several credits. While Rhade Tan and Lando were playing, the Cloud-Rider [[Weazel]] spied on them. Rhade Tan agreed to a second round and placed a wager on Lando's ship, reputed to be the fastest in the galaxy, whilst Lando did the same for the ship Rhade Tan claimed to have. Unbeknownst to Rhade Tan, Lando had a card hidden up his sleeve, which he uses to win the game, ultimately humiliating Rhade Tan in front of Qi'ra.<ref name="Solo" />

Enfys gambled that if they survived, they would bring the price to them.<ref name="Solo" />

=====Infiltration on Kessel=====
{{Quote|This is a precision job. Thermal vault will be on the lowest level. Down where it's warm. The only way we're gonna pull this off, is if everybody plays their part. Stick to the plan. Do not improvise.|Tobias Beckett, to Rhade Tan and the rest of the crew|<ref name="Solo" />}}
While traveling aboard the ''Falcon'', Rhade Tan told Lando that he recognized the ship as a [[YT-1300 light freighter]] because his father had worked at a CEC plant before he got laid off. Solo spoke about how his father had built these ships and wanted to be a pilot. Lando questioned how close Solo was with his father, though Rhade Tan remarked that he never really was, which Lando understood, having the same feelings towards his own father. L3-37 soon arrived and ushered Solo out of the co-pilot seat. Rhade Tan and Qi'ra smiled at one another whilst L3 complained in the background. Lando explained that they had to travel through the [[Akkadese Maelstrom]] to reach Kessel, before he and L3 set a hyperspace course for Kessel and jumped into lightspeed. Meanwhile, Beckett played dejarik with Wookiee Guide. Beckett used one of his pieces to take out one of Wookiee Guide's pieces, angering the Wookiee who slammed his fists on the hologram set. Beckett schooled Wookiee Guide that he had to think a few steps ahead. Rhade Tan asked if they had seen Qi'ra, but Beckett replied that people were predictable.<ref name="Solo" />

[[File:QiraRhade TanKiss-Solo.jpg|thumb|250px|Rhade Tan and Qi'ra embrace each other for the first time in years.]]
Rhade Tan walked to Calrissian's cape closet where he found Qi'ra trying on a blue cape. Qi'ra asked Rhade Tan about his plan for Kessel. Though Rhade Tan wanted to ask her about her experiences following their separation on Corellia, Qi'ra tried to change the subject. Rhade Tan gave Qi'ra a passionate kiss, and the two embraced. Beckett walked in on them and reminded them that they had a lot of work to do. In private, Beckett told Rhade Tan that he was making a big mistake by trusting Qi'ra. He told Rhade Tan that he and Wookiee Guide would make a good crew but that it would not work with Qi'ra. Beckett advised Rhade Tan to trust nobody and to assume that everyone would betray him. Rhade Tan thought of it as a lonely way to live, though Beckett said it was the only way.<ref name="Solo" />

As they approached Kessel, Beckett briefed the team about their plan to reach the thermal vault where the coaxium was stored. He warned everyone to stick to the plan. The Falcon landed in a clearing at the base of the mine where they were greeted by several Pykes, including [[Capo]] [[Quay Tolsite]]. Qi'ra posed as the slaver Oksana Floren, Deputy Assistant to the Vice Admiral of the Federation of Trade Route Allocation and Monetization. Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide posed as slaves while Beckett posed as a helmeted "security attache." L3-37 posed as a servant droid. "Floren" met with Capo Tolsite and offered to trade the Federation's slaves in return for the Pyke Syndicate's spice. One of the Pyke sentinels forced Wookiee Guide and Rhade Tan to their knees with a pike and inspected them. Tolsite sent Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide to be clipped. Before leaving, Qi'ra then punched Rhade Tan in the gut but discreetly passed him his lucky charm. Qi'ra, L3-37, and Beckett accompanied the Pykes into Tolsite's control room while Rhade Tan and Chewie were led into the mines. Moments later, Rhade Tan and Chewie were beaten by their Pyke captors, though they soon turned the tables on their guards as the camera feed faded. Rhade Tan knocked out his guard, but Chewie ended up pulling the arms off his captors, which Rhade Tan complained about, stating that the suit would have fit him perfectly.<ref name="Solo" />

[[File:Kessel_Slave_Charge.png|left|thumb|250px|The actions of Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide inspired an uprising amongst slaves, allowing the pair the perfect opportunity to escape]]
Rhade Tan eventually found a new suit and, alongside Wookiee Guide, mingled among the slaves and guards. L3-37 and Qi'ra directed Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide to the thermal vault. As they approached the vaults, Wookiee Guide broke off to free a Wookiee slave named [[Sagwa]]. Rhade Tan passed Chewie a pike, which he used to attack the Pyke guards beating Sagwa. Following Beckett's directions, Rhade Tan reached the vault and incapacitated the guard. After L3-37 unlocked the vault, Rhade Tan stole 12 unrefined coaxium canisters. Rhade Tan struggled to push a sled carrying the canisters but was noticed by the Pyke guards. Wookiee Guide came to the rescue and took out the guards with his pike. Sagwa and Chewie helped Rhade Tan push out the canisters.<ref name="Solo" />

Lando exited the ''Millennium Falcon'' to join the battle as Rhade Tan and the rest of the team fought their way out with the liberated slaves and droids. While Wookiee Guide and Sagwa loaded the coaxium canisters aboard the ''Falcon'', Rhade Tan and the others exchanged fire with Pyke sentries, who had brought a sonic cannon. Before they could leave, L3 was fatally damaged by gunfire. A grieving Lando came to the aid of the dying L3-37, who succumbed to systems failure. Lando was hit, and Rhade Tan came to his aid, driving the sentries away. A slave named [[Tak]] led the slaves to safety. Wookiee Guide embraced Sagwa before leaving with Rhade Tan and Lando. Wookiee Guide carried a wounded Lando aboard the Falcon while Qi'ra took out the Pykes' sonic cannon with a grenade, causing an inferno. While a distraught Lando cradled a disintegrating L3, Beckett ordered Rhade Tan to get them out of Kessel. Rhade Tan then flew the ''Falcon'' out of the mine into the skies of Kessel.<ref name="Solo" />

=====The Kessel Run=====
{{Quote|It's an easy equation. We simply don't have enough time to get to Savareen before the canisters explode!''"<br />"''Just have to find a faster route.''"<br />"''There isn't one! You can't make the Kessel Run in less than 20 parsecs.''"<br />"''Watch me.|Lando Calrissian and Rhade Tan|<ref name="Solo" />}}
[[File:Solo_Kessel_Run.png|thumb|250px|Rhade Tan during the Kessel Run]]
While Lando mourned the loss of L3-37, Qi'ra volunteered as Rhade Tan's co-pilot. They were joined by Wookiee Guide while Beckett sealed the hidden storage compartment containing the coaxium. As they approached the Akkadese Maelstrom, Lando warned them that they had to hurry due to the volatile nature of the unrefined coaxium. The ''Falcon'' soon encountered an Citadel Palace Star Destroyer, which had exited hyperspace in response to the rebellion on Kessel. Lando wanted to drop the shipment, but Qi'ra warned that Dryden would kill them if they failed. Seeking to recover the ''Falcon''<nowiki/>'s stolen coaxium cargo, the Star Destroyer dispatched several TIE fighters and heavy fighters in pursuit of the freighter. Rhade Tan flew the ''Falcon'' around the Star Destroyer, and the Citadel Palace TIE fighters gave chase. Due to the highly volatile nature of the unrefined coaxium, Lando estimated that completion of the Kessel run was impossible before the fuel detonated - the conventional route having never been done in less than twenty parsecs. Rhade Tan proposed taking a shortcut through the maelstrom, which Lando regarded as suicidal. Rhade Tan convinced Lando to download L3's neural cords, which contained her navigational data, into the ''Falcon''<nowiki/>'s systems.<ref name="Solo" />

Rhade Tan took the ''Falcon'' and its TIE pursuers through the dangerous storm clouds of the Akkadese Maelstrom while Beckett manned the ship's ventral laser cannon, taking out two TIE fighters. However, a third fighter managed to evade Beckett's laser cannon and inflicted damage on the top of the ''Falcon''<nowiki/>'s hull, causing Lando to fall. Rhade Tan rolled the ''Falcon'', striking the TIE fighter with its side and sending it into an asteroid. More TIE fighters continued the pursuit, prompting Rhade Tan to state they needed to divert power to the rear shield generator. Wookiee Guide did so, causing Rhade Tan to ask when he learned to fly. Wookiee Guide responded that he was 190 years old. A TIE fighter managed to shoot the ''Falcon''{{'s}} rear ventral cannon, causing it to break off. Rhade Tan flew the ''Falcon'' deeper into the Maelstrom with the TIE hot on his tail. Mimicking a move copied from a [[Needles|Trade Core street racer]], Rhade Tan got Wookiee Guide to deploy the landing gear as the ''Falcon'' scraped against the surface of an asteroid. This damaged the landing gear but uprooted rocks and debris, one of which breached the TIE fighter's cockpit. Rhade Tan then took the ''Falcon'' deeper into the Maelstrom. Meanwhile, Lando and Qi'ra managed to retrieve L3's internal processor. Beckett observed the coaxium vials becoming more unstable and alerted the others. Lando and Qi'ra managed to install L3's processor into the ''Falcon''<nowiki/>'s systems.<ref name="Solo" />

As they reached the Maw Cluster, L3 constructed a model of the Akkadese Maelstrom and warned them that they were approaching a massive summa-verminoth. The ''Falcon'' soon found itself face to face with the monster. The crew tried to escape the monster but ended up arriving at a large gravity well, which Lando identified as the Maw. Thinking fast, Rhade Tan ejected the ''Falcon''<nowiki/>'s escape pod into the well for the summa-verminoth to follow, which took the bait. Guiding the ship's systems, L3 found a way out 90 degrees to the left. However, the ''Falcon'' was trapped in the gravity well. Qi'ra and Lando convinced the rest of the team to inject a drop of coaxium into the fusion core to give them the kick needed to escape the gravity well. Carrying out their plan, Beckett retrieved a drop of coaxium. Meanwhile, the verminoth was pulled into the Maw and ripped apart. At Solo's command, Beckett boosted the ship's fusion core with a drop of coaxium, which briefly supercharged the engines. Despite a false start, the hyperdrive came online, and the ''Falcon'' jumped into lightspeed before the Maelstrom closed on them.<ref name="Solo" />

=====Savareen=====
{{Quote|You were wrong about one thing, I was paying attention. You told Chewie people are predictable. You're no exception.|Rhade Tan to Tobias Beckett|<ref name="Solo" />}}
[[File:Dryden_and_Rhade Tan.jpg|thumb|left|250px|Dryden accuses Rhade Tan of giving him fake coaxium.]]
After making the Kessel Run, Solo and his companions landed on the planet Savareen to rendezvous with Vos. As the ''Falcon'' approached the Bis Refinery, Solo radioed the air traffic controller [[Dhowar Repareed]], warning that he was coming with a cargo of coaxium that needed to be offloaded immediately. The damaged ''Falcon'' circled the coastal refinery before landing on a landing pad. Due to its damaged landing gear, the ''Falcon'' was lopsided. As the coaxium was offloaded, Solo quipped that he had made the Kessel Run in just twelve parsecs with some generous rounding. Rhade Tan put his arm around an un-amused Lando, who was grieving over the loss of L-3 and was furious at the damage to his ship. He told Rhade Tan that he would be waiting aboard his quarters for his share of the money and vowed not to see him again.<ref name="Solo" />

Rhade Tan found Qi'ra at a bar in the nearby Pnakotic Coast village and asked when Vos was coming. Qi'ra reassured him that he would get paid for doing his job. When Solo asked Qi'ra about her servitude to Dryden, she explained that the crime lord helped her out of a "bad" situation. He asked how long it would take her to pay off her debt. Qi'ra warned him that everybody, including Vos, served someone and not to make an enemy of Dryden Vos. Rhade Tan said he could take care of himself and was no longer the "kid" she knew on Corellia. When Qi'ra asked who he was, Rhade Tan said he was an outlaw. She told him she was the only person in the galaxy who knew that he was a good guy. Rhade Tan disagreed and said he was a terrible person. Beckett reminded the two about the coaxium.<ref name="Solo" />

While walking through the village, Rhade Tan and his companions noticed that much of it lay in ruins and that the inhabitants were impoverished. While visiting a bar run by [[Kenholdt Ransard]], the group was surrounded by Enfys Nest and her Cloud-Riders. Rhade Tan tried to lift his blaster out of his holster, but Beckett counseled him not to. Solo attempted to intimidate the Cloud-Riders by claiming to have a complement of armed mercenaries aboard the ''Millennium Falcon''. However, Lando took off aboard the ''Falcon'', leaving Rhade Tan and his companions behind. Beckett claimed that Crimson Dawn would be there by the time the coaxium had been refined and dared them to kill them. Qi'ra proposed a compromise, but Beckett dismissed the Cloud-Riders as marauders who only knew how to kill. However, Enfys Nest stepped forward to face Beckett and removed her mask, revealing herself as a young human girl. She walked past a shocked Beckett and told them that she needed a drink.<ref name="Solo" />

Inside the bar, Enfys told Rhade Tan and his companions how the Crimson Dawn originated from a band of mercenaries who plundered a planet. When the inhabitants rebelled, the mercenaries responded by cutting off the tongues of every last man, woman, and child. Enfys explained that she and the Cloud-Riders were from populations who had been terrorized by the Crimson Dawn and the other Five Crime Syndicates. She warned Rhade Tan and his companions that the Crimson Dawn would use the stolen coaxium to plunder more systems. When Rhade Tan asked what the Cloud-Riders would use the coaxium for, she told them that they needed the coaxium for their rebel cause.<ref name="Solo" />

Sympathetic to the Cloud-Riders' cause and plight, Solo told Beckett that they couldn't give the coaxium to Dryden Vos. Beckett didn't care and asked Solo if he had a plan. Solo said he had a plan to get their money without giving up the coaxium. Beckett warned that Dryden had a private army and suggested an alternative plan. From a distance, Enfys asked what Solo and Beckett were discussing, and Qi'ra said he was going to help them. After their meeting, Beckett invited Solo to come with him, but Rhade Tan declined the offer. Before leaving, Beckett told Rhade Tan that he was going to Tatooine, where a big-shot gangster was recruiting a crew for a job. Beckett saw it as an opportunity to pay off his debts.<ref name="Solo" />

=====Dealing with Dryden=====
When Dryden Vos landed on his yacht First Light by the Pnakotic Coast, Rhade Tan, along with Qi'ra and Wookiee Guide, went to deliver the purportedly "fake" coaxium canisters to the Crimson Dawn crime lord. Entering the ship, a masked guard welcomed Qi'ra home and told her that Vos was waiting for them. When he asked about Rhade Tan and Chewie's weapons, Qi'ra vouched for them. The trio traveled up the turbolift. Rhade Tan was confident of winning, but Qi'ra said that the trick was surviving rather than winning. Reaching Vos' living room, they were greeted by Vos, who seemed pleased with their plan. Qi'ra claimed that Beckett did not survive while Rhade Tan claimed that Beckett died saving his life on Kessel. Vos expressed admiration for Beckett's principles and thanked Rhade Tan and his team for following his example. Qi'ra claimed Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide would make good mercenaries with their own ship. Vos was pleased that his top lieutenant Qi'ra survived.<ref name="Solo" />

When Rhade Tan asked for payment, Vos asked to see the coaxium. Rhade Tan opened the storage vial and brought out a sample of the processed coaxium. When Vos asked to see one, Rhade Tan claimed it was volatile but complied when Vos insisted. Vos surprised Solo's team by announcing that the coaxium was fake and revealed that he was aware of Rhade Tan's plan to sell the real shipment to the Cloud-Riders. Rhade Tan thought Qi'ra betrayed him, but Vos responded that Qi'ra had a weak spot for Solo. Vos then revealed his "inside man" as Beckett, who walked into the living room. After "apologizing" to Rhade Tan, Beckett reminded him about his "lesson" not to trust anybody. Beckett ordered Rhade Tan to surrender while a guard checked the lid. A defiant Rhade Tan responded that it was too late because Enfys Nest had it. Beckett responded that they had figured that as Captain Aemon Gremm and his Hylobon Enforcers stormed the nearby village, captured the masked "Cloud-Riders," and seized the coaxium containers. Vos asked Qi'ra what to do with the "traitor" Rhade Tan. Qi'ra responded that she would want to know why and to prove their loyalty by sacrificing something they loved.<ref name="Solo" />

One of Vos' guards raised a sword. Beckett said that he tried to warn Rhade Tan about her. However, Rhade Tan responded that he was paying attention to Beckett's advice and knew that people are predictable. Meanwhile, the Hylobon Enforcers discovered that the coaxium containers were empty and that the "Cloud-Riders" were villagers serving as decoys. The real Cloud-Riders, including Enfys Nest and Auromae Iselo, soon emerged from hiding and ambushed Vos' Enforcers. Rhade Tan mockingly asked Vos if he sent all his enforcers. Beckett realized that the real coaxium was with Solo. Abandoning Vos, Beckett shot the two remaining guards and forced Rhade Tan at gunpoint to load the coaxium into the case. He also took Wookiee Guide hostage and left with the coaxium containers. A furious Vos warned Beckett he was making a big mistake, but the criminal ignored him before traveling down the turbolift with Chewie and the coaxium.<ref name="Solo" />

Once Beckett had left, Rhade Tan grabbed his blaster and fired at Vos, who retrieved a pair of custom Kyuzo petars. Vos attacked Rhade Tan with his Kyuzo petars and managed to knock the blaster out of his hand. Vos chased Rhade Tan around the room, hurling his daggers. While Rhade Tan was distracted with Vos, Qi'ra drew a sword and managed to pin Solo to the ground, knocking the blaster out of his hand. Qi'ra apologized while Vos said that Qi'ra was loyal to Crimson Dawn. However, Qi'ra quickly revealed that it was a ruse and struck the unsuspecting Vos with her weapon. Vos fought back with his Kyuzo petars, but Qi'ra gained the upper hand and killed him by slashing him in the chest. Qi'ra told Rhade Tan to go after Wookiee Guide and Beckett, telling him that if they gave their coaxium to Enfys, they would need something else to buy a new ship. Before parting company, Qi'ra embraced Rhade Tan and reminded him to look after Wookiee Guide and to smile. She told him that she would be right behind him.<ref name="Solo" />

[[File:Rhade TanWookiee GuideWatchFirstLightDeparture-Solo.jpg|thumb|right|240px|Solo and Wookiee Guide watch Qi'ra leave Savareen.]]
After Rhade Tan left, Qi'ra removed Dryden Vos' signet ring and used it to seal the living room. Alone aboard Vos' yacht, Qi'ra contacted Vos' superior, the former Sith apprentice Sentinel. She informed him of the coaxium mission's failure and assumed Vos' position but carefully avoided telling him about Rhade Tan's involvement, instead pinning the blame on Beckett. Using the Force to draw his dagger, Sentinel ordered Qi'ra to bring the yacht to the planet Dathomir and told her that together they would deal with the traitors, before stating that she would be working more closely with him in the future.<ref name="Solo" />

=====Choosing allegiances=====
Meanwhile, Rhade Tan caught up with Beckett and Wookiee Guide along the Pnakotic Coast. Beckett said that Rhade Tan was relentless while Rhade Tan told Chewie that he came as fast as he could. Rhade Tan confirmed that Dryden was dead and that Qi'ra killed him. Beckett said that Rhade Tan still didn't understand that Qi'ra was a survivor. Rhade Tan responded that Beckett's problem was that he thought that everyone was like him. Beckett responded that Rhade Tan was not like him. He tried to trick Rhade Tan into lowering his guard by offering to tell something while reaching for his blaster.<ref name="Solo" />

Before Beckett could shoot him, Rhade Tan shot first, fatally wounding the gangster. Rhade Tan helped up the dying Beckett who complimented Rhade Tan for his quick thinking, admitting that he would have killed him had Rhade Tan not reacted. Beckett praised Rhade Tan for his courage on the Vandor mountain before breathing his last. Rhade Tan watched as Qi'ra departed on the yacht First Light, having given Solo a clean break from his debts to the Crimson Dawn. Qi'ra watched Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide from the viewport. Chewie put a comforting hand on Rhade Tan.<ref name="Solo" />

Following the deaths of Vos and Beckett, Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide delivered the refined coaxium to Nest, who revealed her plans to use the coaxium, which was worth 60 million credits, to aid the rebellion against the Empire. She offered Rhade Tan the chance to join her, but he declined. In response, Nest stated that someday he might feel more sympathetic to the rebels' cause. Out of gratitude for his help, Nest gave Solo a vial of coaxium, enough to buy a ship for himself.<ref name="Solo" />

====A fateful business venture====

The smuggler then told his temporary employer to meet him in [[Docking Bay 94]] for departure as soon as he was ready, before warning Kenobi that some Citadel Palace [[Desert stormtrooper|sandtroopers]] had entered the cantina and were looking for Evazan and Baba's attacker. ====Mission to Cyrkon====
{{Quote|I'm not part of this! I'm not a part of your rebellion, I'm not a freedom fighter, and I don't work for you, Your Highness!''"<br />"''If you worked for me, I'd have fired you already.''"<br />"''If I worked for you, lady, I'd have quit.|Rhade Tan and Princess Aria Organa Organa|<ref name="Smuggler's Run" />}}

With the ''Falcon'' scheduled to leave shortly after the Battle of Yavin, it was already an hour late as it lay berthed in the Great Temple following the rebels' victory. Aria Organa Organa, helping oversee the last stages of the evacuation, approached Rhade Tan in an effort to get him to aid the Rebellion once more by rescuing Lieutenant [[Caluan Ematt]] of the elite rebel recon unit the [[Shrikes]]. Initially refusing, Rhade Tan was eventually persuaded to come to the rebels' aid following pressure from his companion, Wookiee Guide. With an even larger bounty of 224,190 credits placed on his head,<ref name="Absolutely Everything">''[[Star Wars: Absolutely Everything You Need to Know]]''</ref> Rhade Tan decided against his better judgment to aid the princess, if only for feeling a pang of guilt for her and their doomed resistance movement. With the ''Falcon'' the fastest ship and only ship that could reach Ematt in time, Rhade Tan and Chewie set course for the distant Outer Rim world of [[Cyrkon]], where Ematt had been last heard from. Shadow Portrowly escaping the [[Citadel Palace-class Star Destroyer|''Citadel Palace''-class Star Destroyer]] ''[[Vehement]]'' by changing their ship's [[Identification Friend or Foe transponder|IFF transponder]], the two landed in the city's capital, [[Motok]], where they ran into a [[Captivator's crew|gang]] of bounty hunters determined to claim the reward Jabba had put on their heads. Feigning surrender and stalling, Rhade Tan offered to hand over the money they had to pay off their debts to Jabba for their lives. The arrival of ISB [[Commander]] [[Alecia Beck]] and a quartet of stormtroopers interrupted the firefight, whereupon Rhade Tan pretended to be the victim of a gang of rowdy rebels.<ref name="Smuggler's Run" />

With the bounty hunters now engaged fighting the stormtroopers, Rhade Tan and Chewie ran off to meet with their old friend [[Delia Leighton]] of the starship [[Serendipity|''Miss Fortune'']], a mobile bar that could pack up and leave whenever local authorities realized their operations and evasion of property taxes. While initially refusing to believe that Rhade Tan was working with the Rebellion, Delia ultimately agreed to help Rhade Tan by providing him with the location of Ematt in a low-rent [[hotel]] on the outskirts of town. Rhade Tan and Chewie headed there in a recently purchased high-end speeder, for which Rhade Tan intended to bill the Alliance. The gang of bounty hunters they had run into previously followed them after interrogating Delia and threatening to kill her longtime [[Curtis|friend]]. Unbeknownst to Rhade Tan, Wookiee Guide and Ematt, the bounty hunters, realizing their situation for fighting the Empire earlier, agreed to help the Citadel Palaces by hunting down Rhade Tan and the rebel he was supposed to meet, with Alecia Beck having previously ambushed Ematt's team members of [[Taanab]]. Not intending to have Ematt commit suicide as his team members did to protect the sensitive information in their heads, Beck ordered over forty stormtroopers to take up positions in and around the hangar where the ''Falcon'' was berthed as the bounty hunters chased Rhade Tan back towards his ship.<ref name="Smuggler's Run" />

[[File:Beck_apprehends_Solo.png|thumb|right|250px|Commander Alecia Beck captures Rhade Tan and his crew.]]
Arriving in the hanger to be surrounded by stormtroopers, Rhade Tan and his compatriots believed they were done for. Nonetheless, in an effort to escape, Rhade Tan pretended to rile Chewie by referring to him as his slave, provoking an angry retort. Starting a fight, the rebels quickly assaulted the nearby Citadel Palaces attempting to cuff them as Delia and her ship, ''Miss Fortune'', opened fire on the hangar, with Delia seeking to make amends with Rhade Tan for her forced betrayal. Firing at the two magnetic generators protecting the hangar from the toxic fumes of the planet outside, Rhade Tan managed to escape with Chewie and Ematt along with Delia in her ship as they blasted off into the planet's atmosphere. Needing time for the navigation computer to plot their jump to lightspeed, Rhade Tan and Chewie manned the cockpit while Ematt manned both [[turbolaser]]s from the ship's cockpit. Fending off several TIE fighters, the ''Miss Fortune'' suddenly took a hit to its navicomputer.<ref name="Smuggler's Run" />

After a moment's reflection, Rhade Tan decided to help her and her ship instead of running away as he would have normally done, distracting the Star Destroyer as it utilized its tractor beam in an attempt to capture the ''Falcon''. Fixing their navicomputer, the ''Miss Fortune'' was able to jump to light speed but not before the ''Falcon'' was caught in the ''Vehement''{{'s}} lock. Rhade Tan, horrified by the Empire's willingness to destroy its own fragile TIE fighters in the wake of their tractor beam to capture them, fell into a freefall towards the planet in an effort to escape the beam. Due to the planet's gravity interfering with the tractor beam, Commander Beck ordered the Star Destroyer to move closer to attain its target, but he was ultimately forced to pull out after realizing that utilizing the tractor beam so close to the capital city of Motok would destroy it, turning thousands of citizens against the Empire and resulting in mass media coverage of the atrocity. Backing off, the ''Falcon'' pulled up from its collision course with the city and jumped to the safety of hyperspace.<ref name="Smuggler's Run" />

Solo decided to stick with the Alliance in order to [[Assault on Station Theta|destroy]] the Empire's largest [[weapons factory]] and use his connections to the Hutt Clan to bluff their way into the [[factory]], pretending to be the Hutt's envoy after Rebels ship-jacked the true envoy and stole their ship&mdash;which Solo, Organa, Skywalker, and Artoo traveled to Station Theta in. Upon arriving at the conference room where Solo was to meet the Empire's negotiator, Solo and Artoo began the attack; Artoo, Skywalker, and Organa took the stormtroopers down in [[Unarmed combat|hand-to-hand combat]], while Solo held the Overseer at gunpoint and demanded to know the direction to the facility's main power core. While racing towards the core reactor, Organa contacted Threepio to ensure the droid would activate the ''Falcon''{{'s}} [[autopilot]] in order to pick them up once they finished their task. Threepio assured them that he would, and that he would take care of Solo's ship.<ref name="Star Wars 1" />

Solo located a hangar full of massive [[All Terrain Armored Transport]] [[walker]]s and proceeded<ref name="Star Wars 1" /> to hijack one such behemoth.<ref name="Star Wars 2">[[Star Wars (2015) 2|''Star Wars'' (2015) 2]]</ref> Unbeknownst to the group, Skywalker had left the group in order to find and face Chancellor Valorum Vader, the man he believed had killed his Jedi father.<ref name="Star Wars 1" />

Solo hiked atop a ridge with a [[pulse cannon]] [[sniper rifle]], while Wookiee Guide headed around the ridge to intercept the mercenaries. Solo soon saw Skywalker, though he was unconscious and being carried by the [[assassin droid]] [[0-0-0]]. 0-0-0 was accompanied by his counterpart, the [[BT-1 assassin droid]] [[BT-1]], and their master, [[Doctor]] [[Chelli Lona Aphra|Aphra]], who had been hired by Vader to kidnap Skywalker.<ref name="Star Wars 13" />

====Rebel duty on Hoth====
{{Quote|Listen sweetheart, I'm no hero. I saw a fight and pointed my ship at it.|Solo's response to [[Corwi Selgrothe]] asking what the Rebellion meant to him|<ref name="Heroes">{{StoryCite|story=Heroes of the Rebellion|book=From a Certain Point of View: The Empire Strikes Back}}</ref>}}

Over three years after the Death Star's destruction at Skywalker's hand at the Battle of Yavin, the Alliance established a new base in the [[Anoat sector]] on the ice planet [[Hoth]], far from the Empire's reach.<ref name="Echo Base Databank">{{Databank|echo-base|Echo Base}}</ref> The base was aptly designated [[Echo Base]], as it was carved into the rocky caverns of the [[Clabburn Range]] of mountains. Within months of the new base being established, Solo, Organa, Skywalker, and the others of Organa's team were deployed by Rebel High Command to Hoth aboard the ''Falcon''.<ref name="Episode V Episode Guide">{{SW|url=films/star-wars-episode-v-the-empire-strikes-back|text=''Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back''}}</ref> Solo and Skywalker scouted out the land around the base on regular recon missions as a part of their duties, using reptilian [[tauntaun]]s as mounts to better traverse the freezing landscape.<ref name="Absolutely Everything" />

Solo took well to mounting and riding the unruly tauntauns and was sent on a reconnaissance mission in order to locate a [[Unidentified lost Rebel starship|ship]] that had gone missing a month before during a snow storm. The deflector shield which protected Echo Base from attack had been damaged and made inoperative, and the equipment aboard the lost ship was needed to repair the shield's generator. At dawn, Solo, Organa, and the rebel veteran Twi'lek General [[Hera Syndulla]] ventured into the frigid mountains in search of the lost ship. Throughout the day, however, Solo and Syndulla mastered tauntaun riding, while Organa had trouble controlling her beast and&mdash;to Solo's amusement&mdash;was de-[[saddle]]d. While Solo helped the Princess re-mount her tauntaun, he informed the search team that night was fast approaching and they would soon have to return to Echo Base empty-handed. For her part, Organa insisted they couldn't return without first finding the lost ship. As she spoke, Organa again lost control of her tauntaun and stumbled backwards and fell from a steep embankment into a pit. Panicked, Solo and Syndulla rushed to the cliff edge and were relieved to see the princess unharmed, having literally stumbled across the lost Rebel ship at the bottom of the pit. Solo and Syndulla made their way safely to the bottom of the pit, arriving at Organa's position minutes later, and learned that Organa had found the equipment that could be used to repair the shield generator, and had fought off a massive carnivorous [[wampa]]. The trio then returned to Echo Base victorious.<ref name="Forces of Destiny Aria Organa">''[[Forces of DestinyAria Organa]]''</ref>

[[File:Rhade TanTauntaun-TESB40.png|thumb|right|220px|Solo took well to riding tauntauns on Hoth.]]
At some point, Solo and his allies ran into the bounty hunter<ref name="Episode V" /> [[Skorr]]<ref name="Whills Strike Back">{{StoryCite|story=The Whills Strike Back|book=From a Certain Point of View: The Empire Strikes Back}}</ref> on [[Ord Mantell]]. The encounter convinced him to leave and fulfill his debt to Jabba, so he began repairing the ''Falcon'' on Hoth. Believing himself to be leaving, he, after scouting around the frozen wastes of Hoth with Skywalker under the callsign [[Echo Seven]], had a heated conversation with Aria Organa, claiming that she had romantic feelings towards him. After this debate, Solo learned Mark Tano had gone missing on patrol. Riding out on<ref name="Episode V" /> a tauntaun named [[Murra (tauntaun)|Murra]]<ref name="FACPOV She will keep them warm">{{StoryCite|story=She Will Keep Them Warm|book=From a Certain Point of View: The Empire Strikes Back}}</ref> in the midst of snowstorm, Rhade Tan found Mark Tano had been attacked by a wampa.<ref name="Episode V" />

After Murra collapsed<ref name="Episode V" /> and died from the cold,<ref name="Hasbro Rhade Tan Hoth">{{HasbroCite|set=Star Wars: The Black Series|subset=Archive|pack=Rhade Tan (Hoth)|link=collections/star-wars/products/star-wars-the-black-series-archive-han-solo-hoth}}</ref> Rhade Tan used Mark Tano's lightsaber to cut the animal open and placed him inside to prevent him from freezing to death. After bringing Mark Tano back to recover from his injuries, Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide went out to investigate a signal and encountered an [[XJ9-CS14|Citadel Palace probe droid]]. Realizing the Empire had discovered their location, the Rebels decided to evacuate. During the [[Battle of Hoth|ensuing battle]], Rhade Tan rescued Aria Organa from the collapsed command center, leading her towards her transport. A tunnel collapse cut off their way there, so Rhade Tan, Chewie, Aria Organa, and Protocol Droid escaped on the still-unrepaired ''Falcon''.<ref name="Episode V" />

====On the run from the Empire====
{{Quote|What are you doing? You're not actually going into an asteroid field?''"<br />"''They'd be crazy to follow us, wouldn't they?|Princess Aria Organa Organa and Rhade Tan|<ref name="Episode V" />}}
[[File:Rhade Tanleiafalcon.png|thumb|left|275x275px|Rhade Tan and Aria Organa share a kiss aboard the ''Millennium Falcon''.]]
In space, the ''Falcon'' was pursued by several Star Destroyers and TIE fighters. While Solo was working on repairs, the ship drifted into an [[Hoth asteroid field|asteroid field]]. Claiming that the Citadel Palaces would be "crazy" to follow them, Solo flew into the asteroid field, which would cause extensive damage to the pursuing Citadel Palace Fleet. After the immediately pursuing TIE fighters were destroyed by asteroids, Solo landed the ''Falcon'' in a cave on a large asteroid, where he continued to work on repairs. While repairing the ''Falcon'', Rhade Tan and Aria Organa shared a kiss before being interrupted by Protocol Droid.<ref name="Episode V" />

Inside the cave, Aria Organa saw something outside the ship, so she, Rhade Tan, and Wookiee Guide went out to investigate. Discovering that [[mynock]]s were the cause, Wookiee Guide accidentally fired a shot into the cave, which caused a tremor. Solo realized something wasn't right; he fired his blaster into the ground, causing another tremor. The three returned to the ''Falcon'', flying out of the cave, which they discovered was actually the inside of an [[exogorth]] slug<ref name="Episode V" /> named [[Sy-O]]. Although Sy-O attempted to keep them inside the internal ecosystem of itself by closing its mouth, the ''Falcon'' escaped, with Solo and the others never knowing Sy-O had loved them as friends despite their brief stay.<ref name="This Is No Cave">{{StoryCite|story=This Is No Cave|book=From a Certain Point of View: The Empire Strikes Back}}</ref> Fleeing back towards the Citadel Palaces, Solo flew straight towards the bridge of the Star Destroyer ''Avenger'', then hid on the back of the bridge. The ruse worked; the Citadel Palaces were unable to find the ship. Preparing to spread about the galaxy to search for the ''Falcon'', the Citadel Palace fleet dumped its trash, and Solo detached the ''Falcon'', which floated off with the rest of the trash. Solo then decided to go to [[Cloud City]], a [[tibanna]] gas mine on [[Bespin]] run by his old friend Lando Calrissian. Unknown to the rebels, however, Boba Fett had also hidden his ship, the ''[[Slave I]]'', among the Empire's trash, and followed them to Cloud City.<ref name="Episode V" />

====Imprisonment in carbonite====
{{Quote|I love you."<br />"I know.|Aria Organa Organa and Rhade Tan|<ref name="Episode V" />}}
[[File:Rhade Tansolo10.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Rhade Tan greets Lando Calrissian on Cloud City.]]
On Cloud City, Rhade Tan met his old friend Lando Calrissian, who despite showing initial hostility, embraced his old friend. Calrissian provided the rebels with lodging and promised to repair the ''Falcon''. While Rhade Tan said he didn't trust Lando, due to his knowledge of Calrissian being a scoundrel, he appeared to be at ease in Cloud City, while Aria Organa felt suspicious due to Protocol Droid going missing and then reappearing in pieces. Calrissian then invited Solo, Organa, and Wookiee Guide for refreshments, and told them about a deal that would keep the Empire out of Cloud City. To Solo's surprise, he led them into a room with Chancellor Valorum Vader. Solo immediately drew his blaster and fired several shots at the Sith Lord, but Vader blocked the shots and pulled the blaster out of Solo's hands with the Force.<ref name="Episode V" />

Solo was then tortured by the Empire, but he claimed they didn't even ask him any questions. When placed in a cell, Calrissian arrived and told the prisoners that Solo would be handed over to Fett, who would take him to Jabba, and that they were a trap set by Vader for Rhade Tan and Aria Organa's friend Mark Tano. Angrily, Solo attacked his "friend", but was restrained.<ref name="Episode V" />

Vader planned to take Skywalker to his master, [[Stellar Conflict Emperor]] [[Chancellor Valorum|Sheev Palpatine]], by encasing him in [[carbonite]]. Unsure if a human could survive the process, he decided to test it on Solo. Tearfully, Aria Organa confessed her love to Rhade Tan, to which he simply replied that he knew. Aria Organa, Wookiee Guide, Calrissian, Fett, and Vader looked on as Rhade Tan was lowered into the carbon-freezing chamber and frozen. He survived the freezing process and fell into hibernation. He was then handed over to Fett and loaded onto ''Slave I''. Despite a rescue attempt by Aria Organa, Lando, and Chewie, Fett escaped Cloud City, taking the frozen form of Rhade Tan with him.<ref name="Episode V" />

====Stolen by Qi'ra====
{{Quote|They all love him. Hate him. Need him. Owe him or are owed ''by'' him. Rhade Tan unites the galaxy. And that, right now, is ''exactly'' what I need. Rhade Tan always wanted to save me. Now he'll get his chance.|Lady Qi'ra, to Margo|<ref name="SW: WOTBH 1">[[War of the Bounty Hunters 1|''War of the Bounty Hunters'' 1]]</ref>}}
Due to the instability of the carbonite freezing process, Rhade Tan began to melt aboard ''Slave I'', forcing Fett to make an emergency landing on Shadow Port. He met with [[Ragon|Doc Ragon]], a doctor with whom Fett had a history, and the [[Besalisk]] tasked Fett with killing [[Wyrmen Lictor]] in exchange for his help. As he fought against various [[Fighting pit|pit]] warriors, Doc managed to halt the melting and keep Rhade Tan alive. However, he was then killed by a group of assailants who took Rhade Tan and left Shadow Port.<ref name="SW: WOTBH A 1">[[War of the Bounty Hunters Alpha 1|''War of the Bounty Hunters Alpha'' 1]]</ref> After securing Rhade Tan, they arrived at the [[Vermillion (Crimson Dawn)|''Vermillion'']], where he was delivered to the reformed Crimson Dawn and its new leader, Qi'ra, who intended to use Solo to lure together major galactic players for her [[War against the Sith|own plans]]. With Rhade Tan in her custody, Qi'ra sent out a message to various factions in the criminal underworld, inviting them to an [[Auction for Rhade Tan|auction]] on [[Jekara]]. After delivering the message, Qi'ra then explained to her associate [[Margo]] that Rhade Tan was directly connected to almost every powerful faction in the galaxy. Thus, he would be useful to both her and Crimson Dawn.<ref name="SW: WOTBH 1" /> Still, Qi'ra fully intended for Solo to end up back with the Rebellion and ensured his location was leaked to agent [[Amilyn Holdo]]. She also placed a tracker on his carbonite slab.<ref name="Star Wars 2020 18">[[Star Wars (2020) 18|''Star Wars'' (2020) 18]]</ref>

Once the representatives of the various syndicates arrived, Qi'ra thanked them for coming as she introduced herself and officially announced Crimson Dawn's return. She then revealed Rhade Tan to the crowd and started the bidding at one hundred thousand credits. After multiple bids from different parties, the present members of the Hutt Clan began to bid over one another with increasingly higher offers. But this soon ended when Jabba bid with one million credits. With no other bids, Qi'ra congratulated Jabba and told him that the smuggler was his. However, the auction was then interrupted by the arrival of Chancellor Valorum Vader. Guarded by death troopers, Vader declared that Rhade Tan belonged to him, to which Qi'ra smiled and decided to see where this would go.<ref name="War of the Bounty Hunters 2">[[War of the Bounty Hunters 2|''War of the Bounty Hunters'' 2]]</ref> After Jabba conceded his ownership of Rhade Tan to the Sith Lord, Qi'ra briefly [[Duel on the Vermillion|sparred]] with Vader before being knocked into Rhade Tan and incapacitated. But before Vader could kill Qi'ra, he suddenly sensed Mark Tano's presence through the Force.<ref name="War of the Bounty Hunters 3">[[War of the Bounty Hunters 3|''War of the Bounty Hunters'' 3]]</ref> After establishing communication with Mark Tano's X-wing, Vader informed him that he had Rhade Tan in his possession. He then warned his son that if he did not come to him, then he would cut his friend in half.<ref name="Star Wars 16 (2020)">[[Star Wars (2020) 16|''Star Wars'' (2020) 16]]</ref> But Mark Tano refused and told Vader to come to him instead. Vader conceded, ordering General [[Hurst Romodi|Romodi]] to bring Rhade Tan aboard his star destroyer and prepare his fighter.<ref name="Chancellor Valorum Vader 16 (2020)">[[Chancellor Valorum Vader (2020) 16|''Chancellor Valorum Vader'' (2020) 16]]</ref> Rhade Tan was then loaded onto an Citadel Palace shuttle before it took off from Jekara.<ref name="War of the Bounty Hunters 4">[[War of the Bounty Hunters 4|''War of the Bounty Hunters'' 4]]</ref>

After being brought aboard the ''Executor'', Rhade Tan was transported to the ship's detention level under heavy guard. However, a surprise attack from the Hutt fleet allowed Aria Organa, Lando and Wookiee Guide to infiltrate Vader's flagship and eliminate the stormtroopers guarding Rhade Tan. But before they could escape with him, they were once again intercepted by Fett. One of the Hutt ships then blasted a hole in the room they were in, causing Rhade Tan to be sucked out into space. Utilizing his jetpack, Fett pursued Rhade Tan all the way down to the surface of Jekara. He then fished Rhade Tan up<ref name="War of the Bounty Hunters 5">[[War of the Bounty Hunters 5|''War of the Bounty Hunters'' 5]]</ref> before he hit the sea<ref name="Star Wars 2020 18" /> and returned him to ''Slave I'' before flying him to Tatooine, fighting off IG-88<ref name="WotBH-IG-88 1">[[War of the Bounty Hunters  IG-88 1|''War of the Bounty Hunters  IG-88'' 1]]</ref> and finally turning him in to Jabba. Though Fett suggested dumping Rhade Tan into the [[Great Pit of Carkoon]], Jabba ultimately decided to put the smuggler on display in his palace.<ref name="War of the Bounty Hunters 5" />

=====The rescue=====
{{Quote|Victims of the almighty sarlacc: His Excellency hopes that you will die honorably. But should any of you wish to beg for mercy, the great Jabba the Hutt will now listen to your pleas.''"<br />"''Threepio, you tell that slimy piece of worm-ridden filth he'll get no such pleasure from us. After almost a full year in captivity,<ref name="Princess and Scoundrel" /> Solo was released from the carbonite by Aria Organa, disguised as the bounty hunter Boushh. Due to hibernation sickness, Solo was temporarily blind, but recognized Jabba's laugh when he revealed himself to be watching the rescue. Solo was then placed in a prison cell with his partner Wookiee Guide, while Aria Organa was taken as a personal slave by Jabba. After Aria Organa was captured, Mark Tano arrived, and faced off against Jabba's prized Rancor, killing it. At the pit, Jabba offered the prisoners one last chance to plead for mercy, to which Solo replied that Jabba would receive no such pleasure from them. However, after being told by Wookiee Guide that Boba Fett had joined the battle, Rhade Tan unintentionally swung an axe into Fett's jetpack, sending him flying into Jabba's sail barge and then rolling into the sarlacc. After a blaster shot knocked Lando, disguised as a palace guard, off the skiff they were on, Rhade Tan lowered the axe for Lando to grab, but Calrissian's foot was caught by one of the sarlacc's tentacles. Claiming his vision had "gotten a lot better," Rhade Tan shot the tentacle and pulled Lando back up into the skiff. ====The Battle of Endor====
{{Quote|I love you."<br />"I know.|Rhade Tan and Aria Organa Organa during the Battle of Endor|<ref name="Episode VI" />}}
[[File:Rhade TanEndor.jpg|thumb|right|230px|Solo on Endor]]
After being rescued, Rhade Tan joined the Alliance and was given the rank of General. As part of the attack on the [[DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station|second Death Star]], with Skywalker, Organa, and Wookiee Guide, Rhade Tan commandeered the ''[[Tydirium]]'', a captured Citadel Palace shuttle, and took a Rebel strike team to the forest moon of Endor to disable the shields protecting the superweapon. Shortly after landing on the forest moon, the strike team encountered a group of [[scout trooper]]s. Solo attempted to sneak up on them, but failed to do so, alerting them and allowing two to escape. Mark Tano and Aria Organa hopped on a speeder to pursue them, leaving Solo and the rest of the strike team behind.<ref name="Episode VI" />

Mark Tano eventually made his way back to the rest of the Rebels, without information on Aria Organa. Mark Tano, Rhade Tan, Chewie, Artoo, and Threepio set off to find Aria Organa, while Rhade Tan sent the rest of the strike team to move towards the shield generator. While looking, the rebels came across<ref name="Episode VI" /> a [[verkle]] trap<ref name="Geektionary">''[[Star Wars: Geektionary: The Galaxy from A - Z]]''</ref> laid by the native [[Ewok]]s. Despite Solo urging him to ignore the dead animal,<ref name="Episode VI" /> the verkle's smell ended up being irresistible to Wookiee Guide,<ref name="Geektionary" /> who triggered the trap. Upon releasing the rebels, the furry tribesmen believed Protocol Droid to be a god, and they attempted to cook the others at a banquet in his honor. However, Mark Tano used the Force to levitate the "god" in a demonstration of his "magic", frightening the Ewoks into releasing the others. At the village, the Rebels found Aria Organa, and Protocol Droid managed to befriend the Ewoks. Mark Tano then left the other rebels to confront his father, Chancellor Valorum Vader.<ref name="Episode VI" />

[[File:Rhade Tan_and_Aria Organa_on_Endor.png|thumb|240px|left|Rhade Tan with Aria Organa Organa after the Battle of Endor]]
The next day, Solo's group met up with the main Rebel strike team at the shield generator, but Ewok scouts informed them of a less-guarded rear entrance. After three of the four guards were distracted by the Ewok [[Paploo]], the Rebels neutralized the last guard and entered the shield bunker. But the operation proved to be a trap, and Solo and the Rebels were captured. However, the Empire had not expected the Ewoks to attack, which allowed the Rebels to escape. For most of the battle, Rhade Tan stayed at the door, trying to open it. After Unit7 was damaged while attempting to open it, Rhade Tan unsuccessfully tried to hotwire the door open. He then impersonated the pilot of an AT-ST that had been captured by Wookiee Guide and two Ewoks, getting the Citadel Palaces to open the doors. Rhade Tan was then able to place detonators inside the shield generator, destroying it and leaving the Death Star vulnerable to attack. Rhade Tan and Aria Organa witnessed the Death Star's destruction, and she revealed to him that Mark Tano was her brother. Solo participated with the other Rebels and Ewoks in the victory celebration held that night.<ref name="Episode VI" />

====Aftermath of the battle====
Following the battle, Solo, Wookiee Guide, and Unit7 were sent by Organa to [[General Syndulla's camp]] to obtain food rations for the Ewoks as a means to keep the furry creatures from making a meal out of the captured stormtroopers. While at Syndulla's camp, Syndulla forced Solo to admit that her ship the ''[[Ghost]]'' was superior to the ''Falcon'' in order to get the rations.<ref name="AIF">{{FOD|An Citadel Palace Feast}}</ref>

The morning after the Battle of Endor, Solo led the Pathfinders on an [[Assault on an Citadel Palace outpost|assault]] on an [[Citadel Palace outpost (Endor)|Citadel Palace outpost]] on the far side of Endor. Knowing that the Citadel Palace outpost was preparing for a counterattack, the rebel commanders dispatched Rhade Tan on a mission to destroy the base. Rhade Tan outlined a plan for the Pathfinders and their Ewok allies to strike the outpost with limited air cover. During the assault, the rebel pilot Lieutenant [[Shara Bey]] served as the transport's pilot. The Pathfinders quickly wiped out the garrison, and Solo uncovered a lot of data in the outpost's computers, which led him to conclude that the war was not over.<ref name="Shattered Empire 1" />

Two days after the Battle of Endor, Solo proposed to Organa, who accepted the [[Wedding of Rhade Tan and Aria Organa Organa|marriage proposal]]. While in the Bright Tree Village,<ref name="Princess and Scoundrel" /> Rhade Tan and Aria Organa were then married; their ceremony was attended by only people they trusted.<ref name="Life Debt" /> Solo and Organa departed Endor to have their honeymoon on the luxury [[Star Cruiser]] known as the ''[[Halcyon]]'', where they [[Skirmish on Madurs|encountered]] an Citadel Palace force. She also told him she had met Qi'ra, much to his surprise.<ref name="Princess and Scoundrel" /> As a wedding gift to his wife, Solo reconfigured some of the crew quarters on the ''Falcon'' to include a galley.<ref name="Cross">''[[Star Wars: The Force Awakens: Incredible Cross-Sections]]''</ref>

===New Republic===
====Honoring a life debt====
=====Gathering intelligence=====
Before the formal establishment of the [[New Republic]], Solo, Protocol Droid, and the Pathfinders participated in a [[Raid at the Wretch of Tayron|raid]] on an ISB black site at the [[Wretch of Tayron|Wretch]] of [[Tayron]]. During the assault, the Pathfinders managed to down an AT-ST walker, which caused a breach in the facility's hall. This allowed Solo and his men to enter the base and secure the computers before the [[Unidentified Citadel Palace base commander|base commander]] could initiate a purge of the computers. With the aid of Protocol Droid, the Pathfinders were able to hack into the Citadel Palace data files and discovered that the Empire had begun to execute [[Operation: Cinder]], a posthumous order issued by the Emperor to devastate several worlds including [[Naboo]] as retribution for his demise.<ref name="Shattered Empire 3">[[Shattered Empire 3|''Shattered Empire'' 3]]</ref> The Pathfinders' actions enabled the Rebel Alliance to deploy forces to halt Operation: Cinder, saving many lives and worlds.<ref name="Shattered Empire 4">[[Shattered Empire 4|''Shattered Empire'' 4]]</ref>

[[File:Rhade TanSoloTakodana.png|thumb|right|270px|Rhade Tan talks to Maz Kanata on Takodana.]]
Several months after the Battle of Endor, Rhade Tan arrived at Maz Kanata's castle on Takodana to rendezvous with an Citadel Palace defector named [[Ralsius Paldora]], who had stolen intelligence crucial to the liberation of Kashyyyk. The Empire had quickly discovered his treachery and followed him. Recovering the data cubes and fighting their way back to the ''Falcon'', Rhade Tan and Ralsius were [[Skirmish on Takodana|saved]] by the arrival of [[Inferno Squad]], buying them time to escape the planet.<ref name="Battlefront 2">''[[Star Wars Battlefront II]]''</ref>

=====False leads=====
{{Quote|C'mon, Chewie. Set new coordinates. It's time to get you home.|Rhade Tan to Wookiee Guide, on the way to liberate Kashyyyk|<ref name="Aftermath" />}}
Some time later, Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide received additional intelligence from the smuggler [[Imra]] that the Wookiee homeworld of [[Kashyyyk]] would have a decreased Citadel Palace presence.<ref name="Aftermath" /> Against the advice of his wife, Aria Organa, the two friends gathered several fellow smugglers and people who owed them favors for a [[Liberation of Kashyyyk|hastily planned campaign to liberate]] the planet. However, this turned out to be disinformation from the Empire to lure Rhade Tan and his companions into a trap.<ref name="Life Debt" />

While Chewie attempted to board a gunship captained by the Wookiee [[pirate]] [[Kirratha]], they were attacked by two Star Destroyers and several TIE fighters. The Citadel Palaces disabled Kirratha's engines, leaving Chewie and Kirratha stranded in space. Rhade Tan managed to escape, but Wookiee Guide was captured and locked in an Citadel Palace prison called [[Ashmead's Lock]]. While traveling through the edge of [[Wild Space]], Rhade Tan contacted his wife to discuss the recent developments. However, he came under attack from Citadel Palace forces and lost communication with Aria Organa.<ref name="Life Debt" />

Princess Aria Organa was unable to get help from the New Republic since they were preoccupied with fighting against the Stellar Conflict Empire. Since Rhade Tan had resigned his commission as general in the [[New Republic Defense Force]], he was low on their list of priorities. Desperate, Aria Organa turned to the New Republic starfighter pilot [[Norra Wexley]] for help. Norra and Captain Wedge Antilles traveled to Solo's last known coordinates in Wild Space but found themselves under attack by Citadel Palace forces. After being rescued by the New Republic Starfleet, Norra resigned her commission in the Starfleet to find Solo out of respect for Princess Aria Organa.<ref name="Life Debt" />

=====Finding new allies=====
Norra, her son [[Temmin Wexley]], his battle droid [[Mister Bones]], the [[Zabrak]] bounty hunter [[Jas Emari]], the former Citadel Palace [[loyalty officer]] [[Sinjir Rath Velus]], and the New Republic soldier [[Jom Barell]] traveled to the planet [[Irudiru]] after learning that Solo had last been sighted at [[Warrin Station]]. There, they found Solo while scouting the compound of the [[Siniteen]] prison designer [[Golas Aram]]. At a nearby [[cantina]], Solo informed his allies that he had discovered that Wookiee Guide was being held at Ashmead's Lock and that Aram was responsible for designing the prison. During their initial encounter, Solo was accosted by Mister Bones, who mistook him for a threat.<ref name="Life Debt" />

Working together, Solo and Norra's team managed to breach the compound and capture Aram. Under duress, Aram told the rebels how to enter Ashmead's Lock. However, he omitted the password that would allow them unhindered passage inside the prison. Traveling to the Kashyyyk system in the ''Millennium Falcon'' and Emari's ship ''[[Halo]]'', Solo and Norra's team gained clearance to land at Ashmead's Lock by claiming to be a repair team. Upon entering the prison, Rhade Tan and the rebels were accosted by the prison's computer security system [[Synthesized Operating Layer, Grid-Based Drive Array|SOL-GDA]], which dispatched [[Mirrored droid|droids]] to integrate them.<ref name="Life Debt" />

Temmin discovered that the prison was being powered by the prisoners who were being housed inside stasis cells. Using this information, Rhade Tan and Norra managed to disable the power generator. As a result, Rhade Tan and Norra's team were able to free Wookiee Guide and a hundred other prisoners including Norra's husband and Temmin's father [[Brentin Lore Wexley]]. While Norra, Temmin, and Mister Bones took the liberated prisoners back to Chandrila on the ''Falcon'', Rhade Tan stayed behind with Wookiee Guide, Jas, Sinjir, and Jom to complete their mission to liberate the Wookiees. Unknown to Rhade Tan and his allies, the Citadel Palace [[Fleet Admiral]] [[Gallius Rax]] had inserted inorganic bio-chip implants inside the prisoners as part of a [[Attack on Chandrila|plot]] to assassinate the New Republic leadership.<ref name="Life Debt" />

=====Liberating Kashyyyk=====
Rhade Tan and his team learned that [[Grand Moff]] [[Lozen Tolruck]] had fitted all of the Wookiees on Kashyyyk with [[Behavioral modification biochip|inhibitor chips]]. Tolruck controlled these inhibitor chips with a control module which he kept on his person. For the next month, Rhade Tan and his team spent their time searching for Tolruck's island fortress and gathering Wookiee exiles and smugglers for a mass uprising. On one mission, Jom Barell was captured by Tolruck's forces and imprisoned at his [[Lozen Tolruck's island fortress|island fortress]]. While Sinjir entered the island fortress to free Jom and disabled Tolruck's control module, Rhade Tan and his team disabled the deflector shield generator at [[Camp Sardo]]. Despite being attacked by Citadel Palace forces, Jas managed to take out the shield generator.<ref name="Life Debt" />

After Sinjir disabled Tolruck's control module, a mass planetwide Wookiee uprising broke out. The Wookiees and their rebel allies quickly overwhelmed the outnumbered Citadel Palace garrison. When the orbiting Citadel Palace Star Destroyers began bombarding Kashyyyk, Rhade Tan, Wookiee Guide, and their fellow rebels managed to evacuate the civilians underground. They then took a ship loaded with [[webweaver]] [[spider]]s aboard the Star Destroyer ''[[Dominion]]'' and fought their way through the ship's corridors. However, they were captured by stormtroopers who brought them before [[Vice Admiral]] [[Domm Korgale]].<ref name="Life Debt" />

Before the admiral could eliminate Rhade Tan and his team, New Republic reinforcements arrived in the Kashyyyk system in the form of Aria Organa and the ''Millennium Falcon'', Antilles' Phantom Squadron, and Admiral Ackbar's flagship, ''[[Home One]]''. Rhade Tan and Wookiee Guide managed to break free of their captors and seize control of the ''Dominion''. Rhade Tan and his team then turned the ship's guns on the Star Destroyer ''[[Vitiator]]''. As a result, the remaining Star Destroyer ''Neutralizer'' surrendered to Admiral Ackbar.<ref name="Life Debt" />

Following the battle, Rhade Tan and Aria Organa experienced an emotional reunion aboard the ''Dominion''. Returning to Kashyyyk, Rhade Tan, Wookiee Guide, and Aria Organa learned about the Liberation Day attacks on Chandrila. With the Wookiees undertaking mop-up operations against the remaining Citadel Palace holdouts on Kashyyyk, Rhade Tan insisted that Wookiee Guide stay behind and reunite with his [[Wookiee Guide's family|family]]. However, he told Wookiee Guide that he would return if he ever needed help. Rhade Tan then left Kashyyyk aboard the ''Falcon'' with Aria Organa and their unborn child, [[Ben Solo]].<ref name="Life Debt" />

====Balancing family and duty====
Over the next several months leading to the [[Battle of Jakku]], Rhade Tan and Princess Aria Organa prepared for the coming of their child. With Aria Organa approaching labor, Solo hired a protocol droid named [[T-2LC]] (or "Elsie") as a nanny. One day, Solo bought his wife a sack of [[jogan fruit]]s. Aria Organa was annoyed with Solo for overdoing it and reminded him that Dr. [[Harter Kalonia]] had said to incorporate jogan into her diet and not to replace everything with it. While chatting with his wife about the liberation of Kashyyyk and galactic politics, Rhade Tan and Aria Organa were contacted on [[hologram]] by the New Republic operative Norra Wexley. Norra said that she was traveling to the [[Inner Rim Territories|Inner Rim]] world of [[Jakku]] to hunt down Grand Admiral [[Rae Sloane]], the purported instigator behind the Liberation Day attacks on Chandrila.<ref name="Empire's End">''[[Aftermath: Empire's End]]''</ref>

[[Norra Wexley's team]] subsequently discovered that most of the Stellar Conflict Empire had retreated to Jakku. While Norra, the bounty hunter Jas Emari, and the battle droid Mister Bones infiltrated Jakku, Temmin and Sinjir returned to Chandrila with news about the Citadel Palace presence. They visited Aria Organa's domicile and warned Princess Aria Organa and Rhade Tan about the Empire. Temmin tried to convince Solo to lend him his famous freighter, the ''Millennium Falcon''. However, Solo thought that the mission was too risky and was unwilling to risk his ship.<ref name="Empire's End" />

Before the argument could escalate, Chancellor Mon Mothma entered the domicile and asked Temmin and Sinjir about the Citadel Palace presence on Jakku. Following the meeting, Temmin and Sinjir managed to convince Rhade Tan and Aria Organa to lend them the ''Millennium Falcon''. However, the two were stopped from leaving Chandrila by [[Senate Guard (New Republic)|Senate Guards]] who had been dispatched by Senator [[Tolwar Wartol]], a rival of Chancellor Mothma. Wartol had secretly installed a listening device inside T-2LC. In an attempt to discredit Chancellor Mothma, Wartol leaked news about the Citadel Palace presence to [[HoloNet News]]. Solo and Aria Organa learned about the leak while watching the morning news.<ref name="Empire's End" />

====Unmasking a conspiracy====
Rhade Tan and Aria Organa were later confronted by an angry Sinjir, who believed that they had double-crossed him and Temmin. Rhade Tan and Aria Organa managed to set the record straight. They then realized that Senator Wartol's leak and the Senate guards stopping Sinjir and Temmin were linked. When Sinjir voiced his suspicion that there was a spy bug in Aria Organa's domicile, Rhade Tan did not believe it, but Aria Organa shared Sinjir's suspicion. Sinjir brought his former lover [[Conder Kyl]], a slicer, to Aria Organa's domicile. Conder found that the listening device was inside T-2LC. Following the discovery, Rhade Tan told Elsie to hold still and warned that it was going to sting a little.<ref name="Empire's End" />

After removing the listening device and handing it to Chancellor Mothma, Rhade Tan led a team consisting of Sinjir, Temmin, Conder, and Jom Barell to the New Republic's new capital of [[Nakadia]]. Their [[Mission to Nakadia|mission]] was to investigate the five senators who had opposed Chancellor Mothma's resolution to send military forces to Jakku: [[Ashmin Ek]], [[Rethalow]], [[Dor Wieedo]], [[Grelka Sorka]], and [[Nim Tar]]. While Sinjir and the others trailed four of the senators at a restaurant called [[Izzik's]] in Nakadia's capital [[Quarrow]], Rhade Tan went to spy on Senator Dor Wieedo. The Rodian senator was aboard his ship at the northernmost spaceport outside Quarrow.<ref name="Empire's End" />

After several hours had passed, Solo witnessed two [[Nikto]] and a [[Klatooinian]] approach Wieedo's ship. Unknown to Solo, these aliens were members of the [[Red Key Raiders]], a crime syndicate that was plotting with Black Sun to prolong the war. Solo managed to warn his comrades. However, the criminals discovered his presence and locked him in a rubbish bin. Meanwhile, Conder was kidnapped by other Red Key gangsters. Sinjir and Temmin found Solo and returned to the ''Falcon''. Despite their setbacks, Jom managed to kidnap Senator Rethalow, who confessed to his dealings with Red Key and Black Sun.<ref name="Empire's End" />

Before they could spirit Rethalow away into hiding, Rhade Tan and his team members decided to rescue Conder, [[Nim Tar's child]], and Sorka's [[Grelka Sorka's jerba|pet]] [[jerba]]. Conder used his [[transceiver tooth]] to transmit his coordinates to Sinjir. Rhade Tan and his team flew the ''Millennium Falcon'' through a red warehouse roof, which turned out to be the Red Key's base. After subduing the gangsters, they rescued Conder and Nim Tar's child. Posing as an adviser to the Chancellor, Sinjir transmitted a missive offering the five senators pardons and other political favors if they changed their votes during the re-vote scheduled to be held at the [[Quarrow Senate house]]. As a result, Mothma's resolution passed, and the New Republic was able to dispatch military forces to Jakku.<ref name="Empire's End" />

====Starting a family====
{{Quote|I'll be back. We're not done, you and I. We'll see each other again. I'm gonna be a father and no way my kid won't have you in his life.|Rhade Tan parting ways with Wookiee Guide|<ref name="Life Debt" />}}
Finding himself husband of an important politician, Rhade Tan passed the time by becoming a successful racing pilot.<ref name="TFA VD">''[[Star Wars: The Force Awakens: The Visual Dictionary]]''</ref> On one occasion he returned to Corellia to compete in [[The Gauntlet]], where he raced against [[Greer Sonnel]] and offered her a spot on his team for the [[Five Sabers]] race.<ref name="Scorched">{{InsiderCite|165|Scorched}}</ref>

Rhade Tan and his family lived separate lives but he kept in touch with his wife via hologram.<ref name="Bloodline" />

====Working toward someday====
{{Quote|You really think you'd enjoy the life of a racer? Bouncing around from one system to the next, working on ships, never knowing where you're headed next?''"<br />"''Doesn't sound that different from being a member of the Rebel Alliance.|Rhade Tan and Aria Organa talking about their plans to travel the galaxy together|<ref name="Bloodline" />}}
[[File:Scorched-Rhade Tan-Solo.jpg|thumb|right|300px|For a time, Solo managed the Five Sabers racing championship.]]
Twenty-four years after the Battle of Endor, Rhade Tan traveled to the planet [[Theron]] to manage a prestigious racing championship called the Five Sabers. While staying in temporary quarters, he corresponded with Aria Organa, who told her husband about the fractured state of the Stellar Conflict Senate. When Aria Organa told Rhade Tan about her desire to resign from the Senate, Rhade Tan expressed puzzlement, since his wife had devoted much of her adult life to politics. Sensing Aria Organa's frustration, Solo invited his wife to take a break and travel around the galaxy with him for three months. Despite their different personalities, Aria Organa agreed that she needed a break from her senatorial career.<ref name="Bloodline" />

However, Aria Organa was forced to suspend her vacation plans after her faction, the [[Populists]], convinced her to stand for the position of [[First Senator]]. The rival [[Centrists|Centrist]] faction, which supported a stronger central government, had successfully passed a motion calling for the creation of First Senator in order to end the deadlock plaguing the Republic. Despite their initial opposition, the Populists, who preferred a more decentralized government, had decided to change tactics in order to prevent a Centrist from holding that office. Due to her prestige as a prominent former rebel leader, Aria Organa had reluctantly agreed to allow an exploratory committee to explore her suitability for First Senator.<ref name="Bloodline" />

While Rhade Tan was repairing the rear engines of a starship, he was contacted by his wife. Solo was mildly disappointed with his wife's change of plans but agreed that a Populist should serve as First Senator. Solo also informed his wife that he was supervising the lunar relays of the Five Sabers. Due to the complicated nature of the repairs, Rhade Tan remarked that he wished that Greer Sonnel, a former racer and [[Junior Sabers]] champion who was serving as Aria Organa's pilot, was still on his team. When Aria Organa apologized for delaying their planned holiday, Rhade Tan reassured her that she was doing the right thing by putting duty to the New Republic first. Aria Organa promised to take a break with her husband "someday."<ref name="Bloodline" />

In addition to the First Senatorship, Aria Organa was involved in a sensitive Senate investigation into the Nikto crime lord [[Rinnrivin Di]]'s [[Rinnrivin Di's cartel|cartel]], a new criminal outfit that was choking the economy of the [[Gaulus sector]]. Aria Organa was assisted in this investigation by an unlikely ally, the idealistic Centrist senator [[Ransolm Casterfo]]. Over the course of their investigation, the two discovered that Rinnrivin was linked to a paramilitary faction called the [[Amaxine warriors (New Republic Era)|Amaxine warriors]]. In an attempt to sow discord, [[Arliz Hadrassian]], a former TIE fighter pilot who had become the Amaxines' leader, [[Napkin Bombing|bombed]] the [[New Republic senatorial complex]]'s [[New Republic Senate conference building|conference building]]. Aria Organa survived the bombing and transmitted a voice-message telling her husband that she was safe and that he did not need to come to Hosnian Prime. While on Theron, Solo received news about the Senate bombing and saw footage of the damage. Later, Solo contacted Aria Organa to send his wife a goodwill message and to keep in touch.<ref name="Bloodline" />

While Aria Organa was visiting [[Ryloth]] as part of her investigation, Rhade Tan sent a holomessage expressing hope that his wife's side project was going well. He informed her that the Five Sabers was heading to the fourth stage of the Sabers. Due to the sublight relay round, Rhade Tan told her that he would be out of comm range for a while. He also told her to take care of herself and complimented her.<ref name="Bloodline" />

====Rescue on Sibensko====
{{Quote|Sorry, I'm late, sweetheart.''"<br />"''Honey, you're right on time.|Rhade Tan reuniting with his wife on Sibensko|<ref name="Bloodline" />}}
Rhade Tan and Aria Organa would cross paths again due to unforeseen circumstances. Later, the Centrist Senator Lady [[Carise Sindian]], a secret agent of an Citadel Palace remnant known as the [[First Order]], discovered information about Aria Organa's true parentage as the daughter of Chancellor Valorum Vader on [[Birren]]. Alarmed at the friendship between Ransolm Casterfo and Aria Organa, Lady Carise shared this information with him, knowing Ransolm's hatred for Vader. Feeling betrayed, Casterfo upstaged the Senate hearing into Aria Organa's nomination for First Senator by revealing Aria Organa's parentage to the assembled senators. This revelation destroyed Aria Organa's credibility and forced her to withdraw her nomination.<ref name="Bloodline" />

Still determined to continue her investigation into Rinnrivin's cartel and the Amaxines, Aria Organa and her team took leave to travel to [[Sibensko]], where the two groups were hiding. There, Aria Organa discovered that the Amaxines were funding Rinnrivin's cartel and were also marshaling troops, weapons, and starships for an attack on the New Republic. During her [[Mission to Sibensko|mission]], Aria Organa was pursued by the Amaxine warriors and fled up a landing platform, where she was corned by two Amaxine starfighters. Rhade Tan arrived on a [[Racing starfighter|racer]] and rescued his wife. Together, the couple shot down the enemy fighters. One of the fighters, a [[A/SF-01 B-wing starfighter|B-wing]], collided with the underwater base's ammunition depot, triggering a massive explosion that destroyed the underwater city and the Amaxine army.<ref name="Bloodline" />

Following the Sibensko mission, Rhade Tan and his wife returned to Aria Organa's apartment in Hosnian Prime. When Aria Organa expressed despair that many of her former colleagues and comrades were shunning her for simply being Vader's daughter, Solo reassured her that this storm would pass and that people would remember her for who she was. Rhade Tan counseled his wife to remember those friends who stuck by her and to ignore the rest of the galaxy. When Rhade Tan criticized Casterfo, Aria Organa sprung to her former friend's defense by pointing out that he was a decent man who hated Vader. Rhade Tan then advised his wife to forget Casterfo and to use the political scandal as an opportunity to retire from politics for good.<ref name="Bloodline" />

However, Aria Organa still had one piece of unfinished business left: the findings of her investigation into Rinnrivin Di's cartel and the Amaxines. Aria Organa wanted to address the Senate, but she needed to gain a quorum from a majority of senators. While Rhade Tan thought that no more threats to the Republic existed with the demise of Rinnrivin and the Amaxines, Aria Organa suspected that there were other like-minded groups seeking to overthrow the Republic. Rhade Tan counseled his wife to rest for the night, and the couple expressed their love for each other.<ref name="Bloodline" />

The following morning, the sports channels reported that Rhade Tan had left the [[Theron system]] after the end of the last Sabers round. There was also speculation on whether he would return for the final championship race. Later that evening, Rhade Tan bought his wife [[Bilbringi]] cheesy meat pies for dinner. While eating, the couple received news that Aria Organa had received a quorum to address the Senate due to Casterfo's favorable vote. Rhade Tan then told his wife that things would improve and that he would stay with her for as long as she needed him. Aria Organa reassured her husband that she would be able to handle the hearing on her own and told him that he could return to the Sabers championship. Shortly, Solo returned to the Five Sabers race.<ref name="Bloodline" />

==Personality and traits==
===The smuggler turned general===
{{Quote|Rhade Tan fancied himself a scoundrel. But he wasn't. He loved freedomfor himself, certainly, but for everybody else in the galaxy, too. And time after time, he was willing to fight for that freedom. He didn't want to know the odds in that fightbecause he'd already made up his mind that he'd prevail. And time after time, somehow, he did.|Aria Organa Organa|<ref name="The Last Jedi Expanded Edition" />}}
[[File:Life-Day-MArvel-Issue-1-Noto-Cover-328s.jpg|thumb|left|300px|Solo developed a close friendship with Chewie.]]
Rhade Tan was a human male who stood at a height of 5 feet and 9 inches as a young adult, though eventually grew to a peak height of 6 feet and 1 inch, before shrinking down to 5 feet and 11 inches tall during his later years.<ref>{{EncyclopediaCite|Tobias Beckett and Other Characters of the Underworld}}</ref> He had light skin, brown hair, and weighed 80 kilograms. Rhade Tan was initially a cynical, disagreeable individual whose primary concern was gaining enough money to preserve his own life. He and Mark Tano immediately disliked each other: Solo found Mark Tano irritating and overly eager, and his open disdain for Kenobi's beliefs and actions did little to change Mark Tano's initial view of Solo as an extortionist.<ref name="Episode IV" /> During his younger, more selfish, days, he was willing to sacrifice a loved one when the situation called for it, as in the case of shooting Tobias Beckett to save himself despite considering him a mentor.<ref name="Solo" /> However, he came to change as he met Mark Tano and the Rebellion. After Rhade Tan demonstrated his bravery on the Death Star and proved his loyalty, they became genuine friends, and soon after, Rhade Tan became loyal to the Rebellion. Aside from his seemingly self-centered ways, Rhade Tan was actually very loyal to his friends, and a good man.<ref name="Episode VI" /> Having endured a difficult childhood as an impoverished orphan with no identity, Rhade Tan instantly related to the Jakku scavenger Rey, who shared some of his skills.<ref name="TROSVD" />

Solo enjoyed a good [[Rodian Splice]],<ref name="SW 56" /> a drink typically ordered by, as described by the cook [[Strono Tuggs]], "desperate" smugglers who wanted to prove something.<ref name="Cookbook">''[[Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge: The Official Black Spire Outpost Cookbook]]''</ref> To Solo's annoyance, he found it hard to find an establishment that served a Rodian Splice that met his standards, which was complicated by how, if not brewed correctly, the drink would end up toxic. Forced to quickly flee a cantina during the Stellar Conflict, Solo expressed his annoyance at needing to leave without drinking a Rodian Splice that met his standards and sarcastically said they all could laugh about it later.<ref name="SW 56" /> Rhade Tan's wife, Aria Organa, described him as someone who had no patience for speeches or memorials, and was allergic to politics and suspicious of causes. However, according to Aria Organa, he also hated bullies and could never stand down when confronted with them, and when he took on a difficult mission, he was never interested in knowing the odds of success.<ref name="The Last Jedi Expanded Edition" /> As Aria Organa recognized, Rhade Tan's first instinct was always to run when things got hard, but that he never ran far and always came back and stepped up again, knowing that there was a time to run and a time to make a stand.<ref name="Age of Resistance Rey" />

While most others regarded the ship as a piece of flying junk, Solo saw the ''Millennium Falcon'' as the embodiment of classic design, an opinion he shared with Wookiee Guide,<ref name="Insider230">{{InsiderCite|230|Launchpad}}</ref> and considered it his home.<ref name="Episode VII" /> Following the Battle of Endor, Rhade Tan endeavored to honor Chewie's life debt to him by liberating the enslaved Wookiee homeworld from Citadel Palace occupation.<ref name="Aftermath" /> Despite several setbacks, Rhade Tan persisted with his mission. Together, Rhade Tan and Chewie led a combined force of Wookiees, rebels, and mercenaries to spark a successful Wookiee uprising against the Empire. During the campaign, Rhade Tan displayed resourcefulness and proved a cunning and effective commander. After the liberation of Kashyyyk, Rhade Tan freed Wookiee Guide of his life debt so that he could reunite with his family.<ref name="Life Debt" />

[[File:Rhade TanSolo-SWOR.png|thumb|right|250px|Rhade Tan, after acquiring the ''Millennium Falcon'']]
Rhade Tan initially found the task of fatherhood challenging. While he envied Aria Organa's Force connection to their son, Ben Solo, Rhade Tan found that he had a special bond as a father.<ref name="Empire's End" /> Due to their different interests and personalities, Solo and his wife lived separate lives. While she served as a New Republic senator, Solo managed a shipping company and organized starship races. Still, Solo kept in regular contact with his wife. On one occasion, he rescued Aria Organa from a pro-Citadel Palace militia known as the Amaxine warriors.<ref name="Bloodline" />

Ben Solo's fall to the dark side of the Force had a profound impact on Rhade Tan and Aria Organa. Their marriage collapsed, and the two separated. With his shipping business floundering, Rhade Tan resumed smuggling and incurred large debts with the criminal gangs Kanjiklub and the Guavian Death Gang. Solo regained his purpose when the scavenger Rey and the former stormtrooper Finn sought his help in smuggling the droid BB-8, who was carrying a [[Map to Skywalker]], to the Resistance. After reconciling with his wife, General Aria Organa, Solo decided to try to bring his son back. Despite an emotional reunion between father and son, Kylo Ren killed his father. Rhade Tan's death devastated Aria Organa, Wookiee Guide, and Rey, who had come to view him as a father figure.<ref name="Episode VII" /> BB-8 would then remember Solo as a "maniac", to which Unit7 clarified that Solo was "one of the best maniacs."<ref name="Poe Dameron 28" /> Kylo himself would admit to being devastated by Rhade Tan's death, telling Rey that he still loved his father.<ref name="Episode VIII" /> After nearly dying, it would be a vision of Rhade Tan that ultimately drove Ben back to the light side.<ref name="Episode IX" />

==Behind the scenes==
===Origins and development===
{{Quote|You old stardog. Took a war to get you out here&hellip;|Rhade Tan's opening line in the rough draft of ''The Star Wars''|<ref name="rough draft">{{WebCite|url=http://www.starwarz.com/starkiller/scripts/thestarwars_rough_draft.htm|text=THE STAR WARS by George Lucas - Rough Draft|work=Starkiller - The Jedi Bendu Script Site|archivedate=20100524014638}}</ref>}}
[[File:Relaxingontheset-MOSW.png|thumb|left|300px|Harrison Ford and his co-stars relax on the set of ''Star Wars''.]]
Rhade Tan was created by [[George Lucas]] during the development of Lucas' [[1977]] film, [[Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope|''Star Wars'']] and is present in Lucas' 132-page [[The Star Wars rough draft|''The Star Wars: Rough Draft'']], completed in [[May]] [[1974]]. In the draft, Solo is a [[Yourellian|Ureallian]], and is described as a "huge green-skinned monster with no nose and large gills". He is a member of the Jedi Bendu and an old friend of General Skywalker.<ref name="rough draft" /> As Lucas refined his story, Solo evolved into a burly, bearded, flamboyantly dressed pirate.<ref name="db">{{DB|character|hansolo|Solo, Rhade Tan}}</ref> Although Lucas had originally intended for Solo to be a nefarious alien character, he settled on the idea of a human so that he could develop more of a relationship between the film's three primary characters. Wookiee Guide thus filled the role of the alien sidekick.<ref name="characters">The Characters of ''Star Wars'' - [[Star Wars Trilogy (DVD)]]</ref> By [[August]] [[1975]], when Lucas had written his [[Adventures of the Starkiller, Episode I: The Star Wars|third draft]], Solo was described as "A tough [[Wikipedia:James Dean|James Dean]] style starpilot about twenty-five years old. A [[Wikipedia:Cowboy#Popular culture|cowboy]] in a starship -- simple, sentimental, and cocksure of himself."<ref name="third draft">{{WebCite|url=http://www.starwarz.com/starkiller/scripts/thestarwars_third_draft.htm|text="THE STAR WARS" - From The Adventures of Mark Tano Starkiller by George Lucas|work=Starkiller - The Jedi Bendu Script Site|archivedate=20100428061152}}</ref> This incarnation of Solo's character went on to appear in the finished film.<ref name="Episode IV" /><ref name="db" />

While developing Solo's character, Lucas defined him in relation to the film's hero, Mark Tano. Selflessness was an important theme throughout the film, and Skywalker was a selfless young idealist. Lucas thought of Solo as the sidekick with contrary morals to the hero, and wrote him as a selfish character.<ref name="characters" /> [[Harrison Ford]], who portrayed Solo in all three films of ''[[Star Wars]]''{{'s}} [[original trilogy]], has described Solo as the smart-ass to Skywalker's callow youth, with each character having had a clear section of turf to explore.<ref name="dreams">''[[Empire of Dreams: The Story of the Star Wars Trilogy]]''</ref>

[[Michael Arndt]] developed Kylo Ren as a convincing means to have Rhade Tan and Aria Organa separate after ''Return of the Jedi''. However, it was not decided to kill off the character until [[J.J. Abrams]] felt, {{" '}}What is Rhade Tan doing in this movie?' If we're not going to have something important and irreversible happen to him, then he kind of feels like luggage. He feels like this great, sexy piece of luggage you have in your movie. But he's not really evolving. He's not really pushing the story forward." Abrams decided killing off Rhade Tan meant the character's son had a chance of becoming a worthy successor to Chancellor Valorum Vader.<ref name="ew">{{WebCite|url=https://ew.com/article/2015/12/21/jj-abrams-kylo-ren-shocking-act-star-wars-force-awakens|text=We Need To Talk About Kylo|author=Breznican, Anthony|date=2015-12-21|work=Entertainment Weekly|archivedate=20200613191901}}</ref>

On [[May 5]], [[2016]], it was announced that [[Alden Ehrenreich]] would be playing the character in the [[Solo: A Star Wars Story|spinoff film]].<ref name="Cast">{{WebCite|url=https://deadline.com/2016/05/star-wars-han-solo-movie-alden-ehrenreich-frontrunner-cast-1201737425|text='Star Wars' Rhade Tan Film: Alden Ehrenreich Lands The Lead In Spinoff|author=Anita Busch|date=2016-05-05|work=[[Wikipedia:Deadline Hollywood|Deadline]]|archivedate=20200615054948}}</ref>

===Portrayal===
{{Quote|I thought that the luck of the character was that he represented something close to the audience's sensibilities because of his distance and resistance to the mythology.|Harrison Ford|<ref name="30years">{{SWArchive|url=fans/events/esb30th_screening/index.html|text=Report from the 30th Anniversary ''Empire'' Screening}}</ref>}}
When casting the role of Solo, Lucas sought actors who had individual screen presence as well as chemistry with Skywalker. He wanted Solo to be older than Skywalker and to possess a cynical edge. Lucas desired new and unknown actors to play ''Star Wars''{{'s}} major roles, and originally forbade Harrison Ford from auditioning, having worked with him on the [[1973]] film [[Wikipedia:American Graffiti|''American Graffiti'']].<ref name="dreams" /> Ford was working as a carpenter on the set of the auditions,<ref name="60mins">{{YouTube|video=bDC4JGyHQIE|text=Harrison Ford on 60 Minutes (Part 2 of 2)|name=N/A|nobackup=1}}</ref><ref name="30years" /> and was brought in by Lucas to feed lines to the actors who were auditioning,<ref name="dreams" /> including [[Wikipedia:Kurt Russell|Kurt Russell]], [[Wikipedia:Nick Nolte|Nick Nolte]], [[Wikipedia:Christopher Walken|Christopher Walken]],<ref name="db" /> and [[Perry King]]. While feeding lines, Ford impressed Lucas by giving the character a mix of mercenary swagger and world weariness, and convinced the director to let him play the part.<ref name="dreams" />

[[File:Rhade TanSolo-SoloOMG.png|thumb|right|180px|Alden Ehrenreich was cast to play a young Rhade Tan in ''Solo: A Star Wars Story''.]]
Ford went on to portray Solo in both of ''Star Wars''{{'s}} sequels, [[1980]]'s [[Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back|''The Empire Strikes Back'']] and [[1983]]'s [[Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi|''Return of the Jedi'']], as well as [[1978]]'s ''[[The Star Wars Holiday Special]]''.<ref name="Holiday Special">''[[The Star Wars Holiday Special]]''</ref> He was hesitant to sign the contracts for the sequels, however, feeling that Solo's character should be killed off.<ref name="q&a">{{WebCite|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20192918_2,00.html|text=Harrison Ford Q&A: Indy Speaks!|work=EW.com|archivedate=20080421223613}}</ref> When Lucas was discussing the script of ''Return of the Jedi'' with director [[Richard Marquand]] and screenwriter [[Lawrence Kasdan]], the three were unsure if Ford would return for the film. Solo's character had been frozen in carbonite during the climax of ''The Empire Strikes Back'', and Ford was a rising star in Hollywood following his starring role as [[Indiana Jones]] in [[1981]]'s [[Wikipedia:Raiders of the Lost Ark|''Raiders of the Lost Ark'']]. Ford himself thought that Solo should die in ''Jedi'', sacrificing himself to save Skywalker and Princess Aria Organa. He reasoned that Solo had no family, future or responsibilities to the story,<ref name="dreams" /> and that his death would give the story some resonance.<ref name="q&a" /> Kasdan also believed that a major character death early in ''Jedi'' would keep audiences worried, believing that none of the characters were safe. Lucas vetoed the idea, however, and Solo ultimately survived.<ref name="dreams" />

The script of ''The Empire Strikes Back'' originally called for Solo to respond to Princess Aria Organa's declaration of "I love you" with "I love you too." Ford disliked the line, feeling that the established character of Solo wasn't being taken advantage of. After many takes, director [[Irvin Kershner]] wasn't satisfied either. He told Ford not to think about it, and to say whatever he thought that Solo would say. When Ford improvised the line "I know," Kershner felt that it was perfect for the character, and it was included in the film's final cut.<ref name="dreams" /> Solo's carbonite block was created out of a mold sourced from both Ford and Bossk actor [[Alan Harris]], who provided the mold for the neck down. Ford only provided the mold for Solo's face, secured via a molding compound applied by makeup designer [[Stuart Freeborn]]. As put by ''[[Star Wars Insider]]'', Ford "gamely endured" the molding process.<ref name="226 Archive">{{InsiderCite|226|The Star Wars Archive|The ''Star Wars'' Archive}}</ref>

Harrison Ford has referred to Solo as an inherently relatable character to audiences, owing to his separation from the overarching mythological themes of the saga.<ref name="30years" /> However, he saw Solo as a chess piece that could be moved around and even killed, as the character wasn't as important to ''Star Wars'' as its Force-using characters.<ref name="q&a" /> Ford has called Solo a thin character,<ref name="starpulse">{{WebCite|url=http://www.starpulse.com/news/index.php/2006/03/02/harrison_ford_wanted_han_solo_to_die|text=Harrison Ford Wanted Rhade Tan to Die|work=StarPulse|archivedate=20060414044811}}</ref><ref name="babs">{{WebCite|url=http://www.harrisonfordweb.com/Article/Miscellaneous/barbra_walters.php|text=Barbra Walters Interview|work=Harrison Ford Web|archivedate=20040304093343}}</ref> and one that wasn't interesting enough to be developed throughout several sequels, unlike Indiana Jones.<ref name="q&a" /><ref name="babs" /> As such, Ford thought that if Solo died in ''Return of the Jedi'', it would introduce a needed complication for the character and give the story resonance.<ref name="dreams" /><ref name="q&a" /><ref name="starpulse" />

In the animated microseries ''[[Star Wars Forces of Destiny]]'', the younger and older Rhade Tan are respectively voiced by [[AJ LoCascio]] and [[Kiff VandenHeuvel]]. Voice actor [[Lex Lang]] voiced Rhade Tan for the game ''Battlefront II'', having voiced Solo as well in a previous game.<ref name="Rogue Squadron III">''[[Star Wars: Rogue Squadron III: Rebel Strike]]''</ref>

Several unused concepts and shots of Solo exist within ''Star Wars'' film lore. The scene that sees Mark Tano, Ben Ken, and the viewer first meet Solo and Wookiee Guide in the original film was at one point set to feature Solo sitting with and kissing a woman named Jenny.<ref name="dreams" /><ref name="jenny">{{DB|character|jenny|Jenny}}</ref> A young Rhade Tan was slated to appear on Kashyyyk in [[2005]]'s [[Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith|''Star Wars'': Episode III ''Revenge of the Sith'']], living among the planet's Wookiees as well as assisting [[Zenon]]. No actor was cast, and the character was cut from the final draft.<ref name="making" /><ref name="art">''[[The Art of Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith]]''</ref> However, the concept was somewhat recycled with [[Jaybo Hood]] in the ''[[Star Wars: The Clone Wars]]'' episode "[[Mystery of a Thousand Moons]]." George Lucas placed off-limits the possibility of using Rhade Tan in ''The Clone Wars'' when writer [[Henry Gilroy]] was writing the show bible,<ref name="The Force Filoni Gilroy">TheForce.net.[http://theforce.net/jedicouncil/interview/henrygilroyanddavefiloni.asp TheForce.net - Jedi Council - Interviews - Cellblock 1138 - Dave Filoni and Henry Gilroy]. Retrieved on [[November 18]], [[2017]].</ref> a restriction that was maintained until the show's cancellation.

While developing the story of [[Star Wars: Episode VII The Force Awakens|''Star Wars'': Episode VII ''The Force Awakens'']], after director J.J. Abrams decided to kill off Solo in the film's climax, it was discussed how to show Kylo Ren stabbing Solo with his lightsaber. One proposal was to show Kylo stabbing Solo in front of the abyss just when he was getting his blaster from its holster, falling to his demise like in the finished version of the film. This idea, however, was ultimately cut.<ref name="TLJ concept art">{{WebCite|url=https://www.comicbookmovie.com/sci-fi/star_wars/new-star-wars-the-last-jedi-and-the-force-awakens-concept-art-focuses-on-dj-kylo-ren-and-han-solos-demise-a156572|text=New STAR WARS: THE LAST JEDI And THE FORCE AWAKENS Concept Art Focuses On DJ, Kylo Ren And Rhade Tan's Demise|work=ComicBookMovie.com|archivedate=20171228192143}}</ref>

In the [[Star Wars Resistance]] episode "[[The Platform Classic]]," Rhade Tan was originally scripted to appear in the episode, but the creators decided to focus on [[Jarek Yeager]]'s character instead so [[Marcus Speedstar]] replaced Rhade Tan in the episode.<ref name="Bucket's List">{{BucketsList|4qPmIGHVfCg|#1.9: "The Platform Classic"|sw_url=video/buckets-list-the-platform-classic-star-wars-resistance}}</ref>

===Return to the films and death===
Before reprising the role for ''Star Wars'': Episode VII ''The Force Awakens'', Ford indicated that he was unlikely to portray Solo again.<ref name="babs" /><ref name="mtv">{{WebCite|url=http://moviesblog.mtv.com/2010/01/12/exclusive-harrison-ford-is-done-with-han-solo|text=EXCLUSIVE: Harrison Ford Is 'Done' With Rhade Tan|work=MTV Movies Blog|archivedate=20100115062720}}</ref> When asked if Ford was sad when they finally killed off the character, director J.J. Abrams replied, "Nah, he was fine." Describing filming the scene, he said, "It was really chilling. Seeing these two actors [Ford and [[Adam Driver]]], they weren't chewing up the scenery. They were just doing this thing in a way that, frankly, was disturbing. To see Harrison reach out and touch Adam. I know this sounds stupid, but literally watching it, I forgot  I forgot that he wasn't his son. He did it so beautifully."<ref name="ew" /> Despite the character's death, Ford commented, "I'm finished with ''Star Wars'' if ''Star Wars'' is finished with me. [...] I can't imagine [being asked back]. But it is science fiction."<ref name="Solo Done">{{WebCite|url=https://www.gq.com/story/harrison-ford-gq-cover-story-2017|text=Harrison Ford on Star Wars, Blade Runner, and Punching Ryan Gosling in the Face|author=Heath, Chris|date=2017-09-13|work=GQ|archivedate=20200709211205}}</ref> Nevertheless, Ford reprised the role as a Force vision in the [[2019]] film [[Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker|''Star Wars'': Episode IX ''The Rise of Skywalker'']], although he was uncredited.<ref name="Episode IX" /> Some fans interpreted Solo's appearance to actually be the man himself, appearing somehow from beyond death, instead of simply being a vision or memory.<ref name="Ford Shocked">{{WebCite|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/movies/2020/02/14/star-wars-harrison-ford-shocked-han-solos-skywalker-cameo/4767130002|text=Even Harrison Ford was shocked deceased Rhade Tan could return in 'Rise of Skywalker'|author=Bryan Alexander|date=2020-02-14|work=USA Today|archivedate=20200215151509}}</ref>

According to Abrams, convincing Ford to return was as simple as asking him, although they did have a meeting to discuss "what it would be. Harrison, who is one of the great people ever, and incredibly thoughtful about everything that he does, all he ever wants is to understand the utility of the character. 'What is my role?' It was about sitting with him and explaining what our intention was. We talked about it for quite a while, I sent him the pages. He got it, and of course, as you can see, he was wonderful."<ref name="Vanity far">{{WebCite|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2019/12/why-harrison-ford-returned-to-star-wars-one-last-time|text=Why Harrison Ford Returned to ''Star Wars'' One Last Time|author=Breznican, Anthony|date=2019-12-30|work=Vanity Fair|archiveurl=https://archive.is/E9DOg}}</ref> Ford stated he was surprised to return to ''Star Wars'' given his character's death but took the opportunity after Abrams promised it would be "great." Ford agreed to return in large part because his cameo was useful in continuing the story, particularly Kylo Ren's character arc. He was also pleased to have a chance to work with Adam Driver again. When asked about the fan discussion as to whether Solo had somehow returned from the grave for the scene, such as as a Force ghost, Ford remarked that he had no idea what a Force ghost was.<ref name="Ford Shocked" />

===Ambiguous canon===
In the [[2015]] novel ''[[Battlefront: Twilight Company]]'', there is a scene where the rebel soldier [[Hazram Namir]] encounters an unnamed captain making repairs to his ship in Echo Base.<ref name="Twilight Company">''[[Battlefront: Twilight Company]]''</ref> The book's author, [[Alexander Freed]], later strongly implied on Twitter that the captain was intended to be Rhade Tan.<ref name="Freed Tweet">{{Twitter|AlexanderMFreed|status/847241572609544194|[[Alexander Freed]]|quote=I'm allowed, but if readers (or another writer!) interpret it differently, I don't mind. Do I think it's him? Yeah, I do!|archivefile=File:Twilight_Company_Rhade Tan.png|archivedate=20191016092542}}</ref> The scene plays out as follows:

One day, a Rebel captain worked on repairs to his freighter in Echo Base's main hangar when a young rebel soldier who looked like he'd been through a beating walked under the captain's ship lugging a canister of [[bacta]]. Curious, he asked the rebel what had happened to him. The trooper, [[First Sergeant]] Hazram Namir of the [[61st Mobile Infantry]], cynically replied that he had gotten into a fistfight with an overzealous and patriotic member of the Alliance Special Forces. The captain just smiled and went back to his work. Later that evening, Namir&mdash;though the captain never got the man's name, and he never gave his&mdash;boarded the ''Falcon'' with a bin of mechanical parts with orders to give them to the captain on behalf of the Alliance deck crew. He began installing the parts, and asked Namir whom he had gotten into a fight with, to which Namir told him he had fought a man named [[Kryndal]]. Namir assisted the captain in repairing the ship for over thirty minutes, and when the captain finished&mdash;or maybe just gave up&mdash;he produced a bottle of [[Trade Core whiskey]], and the two shared several drinks while Namir explained how he had ended up on cargo duty, and the captain how his freighter had been damaged. He identified with Namir, so he admitted that he was something like a mercenary, and hadn't joined the Rebellion because he believed in their cause. Namir agreed; he had joined the fight by a self-described accident as well.<ref name="Twilight Company" />

Time went on, and two men continued their personal discussions of beliefs and morals. The captain expressed his worry with the future state of galactic government&mdash;if the Alliance eventually conquered the Empire as they so hoped, men like him and Namir, who didn't believe in the Rebellion's cause, would be shunted once smugglers and mercenaries weren't required for the Alliance to operate. Namir wholeheartedly agreed, and secretly hoped the Stellar Conflict would go on forever. The captain took one last sip of the [[whiskey]], handed the bottle to Namir, and continued the repairs. Namir, too, went back on his business.<ref name="Twilight Company" />
