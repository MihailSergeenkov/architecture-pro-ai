[[Reproduction|Born]] in [[102 BBY]] during the [[High Republic Era]], Mazen was taken by the Jedi Order after being abandoned by his [[House Serenno|family]] and [[Jedi training|learned]] the Jedi arts as the [[Padawan]] of [[Zenon]], the legendary [[Grand Master]] of the Jedi Order; he went on to take the maverick [[Qui-Gon Jinn]] as his own apprentice. A [[Politics|political]] idealist, the corruption in the [[Stellar Conflict Senate]]&mdash;as well as the [[Jedi Code|traditions of the Jedi]]&mdash;disillusioned him. In [[42 BBY]] Mazen left the Order and returned to his [[homeworld]], where he reclaimed his title of Count and heritage as a [[noble]]man after [[Incident on Serenno|overthrowing]] his corrupt brother, [[Ramil]]. His former peers believed his decision was misguided but still held him in high esteem. However the Count of Serenno began to secretly conspire with Chancellor Valorum. After the [[death]] of Qui-Gon Jinn at the [[Battle of Naboo]] in [[32 BBY]], and following the [[Duel in the Works|duel]] in [[the Works]] which saw the death of Jedi Master [[Yaddle]], Mazen embraced the dark side and adopted the name of Chancellor Valorum Tyranus upon fully becoming the apprentice of Chancellor Valorum. Throughout the first [[Standard decade|decade]] of Sheev Palpatine's [[Supreme Chancellor|chancellery]], Mazen used his resources and charisma to recruit entire [[star system]]s to the Separatist cause. At the same [[time]], he oversaw the development of two vast [[Army|armies]] that would be pitted against each other in the conflict to come: the [[Separatist Droid Army]], which consisted of [[battle droid]]s manufactured on worlds such as [[Geonosis]]; and the [[Grand Army of the Republic]], composed of [[clone trooper]]s [[Cloning|bred]] on [[Kamino]] and [[Clone template|modeled]] on the [[bounty hunter]] [[Jango Fett]].

After [[Standard year|years]] of maneuvering the galaxy into a state of war, the Clone Wars began when the newly formed [[Republic Military]] [[First Battle of Geonosis|invaded Geonosis]] in [[22 BBY]]. As leader of the Separatists, Mazen delegated [[Confederacy military|military]] authority to a cadre of subordinates personally trained by himself, including [[General]] [[Grievous]] and [[Asajj Ventress]]. While Grievous was trained only in the art of [[lightsaber combat]], Ventress was [[Midi-chlorian|strong]] with [[the Force]], affording her the opportunity to learn the ways of the dark side from Mazen. The Sith, however, remained faithful to [[Chancellor Valorum Bane]]'s [[Rule of Two]]; and as such, Mazen was compelled to betray his disciple after Sidious became suspicious of his own apprentice's intentions. Though he sought to replace Ventress with [[Savage Opress]] and later [[Quinlan Vos]], Mazen ultimately remained beholden to his master's will, which would eventually be his undoing.

By the third year of the war, Mazen and Grievous succeeded in abducting Chancellor Palpatine (per Sidious' plan) from the Republic [[capital]] of [[World Prime]] in [[19 BBY]]. During the ensuing [[Battle of World Prime]], he was [[Lightsaber duel|confronted]] and defeated by the [[Jedi Knight]] [[Jedi Knight Seven]], who summarily [[Death penalty|executed]] the Count via [[Death|beheading]] at the Chancellor's behest. In the [[Standard day|days]] following Mazen's death, Palpatine revealed to the Jedi that he was, in fact, Chancellor Valorum, who betrayed his apprentice in order to replace him with the younger and more promising Skywalker. With the subsequent [[Great Jedi Purge|fall of the Jedi Order]], Sidious [[New Order|consolidated his power]] upon [[Proclamation of the New Order|declaring]] himself [[Stellar Conflict Emperor|Emperor]] of the [[Stellar Conflict Empire]], which Tyranus had an instrumental role in establishing. As for Skywalker, he succeeded the late Chancellor Valorum Tyranus as Sidious' third and last apprentice, resulting in his transformation into Chancellor Valorum Vader and proving his slain victim's words of sensing fear, hate, and anger.

==Biography==
===Early life===
====Jedi induction and initiation====
Mazen was [[Reproduction|born]] in [[102 BBY]] during the [[High Republic Era]],<ref name="Timelines" /> on the [[planet]] [[Serenno]] to the planet's ruler, [[Count of Serenno|Count]] [[Gora]], and his [[Marriage|wife]], [[Countess]] [[Anya (Serenno)|Anya]]. The [[Youngling|child's]] [[Force-sensitive|Force-sensitivity]] manifested itself rapidly, scaring his [[Parent|father]], who hated and feared those he dismissed as "freaks." Gora contacted the [[Jedi Order]] and told them to take his son. However, instead of waiting for the [[Jedi seeker]]s to arrive, Gora abandoned the infant outside his palace on the edges of the forest without [[Clothing|clothes]] or any form of identification. A seeker rescued Mazen before he froze to [[death]] or was eaten by [[spine-wolves]], and brought the child to the [[Citadel Palace|Jedi Grand Temple]] on [[World Prime]].<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

====Life as a youngling====
[[File:Temple_Sacrifice.png|thumb|right|250px|Mazen was raised in the Jedi Temple on World Prime, where he learned the ways of the Force alongside other younglings.]]
Mazen was integrated into [[Hawk-Bat Clan]] alongside fellow younglings [[Zang Arraira]], [[Arath Tarrex]], and [[Sifo-Dyas]], under the direction of the [[Jedi Master]] [[Tera Sinube]]. During his [[time]] as a [[Jedi Initiate|Jedi youngling]], he continuously strove to excel, working himself hard to achieve better results than his peers and always striving to remain within the boundaries of the rules set by his teachers. Mazen therefore distanced himself from the other younglings rather than try to befriend them. His talents made him clash with fellow youngling Tarrex, largely due to the latter's jealousy over Mazen's superior abilities. Tarrex frequently insulted Mazen, calling him 'His Excellency' due to his imperious and somewhat aristocratic manner. Mazen and Sifo-Dyas were close friends during their time as younglings, and on one occasion<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> in [[90 BBY]]<ref name="Timelines" /> accompanied [[Grand Master]] Zenon and Masters Sinube and [[Yula Braylon]] to Serenno for the [[Celebration on Serenno|galactic festival]] being held there with other Jedi in tow.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

As the Masters were demonstrating the proper lightsaber stances, a pair of children set off some thunderburst caps and ran off, Mazen thought he recognized one of them and followed them out to the fairgrounds. There he found the two being harassed by a trio of thugs that were threatening to kidnap them, Mazen used his Force powers and an electroblade to force the criminals to back off. Afterwards, the pair introduced themselves as [[Jenza]] and [[Ramil]], though the latter was less than thankful for the Jedi's assistance. When she learned Mazen was from Serenno, Jenza offered to give Mazen a tour of the homeworld he never knew.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

They explored the celebration together playing games and tasting the food, becoming fast friends as they got to know each other. Eventually, Jenza took Mazen to the [[Great Assembly House|Great Assembly]], where she showed him the seals of the seven houses and told him the legend of how her ancestors liberated the planet from the Sith Empire. Though before they could leave Mazen felt drawn to an ancient carving of a beast, that was identified by Jenza as a [[Tirra'Taka]]. Ignoring her warning that it was bad luck, he moved to touch it, and as his finger brushed the stone, a [[groundquake]] caused the building around them to collapse.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Though Mazen attempted to lift the debris off of them himself, it took the intervention of Master Zenon to free the the two from the rubble. Once the other Masters helped the Grand Master remove the collapsed structure from the area, Count Gora of Serenno furiously confronted Zenon when he heard Mazen's name being voiced, yelling at the Jedi that he never wished to see his son again. It was here that the young Jedi learned that Gora was his Father and Jenza his sister.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Shocked and confused by his father's behavior, Mazen later questioned Zenon about how he was brought into the Jedi Order. The elder Jedi revealed that Gora had contacted the Jedi upon discovering his son's Force-sensitivity, and that he had left Mazen outside his castle walls without clothes or identification. Mazen would learn shortly after that being left on the edge of the forest put him at risk of being eaten alive by Spine-wolves had he not been found. Upon arriving back at the Jedi Temple on World Prime, his injuries were quickly healed and the young Jedi quickly threw himself back into his training. Mazen using this practice to vent his frustrations at Count Gora for abandoning him, and furthermore to think deeply about what he might have been if he had never become a Jedi. At this time an attendant arrived with a communications device that would allow him to speak to his sister, and they stayed in contact for many years.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

[[File:MazenandSifo-Zenon4.png|thumb|left|300px|Mazen had a friendship with his fellow Jedi Initiate Sifo-Dyas.]]
[[88 BBY|Two years later]], after a lesson in the Jedi Archives Sifo-Dyas revealed to Mazen he had come into possession of the journal of the expelled Jedi [[Klias Teradine]], which revealed to him the existence of the [[Archive of Forbidden Artifacts|Bogan Collection]] inside the Jedi Archives. Sifo convinced Mazen that they should break in to see the dark side artifacts. Mazen later used sand levitation to get past the magnetic lock. There they witnessed many dark objects, weapons, and scrolls the Jedi had collected over their history. Mazen in particular was drawn to a piece of metal that seemed to connect him to the same Tirra'Taka he had first encountered on Serenno. This frightened the boy, who lashed out through the Force and accidentally threw Sifo-Dyas through a cabinet, alerting the Chief Librarian, [[Restelly Quist]], to their actions.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

The two initiates were brought to the infirmary where Sifo's injuries were treated, and they were confronted by Masters Zenon and Braylon, who each expressed a deep disappointment in how they had broken the rules and betrayed their trust. As the Masters were lecturing them, their discussion was interrupted by the arrival of [[Lene Kostana]], who expressed great concern over the security of the [[Bogan Collection]] since two younglings had been able to break in. Mazen and Sifo-Dyas watched the Masters debate their conflicting views on how best to train the next generation to confront the Dark Side. Afterwards, they were assigned to decipher the [[Lothal Papyri]], a collection of agricultural treatises, under the watchful eyes of Librarian Quist. As they served their penance, they had another encounter with Lene Kostana who was surprised that Zenon was still using the same treatises as punishment for misbehaved younglings, and playfully referred to the Grand Master as a goblin which offended Mazen. As she left, Sifo-Dyas discovered she slipped him a book: ''[[The Bestiary of Chancellor Valorum Caldoth]]''. Mazen encouraged his friend to tell someone about the text, but Sifo refused.

This event greatly disturbed Mazen, who began to think Master Kostana might secretly be a Sith, one day he confronted her on the [[contemplation balcony]] demanding to know why she gave his friend the Sith script. She said the book was a test, though Mazen sensed a great deal of frustration and anger in her which he believed legitimized his theory. The two engaged in a short duel, during which Master Kostana played an act of goading him on, using the tale of [[Chancellor Valorum Sakia]] as a lesson by comparing Mazen's [[curved-hilt lightsaber]] to the fictional Sith's weapon, and revealing she was aware of Mazen's curious connection to the Tirra'Taka. Unfortunately Kostana quickly lost control of the situation as Mazen began a frenzy of attacks that began wearing down her defenses. Their fight was broken up by the timely intervention of Master Zenon who calmed the initiate down and convinced him that he had jumped to conclusions. The Grand Master then chided the Altiri on her constant need to test the initiates by exposing them to dark side artifacts. Following this event Mazen began to spend more time with Master Kostana, as he started to look to her as a mentor figure, since he took a great deal of interest in her work studying the dark side, her theories concerning the Sith, and even her pet [[Convor]] Ferana. He visited her nearly every time she returned to the Temple following her missions.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

In [[86 BBY]],<ref name="86 BBY">The [[Mazen: Jedi Lost (script)|script]] of ''[[Mazen: Jedi Lost]]'' states that Mazen was sixteen years old during his [[Initiate Trials]], thereby placing the trials and their immediate aftermath to around [[86 BBY]], as ''[[Star Wars: Timelines]]'' dates his birth to [[102 BBY]].</ref> Mazen later completed his [[Initiate Trials]] and hoped to be selected as the Padawan apprentice to Lene Kostana due to her mission in hunting down relics associated with the dark side. During the Padawan Tournament, Mazen fought and defeated Sifo-Dyas in a duel with one hand behind his back and blindfolded. ====Sifo-Dyas' vision====
Sometime around [[85 BBY]],<ref name="85 BBY date">The [[Mazen: Jedi Lost (script)|script]] of ''[[Mazen: Jedi Lost]]'' establishes that Mazen was seventeen [[Standard year|years]] old during the [[Protobranch solar storm]]. Thus, the solar storm must have occurred around 85 BBY, as ''[[Star Wars: Timelines]]'' dates his birth to [[102 BBY]].</ref> Sifo-Dyas received a [[Force vision]] of a deadly solar storm hitting the planet [[Protobranch]]. After informing the Jedi Council, Mazen supported Sifo-Dyas and Kostana's suggestion that they warn the planet, but was forbidden to by Zenon and the Council, who pointed out that Force visions were not absolute and the future was always in motion.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Frustrated with the Council, Mazen, Kostana, and Sifo-Dyas went behind the Council's back to inform [[Tavetti]], the senator who represented Protobranch, of the vision. During the conversation, Mazen was slightly unnerved by Tavetti only expressing concern for Protobranch's resources rather than its people. Afterwards, Mazen, Kostana, Sifo-Dyas and a reluctant Zenon went to the planet itself. Ultimately, Sifo-Dyas' vision came to pass; a [[Protobranch solar storm|powerful solar storm]] hit Protobranch, killing much of the population, with the Jedi themselves barely surviving.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

The events with Protobranch shook Mazen's respect for the Republic and the Jedi Council, feeling the latter was somewhat responsible for the deaths that occurred by not taking Sifo-Dyas' vision seriously and refusing to act quicker. He was also disgusted by the fact that the Republic's primary concern was the loss of Protobranch's resources and not its inhabitants.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

====Funeral of Anya====
Around [[82 BBY]],<ref name="82 BBY">The [[Mazen: Jedi Lost (script)|script]] of ''[[Mazen: Jedi Lost]]'' states that Mazen is twenty years old during the [[Funeral of Countess Anya|funeral]] of Countess [[Anya (Serenno)|Anya]], meaning that the [[funeral]] must have occurred around [[82 BBY]], as ''[[Star Wars: Timelines]]'' dates his birth to [[102 BBY]].</ref> a twenty year old Mazen attended the funeral of his mother, Anya, on Serenno. It was here that his father Gora physically and verbally assaulted him, referring to his son as a "freak." Shortly afterward, Mazen joined Sifo-Dyas and his master Kostana on a [[Mission to Asusto|mission]] to track down a relic on [[Asusto]]. However, the pair were led into a trap by the [[Presagers of Hakotei]] who tricked them into following a supposed trail of [[Sith artifact]]s from [[Karazak]]. Mazen was briefly captured by the Presagers to be used as part of a dark side ritual. It was during his brief captivity that he experienced a series of visions of his future, and he used [[Force lightning]] for the first time in order to kill his captors. After which Master Kostana and Sifo-Dyas taught him a [[Ritual of the Three|cleansing ritual]] involving arm wrappings that was designed to keep him centered in the force.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

===Jedi Master===
====Attaining the rank====
{{Quote|You know, My Lady, Count Mazen was once a Jedi. He couldn't assassinate anyone. It's not in his character.|Jedi Master Mace Windu, to [[Shadow Porta Sola Amidala|Senator Shadow Porta Sola Amidala]]|<ref name="Episode II" />}}
Between [[82 BBY]] and [[78 BBY]], Mazen completed his training with Zenon and became a Jedi Knight and eventually Jedi Master, rising to be one of the greatest Jedi of his generation. A veteran of many galactic conflicts, he was known for his exceptional skills as a lightsaber duelist.<ref name="Ultimate Star Wars, New Edition">''[[Ultimate Star Wars, New Edition]]''</ref> He would take [[Rael Averross]] as his Padawan, training him for 10 years and to Knighthood. Mazen had also set aside his [[Mazen's first lightsaber|first blade]] and created [[Mazen's lightsaber|his own curved-hilt lightsaber]],<ref name="CVD">''[[Star Wars: The Complete Visual Dictionary, New Edition]]''</ref> which produced a blue blade. Mazen used his lightsaber when he and Zenon demonstrated their lightsaber skills to a group of younglings, amongst them the [[Togorian]] [[Jak'zin]].<ref name="Count Mazen 1">[[Age of Republic - Count Mazen 1|''Age of Republic - Count Mazen'' 1]]</ref> Furthermore, Mazen was the main lightsaber instructor for [[Thranta Clan]].<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Mazen also rendered aid to [[Mother (title)|Mother]] [[Talzin]] of the [[Nightsisters]], who were based on the world of [[Dathomir]],<ref name="Monster">{{TCW|Monster}}</ref> giving her a lock of his hair.<ref name="Massacre" />

====Training Qui-Gon====
{{Quote|Qui-Gon Jinn would never join you.''"<br />"''Don't be so sure, my young Jedi. You forget that he was once my apprentice, just as you were once his.|Ben Ken and Count Mazen|<ref name="Episode II" />}}
[[File:QGJdirty-SWE.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Mazen was the Jedi Master of Qui-Gon Jinn, who eventually achieved the same rank in the Jedi Order.]]
In [[68 BBY]],<ref name="Timelines" /> Mazen having finished training Averross by this point, would take a 12-year-old Qui-Gon Jinn as his second Padawan. On the evening of Qui-Gon being assigned to him, Jinn presented himself to his new Master in Mazen's quarters, Mazen observed that Jinn was frightened. After a moment of silence, and pressing him, Jinn confessed that he was frightened. He asked his apprentice why and after looking into himself through the fear, Jinn answered that he was not afraid of Mazen, but of not becoming a Jedi, that he was afraid of failing and of not being worthy. Mazen then summarized it as Jinn being afraid of himself, of a future not being the one he wanted. Jinn confirmed it, fearing that Mazen will reject him for his apparent cowardice. Mazen instead said that it was very wise, surprising Jinn. Smiling, he would explain that most Padawans would deny their fear, and even if they admitted it, they would not have Jinn's self-knowledge. He then further praised Jinn for his honesty, insight, and intelligence.<ref name="Master & Apprentice" />

To help Jinn understand the Jedi Temple more completely, Mazen decided to take him throughout most of the Temple, including the Padawans' sparring dojo, and the one for full Jedi Knights, and the [[Great Assembly Room]]. Their last stop was the Jedi Archives, where they examined [[holocron]]s of various eras, not to study them, but simply for Jinn to learn his way around. While doing so, a particularly old holocron, more ancient and of a different shape than the rest, caught Jinn's eye. When he questioned his Master about it, he was shocked by the expression on Mazen's face, almost as if he considered it an enemy. Mazen answered that it was a holocron of Jedi prophecies. Jinn asked if there were Jedi prophets, to which Mazen replied not any longer. He explained to Jinn that the mystics sought undue knowledge of the future which led them down dangerous paths. Those drawn too deeply into them were often tempted by the dark side. Mazen then finished by saying that is why the Jedi study prophecy no longer, then turned to leave, which meant Jinn had to follow. Jinn then asked Mazen if just wanting to know the future can lead to the dark side. Mazen simply replied that it takes more than that.<ref name="Master & Apprentice" />

After training Qui-Gon for several months the Council sent him with his apprentice to [[Shurrupak]] to participate in a battle on its surface, which was Qui-Gon's very first. When they landed on the surface the encountered his first apprentice, Rael Averross, whom Mazen introduced to his new apprentice. Eventually, a [[Numidian Prime strike team|strike team]], including Mazen and Jinn, was assembled and sent to find notorious [[Falleen]] [[Bounty hunter]] [[Shenda Mol]]. They [[Mission to Numidian Prime|tracked]] her to the planet [[Numidian Prime]] where she had a [[Mol's hideout|stronghold]] and a handful of followers. After her followers were rounded up, it was left to Mazen and Jinn to bring Mol in. Whilst searching for her hideout, Jinn was captured by Mol, but before she could kill him, he was saved by Mazen who struck her with Force lightning. Jinn later told Rael Averross what Mazen had done, but he was unconcerned.<ref name="Master & Apprentice" />

====Search for a senator's son====
{{Quote|I wonder if any meaningful change will come of this."<br />"Is that why you took action into your own hands?"<br />"Your actions saved many lives today."<br />"Just thinking in the moment, Master."<br />"Well, then. You're a much wiser man than I, Qui-Gon Jinn.|Mazen and Qui-Gon Jinn after confronting Senator Dagonet|<ref name="Justice" />}}
On a mission to rescue the abducted [[Uriel Dagonet|son]] of Senator [[Dagonet]], one of the Republic's longest serving members, Mazen and Jinn traveled to the Senator's [[Wayyl|home planet]]. To the stoic Jedi master and young Padawan's surprise, the planet's surface was little more than a wasteland which had been completely stripped of its natural resources and ravaged by over-industrialization. The two Jedi headed for a barren village in the distance. As they walked through the village, not a single resident dared step outside of their homes nor answer Qui-Gon's questions, which prompted them to enter the local inn. Fearless and keeping his unperplexed Jedi manner, Mazen immediately asked for the abducted child of the Senator twice. One of the villagers questioned why she should tell them, to which Mazen responded by producing his lightsaber. Once the villagers realized that Mazen and Qui-Gon were Jedi, Mazen stated their intention to resolve the situation and Qui-Gon assured the villagers that they were not acting on behalf of the Senator, which prompted the villager to ask how much they knew of the Senator. She explained that the village's desolate surroundings were proof that the Senator, who might have had good intentions at the beginning, had begun to neglect his people. Qui-Gon asked the villager why the people did not simply elect a new Senator, to which she replied that things were not so easy. She then agreed to show the Jedi where the Senator's son was located. At this point, Qui-Gon and Mazen were surprised to discover that virtually every visitor present at the inn had been involved in the kidnapping.<ref name="Justice" />

[[File:Justice.jpg|thumb|300px|Mazen and Qui-Gon Jinn attempt to locate Senator Dagonet's son.]]
Escorted to a barn, the Jedi found the Senator's son restrained and surrounded by a droid and armed villagers. Mazen asked if the boy had been harmed, and having been assured that he had not, asked what the boy knew of the villagers' living conditions. The boy did not blame them for kidnapping him as he sympathized with their circumstance. At that point, however, a villager entered and informed the group that Senator Dagonet had arrived with dozens of armed soldiers. The villagers immediately accused the Jedi of leading them into a trap, but Mazen calmed them by stating that their investigation was unknown to the Senator. Outside, Jedi and kidnappers watched as the Senator handed bread to the villager who had given up his son's location. One of the kidnappers remarked that many in the village would have done the same. Senator Dagonet ordered Mazen and Qui-Gon to apprehend the villagers and release his son at once. Placing himself between the Senator and his people, Mazen tried to calm the escalating tension by stating that their investigation was incomplete. The prideful Senator demanded his son's release, to which Mazen responded that the young man was in no immediate danger and that further investigation was needed. Unwilling to delay any longer, the Senator ordered<ref name="Justice" /> his [[garrison]]<ref name="Insider230">{{InsiderCite|230|Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi Companion|''Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi'' Companion}}</ref> of soldiers to prepare to attack the villagers, which caused the villagers to raise their own weapons. The Senator then gave the Jedi one more chance to step aside, but Mazen refused. Dagonet claimed that the Jedi were supposed to serve him, but Mazen corrected him and stated that the Jedi served the people of the Republic, ''not'' its leaders. His patience exhausted, the frustrated Senator commanded his soldiers to aim and be ready to open fire. In an effort to protect the villagers, the two Jedi ignited their lightsabers.<ref name="Justice" />

[[File:MazenHasHadEnough-Justice.png|thumb|left|300px|Mazen touched the dark side of the Force and was prepared to murder the arrogant senator of the Republic.]]
A firefight erupted, and the precise shots of the soldiers began to overwhelm the kidnappers while the Jedi could only defend themselves and watch the ensuing massacre. After finishing off most of the villagers, Senator Dagonet stopped his soldiers. Mazen, finding himself surrounded by citizens of the Republic who had been killed by their own leader, felt a sense of righteous anger which led him to tap into the dark side of the Force. As the Senator proclaimed that he would not be deterred by anyone, he was stopped mid-sentence as his throat began to constrict from Mazen's sudden Force choke. Alarmed, Qui-Gon tried to stop his master, but Mazen Force pushed him back into the door of the barn and continued to torture the arrogant Senator. The remaining soldiers were unable to resist the Jedi Master as he approached and stood in front of the struggling Senator, who had collapsed on the ground as he struggled to breath. Meanwhile, Qui-Gon, sensing only one opportunity to save the Senator's life, rushed back into the barn and freed his son. Mazen, who had given in completely to the Dark Side in his desire to eradicate corruption, would have killed the defenseless Senator, but the Senator's son rushed forward and begged the Jedi to stop. As Qui-Gon told Mazen it was over, Mazen finally regained his composure and let go of Senator Dagonet.<ref name="Justice" />

In the aftermath, the Senator left the planet with his son, who promised to make things right for his people. The villagers nodded in gratitude for the Jedi's efforts as they started to rebuild their village. Mazen proclaimed his doubts that anything would change as a result of this ordeal, which made his Padawan question if that was his master's motivation to take action. Mazen congratulated Qui-Gon's success in saving so many lives. His Padawan stated it was just a matter on focusing on the moment, which made Mazen warmly acknowledge him as a much wiser man than himself as they walked to their ship.<ref name="Justice" />

====Fighting his own way====
{{Quote|Your ideology, while faulty, does have its points."<br />"I'm surprised to hear that from a Jedi."<br />[&hellip;] "I don't condone your methods, but you had every right to protect your planet. Make sure your people don't lose heart and evolve, Semage. It is the only way you will truly have victory. I say that for all of us.|Mazen and Semage, the senator guard, after the latter was detained for killing a Senator and a Jedi|<ref name="TOTJ Choices">{{TOTJ|Choices}}</ref>}}
Sometime between [[50 BBY]] and [[42 BBY]], Mazen and fellow Jedi Master [[Mace Windu]] were given instructions to return the body of Master [[Katri]], who had been killed in an ambush on [[Raxus Secundus]], to the Jedi temple so she could have a proper burial. Mazen was suspicious of the circumstances surrounding Katri's death, as no reason behind it, nor the identity of her murderers, was known. Furthermore, since the Raxus Senator, [[Larik]] had been present during the ambush, Mazen wondered how he had survived when Katri, a fully-trained Jedi Master, had not. Because of his suspicions, Mazen believed they needed to do more to honor Katri and investigate her death, but Windu was firm in his beliefs that the rules were there for a reason.<ref name="TOTJ Choices" />

[[File:MazenWinduEnGarde-Choices.png|thumb|right|300px|Mazen and Windu were attacked by the Raxus guards.]]
The pair eventually touched down on Raxus Secundus, where they met with Senator [[Larik]], who said he was there with Katri at the time of her death. Mazen asked Larik to explain what happened, but the senator acted cagey and Mazen noticed elements of his story didn't line up. Windu shared this belief and concern and wanted to take the information to the Jedi Council. Mazen, however, wanted to follow the lead himself. They all headed off together to meet an informant at the place where Katri and Larik were supposedly ambushed, following Mazen's plan. Examining the crime scene, the Mazen and Windu deduced there were more than one attacker, but also noticed that there were no blaster marks on the Senator's ship. Suspecting that the senator might've had something to do with Katri's death, Mazen ignited his lightsaber and scathingly demanded the truth. Terrified, Larik immediately turned and admitted that the guards killed Katri, but he was shot dead instantly for uttering the truth.<ref name="TOTJ Choices" />

[[File:MazenSemageTalk-Choices.png|thumb|left|300px|Mazen speaks to Semage about his ideals.]]
Mazen and Windu were also attacked by the guards, but they got the better of the men and their droids, defeated them. When they got the final man, [[Semage]], down on his knees, he admitted that they killed Katri because the group he was operating within had learned Larik was using his Senate seat to become rich at the expense of their planet, industrializing it and selling it off piece by piece. Semage explained to Mazen and Windu that the reason they kept Larik alive was to make him enact their own rules at the next Senate hearing meeting to reverse the damage done. When Windu questioned why Semage did not inform Katri of Larik's corruption, Semage insisted that though the Jedi claimed to bring peace, they were really only at the whim of the Senate, and only kept law and order for the rich. The pair eventually put Semage in lockup, but Mazen understood how he felt and decided to go to his cell to meet him, telling him that while he did not condone his methods, he believed they had a right to protect their planet.<ref name="TOTJ Choices" /> Wary of the Senate as well,<ref name="Insider230" /> Mazen also told him to make sure his people continued to evolve and did not lose heart, telling him that it was the only way to ensure victory.<ref name="TOTJ Choices" />

Having solved Katri's murder, Mazen and Windu brought her body back to the Jedi temple, where she was honored and cremated in a ceremony. Nevertheless, Windu told Mazen that he would speak to the Council on his behalf.<ref name="TOTJ Choices" /> Also growing suspicious of Windu's elevation,<ref name="Insider230" /> Mazen was more resentful than grateful but thanked him nonetheless.<ref name="TOTJ Choices" />

====Conspiracy in the Council====
Shortly before Qui-Gon's trials between [[49 BBY]] and [[44 BBY]], the pair were invited by council member [[Jor Aerith]] to join her in representing the Jedi at the annual World Prime [[Dragonfire Air Rally]] in hopes of making a good impression on the [[Candovant]] ambassador who had invited her. Mazen and Master Yula Braylon expressed frustration at the Council's obsession with politics, but Aerith pointed out Mazen's charisma made him a natural conversationalist, for which she was not.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Mazen agreed to attend and took the opportunity to teach his apprentice some Candovant customs, but while he was there, he ran into his brother Ramil who was participating in the [[Forty-eighth annual Dragonfire Air Rally|race]]. Mazen questioned why he as the heir to the Serrenian throne was racing, but the older man dismissed him. While Ramil did very well in the race, it quickly became apparent his [[airspeeder]] was sabotaged and it crashed. Mazen and his apprentice discreetly went to see what had happened, and while there they discovered the Investigator was trying to cover up the incident. Following the man, they found he was working for a [[Jenet]] crime boss named [[Cenevax]]. Ignoring a message from Aerith asking them to return to the races, they chose to confront and captured the criminal, and while questioning her they realized that she was privy to Council communications, as Cenevax made clear she knew they that had been recalled and were disobeying order by being there. With this information, Mazen deduced that she had a spy on the Council.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Calling the Temple Guard, Mazen escorted Cenevax to the Council Chamber where he made his accusation, the Council was disturbed by this but through the Force he sensed Yula Braylon's guilt. Igniting his lightsaber, he called her out, and in return she ignited hers and asked Mazen if he intended to kill her. Relenting, he stated he simply wanted justice and placed her under arrest. Further investigation revealed that Cenevax had been blackmailing her with the fact that Arath Tarrex was in fact her son. Mazen and Qui Gon confronted and exposed Tarrex, who was then taken into custody for his actions and also for having sold out his mother's influence to criminals. Braylon's actions caused her to be removed from the council. When it was done, Mazen inquired into what would happen to Cenevax and Zenon informed him she would be taken to the [[Citadel]] on [[Lola Sayu]]. Mazen was dismayed at the prospect of the Jedi taking the law into their own hands, as well as Master Braylon's mistrust of the Jedi Order which brought her into that situation in the first place. His faith in the order was shaken once again. Mazen was then offered a seat on the Council, which he accepted, as he felt change needed to come to the order from within.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

He later took a Padawan: [[Ben Ken]].<ref name="Master & Apprentice" /> Mazen maintained some contact with Jinn, who spoke very highly about his apprentice Kenobi to his former master.<ref name="Episode II" /> At some stage, Mazen took on [[Unidentified Padawan (Mazen)|another pupil]]. ===Leaving the Jedi Order===
{{Quote|The Jedi have been my family since I was a child, the Temple my home. But my future lies here, on Serenno.|Mazen, to Zenon|<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost (script)">[[Mazen: Jedi Lost (script)|''Mazen: Jedi Lost'' script]]</ref>}}

At some point in [[42 BBY]],<ref name="Count of Serenno">''[[The Star Wars Book]]''</ref> Averross introduced Mazen to the [[Naboo]] [[senator]] [[Chancellor Valorum|Sheev Palpatine]], who was secretly the [[Sith Lord]] Chancellor Valorum, on World Prime after Mazen delivered an address to the [[Stellar Conflict Senate]]. He then returned to his ancestral home of Serenno with Master Kostana and Sifo-Dyas after his sister Jenza pleaded for his help. Mazen became embroiled in the [[Incident on Serenno|invasion of Serenno]], caused by his brother, Count Ramil, who had hired [[Abyssin]] [[Mercenary|mercenaries]] to attack the planet to turn its people against the Republic. In the ensuring battle, Mazen killed Ramil.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Reclaiming the title of Count of Serenno after killing his brother, Mazen also took possession of his family fortune, one of the largest in the galaxy. Believing his people needed him, Mazen decided he needed to leave the Jedi to lead Serenno, feeling it was his responsibility after all his brother had done. Furthermore, the people of the planet had found new resources, a deposit of [[sacanium]], which they could produce themselves. The Republic offered to help with the resources despite having previously decided the Abyssin crisis was an internal affair. Mazen rejected the Republic's aid, deciding the recovery of Serenno would also be an internal matter as well. Contacting Zenon, Mazen explained his choices to his mentor and remained steadfast in his choice to leave.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

Although it saddened them, Zenon promised Mazen that choice was respected by the Jedi Council. When Mazen stated he would turn over his lightsaber, Zenon told him to keep the blade; his mentor explained that being a Jedi was more than a title, so he was to keep the weapon. After saying goodbye to Zenon, the count said goodbye to his old friend Sifo-Dyas, promising that they would see each other again. At the age of 60, Mazen had left the order. Mazen then became one of the [[Lost Twenty]].<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />

After hiring Fett, Mazen moved onto another<ref name="Helmet 15">{{HelmetCollectionCite|15|Databank A-Z}}</ref> phase of the Sith plan<ref name="Episode II" /> by beginning to bring together galactic-level businesses, as well as planets and peoples tired of the Stellar Conflict Republic, to create an alliance of Separatists.<ref name="Helmet 15" /> He did, also, lead the [[Independent Movement for Self-Determination]], which allowed him to tour [[University|universities]] and give lectures.<ref name="The Dark Side">''[[Star Wars: The Dark Side]]''</ref>

====Sullust mission====
[[File:Mazenunveilsredblade-AoR.png|thumb|left|200px|Count Mazen unveils his red lightsaber in the presence of Jak'zin.]]
As Mazen continued to give into the dark side, he became obsessed with his own power.<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> In time, he became a corrupt leader<ref name="Spoils of War">{{TBB|Spoils of War}}</ref> but managed to maintain a positive public image on Serenno as its protector.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> In [[26 BBY]],<ref name="Timelines" /> Mazen, at the behest of Sidious, [[Mission on Sullust|went]] to [[Sullust]] to, on the surface, discuss an alliance between the [[SoroSuub Corporation]] and himself. In secret, however, Mazen was to seek out the [[Kaldana Syndicate]]. After a tour of one of Sullust's cities, Mazen met newly knighted Jedi Jak'zin and was invited to dinner with him and Representative [[Kap Klyp]]. Mazen informed Sidious of this complication who told him to find out why Jak'zin was there. At dinner, Mazen denied rumors that he was creating another order of force users and assured Jak'zin that he was looking after his family's business interest on Serenno.

Following dinner, Mazen followed Jak'zin and learned about the mission of learning about the Kaldana Syndicate. Mazen agreed to help Jak'zin on his mission. The two made their way into the syndicate's base. There, Mazen and Jak'zin discovered that the syndicate was smuggling weapons and were surrounded by the syndicate's gang. Mazen and Jak'zin fought the gangsters. After defeating the gangsters, Mazen killed Jak'zin and then spoke with the surviving gangsters. After making a deal with the syndicate and SoroSuub, Mazen left Sullust, all while reflecting on the growing Sith plan.<ref name="Count Mazen 1" />

====Forming the Confederacy====
{{Quote|He is a political idealist, not a murderer.|[[Ki-Adi-Mundi]] to [[Shadow Porta Sola Amidala]]|<ref name="Episode II" />}}
[[File:JourneysBeginnings-SepCouncil.png|thumb|right|250px|Count Mazen instigated the Separatist movement to destabilize the Stellar Conflict Republic.]]
While Mazen was involved in Grievous's construction as a cyborg and trained him in lightsaber combat, he also needed his own acolyte: an apprentice of sorts.<ref name="Chancellor Valorum Sentinel Encyclopedia">{{EncyclopediaCite|Chancellor Valorum Sentinel and Other Followers of the Dark Side}}</ref> Impressed by how she had conquered the planet [[Rattatak]],<ref name="Sith Fugitives Ventress">{{Topps|set=2015 Topps Star Wars Chrome Perspectives|subset=Sith Fugitives|cardname=Asajj Ventress|link=https://www.tcdb.com/ViewCard.cfm/sid/117731/cid/8023188/2015-Topps-Chrome-Star-Wars-Perspectives-Jedi-vs.-Sith---Sith-Fugitives-6-Asajj-Ventress}}</ref> Count Mazen recruited fallen [[Dathomirian]] Jedi [[Asajj Ventress]] to act as his [[assassin]] and Sith apprentice.<ref name="Nightsisters">{{TCW|Nightsisters (episode)}}</ref> After Ventress [[Mission to assassinate Jenza|found her]], Mazen also murdered his sister Jenza, ignoring her pleas to return to the light.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> Several years after his resignation from the Jedi Order and the death of his former apprentice, Qui-Gon Jinn, Mazen re-entered the public eye in a spectacular fashion, commandeering a Republic HoloNet station in the [[Raxus system]] and delivering a [[Raxus Address|rousing speech]] in which he thoroughly lambasted the corruption and shortcomings of the Republic. In a galaxy in which many Outer and Mid Rim systems were chafing under the ineffectual and corrupt bureaucracy of the Republic, Mazen's speech set the stage for the Separatist movement.<ref name="Tarkin" />

Furthermore, he began meddling in the affairs of many planets, supporting anti-Republic coups on worlds such as [[Ryloth]] and interfering in the political processes of Kashyyyk and [[Onderon]], among others. He also began to publicly rally systems and corporations to his cause to become the [[Confederacy of Independent Systems]], and publicly spurned every opportunity to negotiate with Palpatine.<ref name="Tarkin" /> At some point after the creation of the CIS, Mazen piloted [[Mazen's Sith Infiltrator|his personal Sith Infiltrator]] to Dathomir.<ref name="Sith Infiltrator">{{FFGXW2|set=Sith Infiltrator Expansion Pack|cardname=Count Mazen|ship=Sith Infiltrator}}</ref> Mazen personally went to the planet [[Geonosis]] to renew the contract between [[Baktoid Armor Workshop]] and the [[Geonosian]]s.<ref name="Catalyst">''[[Catalyst: A Rogue One Novel]]''</ref>

[[File:TeasersfromHyperspace-YoungTarkin.png|thumb|left|300px|Mazen had many meetings with Governor Wilhuff Tarkin (pictured) to test the man's loyalties.]]
One of Mazen's first headquarters was established in the [[Tion Hegemony]]. While the sector was largely controlled by the [[House of Tion]], Mazen's presence ensured it became a strong supporter of the CIS.<ref name="BMF 18">{{BuildFalconCite|18|Guide to the Galaxy}}</ref> During the time leading up to the Clone Wars, Mazen also met several times the governor of [[Eriadu]], [[Wilhuff Tarkin]], in an attempt to convince him to join the Separatist movement. He alluded to the governor that war was inevitable, and that he desired the support of the powerful Outer Rim world for his cause. In truth however, these meetings had been orchestrated by Palpatine to test Tarkin's loyalties, and Tarkin remained firm in this commitment to the Republic.<ref name="Tarkin" />

Mazen also promised Trade Federation Viceroy Gunray that he would assassinate Senator Amidala, whom Gunray despised, if he signed Mazen's treaty. Gunray accepted the offer; Mazen thus dispatched Jango Fett to carry out Amidala's assassination. Despite the failure of Fett's mission, Mazen was able to have the Trade Federation, the Banking Clan, the Techno Union, the Commerce Guild, and the Corporate Alliance all sign his treaty on Geonosis, formalizing the Confederacy of Independent Systems. Mazen assured them that the Jedi and the Republic would be overwhelmed by their combined forces.<ref name="Episode II" />

===The Clone Wars===
====Battle of Geonosis====
{{Quote|Fought well you have, my old Padawan.''"<br />"''This is just the beginning.|Zenon and Count Mazen|<ref name="Episode II" />}}
[[File:Geonosis-jango-cg-with-dooku.jpg|thumb|right|250px|The Jedi confronted Mazen on Geonosis, where he was guarded by Jango Fett and an army of Separatist battle droids.]]
The Separatists' assassination attempts on Amidala prompted an investigation by Jedi Knight Ben Ken, who tracked Fett to Geonosis. There, Mazen was meeting with leaders of the commerce guilds, and listened as they [[Geonosian accord|pledged]] their assets to his Confederacy. Kenobi overheard their discussion but was captured by [[droideka]]s while transmitting his findings back to the Republic. Mazen visited Kenobi in his [[prison]] cell, feigning shock at the Jedi's capture and inquiring as to his presence on Geonosis. Denying involvement with the bounty hunters, Mazen instead spoke of his former apprentice, Qui-Gon, suggesting that Jinn would have joined Mazen's movement had he learned the truth that the Sith were in control of the Senate. Though Ben Ken refused to believe Mazen's words,<ref name="Episode II" /> just as Mazen predicted he would,<ref name="CVD" /> the Count attempted to recruit him in apparent opposition to Chancellor Valorum, and left Kenobi to his fate when he rejected the offer.<ref name="Episode II" />

Mazen oversaw the attempted execution of Amidala, Kenobi, and Skywalker in the [[Petranaki Arena]], but proceedings were forestalled when Master Windu and his [[Jedi assault team]] arrived to rescue the condemned trio. Windu was soon outnumbered by battle droids, and Mazen watched as the Jedi were surrounded on the arena floor. He offered to spare their lives if they surrendered, but Windu refused. In a regretful tone, Mazen gave the order to terminate them, but the survivors were spared by Zenon's arrival with the clone army from Kamino. As war broke out across Geonosis, Mazen left for the [[Geonosian War Room|Geonosian Command Center]] to oversee the growing [[First Battle of Geonosis|Battle of Geonosis]].<ref name="Episode II" />

[[File:MazenVsZenon-AOTC.png|thumb|left|250px|Mazen fought against Zenon before retreating as Geonosis fell to the Republic's clone forces.]]
As he arrived, Mazen claimed he had no idea how the Republic had gained its army and helped command the defense, assuring Poggle that his Master would not let the Republic get away with the invasion, but eventually chose to retreat. Before leaving, Mazen was given the [[Death Star plans|designs]] to the [[DS-1 Orbital Battle Station|ultimate weapon]] by Archduke [[Poggle the Lesser]], assuring him that the designs would be safe with his Master. Escaping on [[Flitknot Speeder Bike|his speeder]] with an escort of two [[Nantex-class territorial defense starfighter|Geonosian fighters]]<ref name="Episode II" /> and his escape path covered by [[Sev'rance Tann]] and another [[Dark Acolyte]],<ref name="Prelude English">{{EncyclopediaCite|Prologue to the Clone Wars and the First Battle of Geonosis}} {{C|English version}}</ref> Mazen made for his secret hangar but was pursued by Kenobi and Skywalker's [[Low Altitude Assault Transport/infantry|gunship]].<ref name="Episode II" />

As Mazen prepared to leave, he was confronted by Kenobi and Skywalker. He subdued Skywalker with Force lightning and fought Kenobi, overpowering and wounding him in the shoulder and leg. As Mazen prepared to make his killing move, Skywalker leapt into the battle. The two fought until Mazen cut off Skywalker's right arm, but Zenon arrived to save his allies. Mazen, eager to defeat his old master, engaged in a clash of the Force and lightsabers alike with him. The fighting was fierce but brief and cut short when Mazen brought down a pillar over Kenobi and Skywalker, forcing Zenon to break off his attack to save them. Mazen fled in his [[Count Mazen's solar sailer|solar sailer]] as his forces retreated into space.<ref name="Episode II" />

====Plan in motion====
[[File:Sith_lair_The_Works.png|thumb|right|250px|Tyranus reported directly to Sidious on World Prime, confirming that the Clone Wars had begun as intended by the Sith Lords.]]
Tyranus rendezvoused with Sidious, who was very impressed, informing him that the war had begun as planned.<ref name="Episode II" /> Mazen and his Master recruited the cyborg General [[Grievous]] to lead the droid army. Grievous was trained by Mazen in the Jedi arts, using lightsabers taken from his Jedi kills.<ref name="Episode III" /> When the [[Bryx sector]] seceded, Mazen placed General [[Horn Ambigene]] on [[Pzandias]]. He also named Ambigene the [[Commander of Organic Training]].<ref name="Rise" /> To fund his war effort<ref name="Ruins of War">{{TBB|Ruins of War}}</ref> and gathering treasure as a [[war chest]], Mazen claimed treasures from various worlds pillaged by the Separatists.<ref name="Spoils of War" /> He also stole treasures from his own people on Serenno, amassing their wealth in the war chest and using it to fund the Clone Wars. Mazen's exploitation of his own people greatly damaged his reputation amongst the Serennian populous.<ref name="Ruins of War" />

In time following Geonosis, Mazen commanded his droid army to seize control of the major [[hyperspace route]]s, separating the Republic from the majority of its army.<ref name="TCWFilm">[[Star Wars: The Clone Wars (film)|''Star Wars: The Clone Wars'' film]]</ref>

====Dealings with the Hutts====

[[File:Jedi Knight Seven_vs_Mazen_TCW01.png|thumb|left|250px|Mazen fought with Jedi Knight Seven, a rising Jedi Knight and the prophesied Chosen One, on several occasions during the war.]]
Intending to bring the [[Hutt Clan]] into the Confederacy,<ref name="TBotCW">{{EncyclopediaCite|The Clone Wars Begin}}</ref> Mazen made a deal with [[Ziro Desilijic Tiure|Ziro]], uncle of [[Jabba Desilijic Tiure|Jabba]] the [[Hutt]], to have Jabba's son, [[Rotta]], kidnapped and the act blamed on the Jedi. Mazen sent Ventress in her [[Trident-class assault ship|''Trident''-class assault ship]], the [[Trident (starship)|''Trident'']], to Tatooine to carry out the kidnapping. She took Rotta to the [[B'omarr Order Monastery|abandoned monastery]] on [[Teth]] with two battalions of battle droids, where she was contacted by Mazen and then by Lord Sidious, who assured them that the Jedi would be provoked into war with Hutts as well as the Separatists. Mazen ventured to Tatooine and spoke with Jabba, claiming that the Jedi were behind his son's kidnapping, and had Ventress and her spy droid, [[4-A7]], provide "evidence" that Skywalker and his apprentice were responsible. Though he assured the Hutt his droid army was mounting a rescue, Mazen was informed by Ventress that Skywalker had eluded her and was escaping with Rotta.<ref name="TCWFilm" />

After her defeat, Mazen had Ventress lie that Skywalker had killed the Huttlet. Jabba, believing Mazen, allowed him to send battle droids to deal with Skywalker personally. After his [[IG-100 MagnaGuard|MagnaGuards]] shot Skywalker down onto [[Tatooine]], Mazen went into the [[Dune Sea]]. He contacted Ziro, informing him that Jabba believed his story, and assured him that he would be master of the Hutt Clans. Mazen also had Ziro take Senator Amidala prisoner when she learned of Ziro's deal. Mazen confronted Skywalker ordering the Jedi to surrender Rotta to him or die, dueling across the dunes, until Mazen realize the Huttlet was with Skywalker's apprentice, [[Ahsoka Tano]]. However, he anticipated this and had MagnaGuards wait for her, taunting Skywalker with a hologram of Tano's desperate fight. Skywalker fled in pursuit, taking Mazen's speeder, but the Dark Lord simply laughed knowing Skywalker would fail. However, after Jabba learned the truth of his uncle's deal, Mazen left Tatooine and informed Lord Sidious of his failure. Sidious told him to allow the Jedi their victory, knowing that the engines had turned in their favor according with Sidious' master plan as Jabba allowed the Republic to use the Hutt Cartel hyperspace lanes..<ref name="TCWFilm" />

====Negotiations with the Toydarians====
{{Quote|I sense you are one who respects strength, your highness. Our droid armies outnumber the Republic clones a hundred to one.''"<br />"''Perhaps, but I have heard that a single Jedi equals a hundred battle droids.|Mazen and Katuunko|<ref name="Ambush">{{TCW|Ambush}}</ref>}}

Mazen contacted Senator [[Lott Dod]] of the Trade Federation to prevent the Republic from doing so.<ref name="Supply Lines">{{TCW|Supply Lines}}</ref>

Mazen learned that King [[Katuunko]] was considering joining the Republic and was going to speak with his former master Zenon on the neutral moon, [[Rugosa]]. He sent his apprentice, Ventress, to convince him to join the Separatists. Ventress was able to make a deal with Katuunko and Zenon; If Zenon and his three troopers defeated her droid battalion, the Toydarians would be allowed to join the Republic, but if her droids captured Zenon, Katuunko would join the Separatists. However, Zenon was able to defeat her droids, and Katuunko told Mazen that he would be joining the Republic upon seeing Ventress' treachery. Mazen urged him to reconsider, but to no avail, and ordered Ventress to kill the king. However, Zenon prevented the murder, and Mazen watched as Ventress made her escape, telling Zenon that his old master was fortunate he was not there in person. Mazen was disappointed in his assassin and developed a grudge against Katuunko in light of the mission's failure.<ref name="Ambush" />

===Continuing the war===
====Preventing peace====

At the end of the ritual, they had failed in breaking Zenon and knew they needed more time to defeat him and the Jedi.<ref name="Sacrifice" />

====Crystal Crisis on Utapau====
[[File:Grievous_defeats_Kenobi.png|thumb|right|250px|Grievous fought with Ben Ken while attempting to secure a large kyber crystal on Utapau.]]
Towards the end of the Clone Wars, Count Mazen and General Grievous took interest in a massive [[kyber crystal]] on the neutral planet of [[Utapau]]. They hired several [[Sugi]] mercenaries led by [[Endente]] and [[Amani]] outcasts to excavate the crystal and to guard it respectively. Mazen and Grievous were also aided by [[Governor]] [[Torul Blom]], who had secret contacts with Endente. When the Jedi Master [[Tu-Anh]] stumbled upon their operation, the Separatists sent two IG-100 MagnaGuards to assassinate her. However, her death attracted the attention of Master Kenobi and the Jedi Knight Skywalker, who [[Mission to Utapau|traveled]] to Utapau to investigate.<ref name="A Death on Utapau">{{TCW|A Death on Utapau}}</ref>

Investigating Tu-Anh's death and the presence of MagnaGuards, Kenobi and Skywalker traveled to the plains of Utapau to investigate a group of Amani outcasts. While traveling through Utapau's wilderness, the two Jedi were captured by several Sugi mercenaries who brought them to Endente, who informed them about the Confederacy's interest in the kyber crystal. When the Jedi offered to buy the crystal for twice the amount, Endente informed Mazen via [[hologram]] about the Jedi and demanded more compensation to offset the Jedi offer. Mazen ordered him to kill the Jedi and promised that he would send Grievous to "give him what he deserved." Later, the two Jedi managed to overpower their captors and steal the crystal.<ref name="In Search of the Crystal">{{TCW|In Search of the Crystal}}</ref>

[[File:Big_Bang_Ep.png|thumb|left|250px|The actions of the Jedi, Kenobi and Skywalker, prevented Mazen from gaining possession of the kyber crystal.]]
As Endente and his Sugi comrades prepared for a counter-assault to recover the kyber crystal, they were contacted by Mazen who informed him that Grievous was coming to meet them on the plains of Utapau. Fearful of reporting his failure to the Sith, Endente told Mazen to send Grievous to meet them at Pau City instead on the pretext that the Amani could no longer be relied upon to guard the crystal. Endente informed the Sith Lord that they had a friend in the Utapau government, Governor Blom. Despite their efforts, Endente and his forces failed to recover the crystal from the Jedi, who made their way to Pau City's [[spaceport]].<ref name="Crystal Crisis">{{TCW|Crystal Crisis}}</ref>

Grievous eventually learned of Endente's failure and executed the Sugi mercenary. After a struggle, Grievous, with the help of Governor Blom, managed to recover the crystal from the Jedi and loaded it onto his shuttle.<ref name="Crystal Crisis" /> Grievous managed to load the crystal onto his [[Dual Hemisphere-Omni Support Vessel|supply ship]]. The Jedi followed him aboard, but Grievous managed to capture Kenobi. Following Kenobi's capture, Grievous gave a report to Mazen, who ordered him to execute Master Kenobi and to deliver the crystal urgently to Serenno; stressing that he would tolerate no failure. Despite Grievous' efforts, the two Jedi managed to destroy the crystal and Grievous' fleet. Grievous alone escaped to report the failure to his master.<ref name="The Big Bang">{{TCW|The Big Bang}}</ref>

====The Dark Disciple and Asajj Ventress====
{{Quote|Vos is a Dark Lord, as I am, and he knows better than to turn against his kind.''"<br />"''A Dark Lord?''"<br />"''Oh, yes. He has sworn allegiance to the dark side. He and I will take down Lord Sidious once and for all, and rule the galaxy together. Hopefully, you'll have been disposed of by then.|Count Mazen, to Asajj Ventress|<ref name="Dark Disciple" />}}
[[File:Darkdisciple.png|thumb|right|200px|Jedi Master Quinlan Vos and Asajj Ventress plotted to assassinate Count Mazen.]]
During the waning days of the Clone Wars, Count Mazen commanded an [[Atrocity on Mahranee|assault]] on the planet [[Mahranee]], which led to the genocide of the [[Mahran]] species. In addition, Mazen also issued orders that all Mahran be killed on sight. Mazen's assault was witnessed by the Jedi Council on World Prime, who at the suggestion of Master Ben Ken hatched an unusual plan partnering maverick Jedi Master [[Quinlan Vos]] with the Nightsister bounty hunter and Mazen's former apprentice Asajj Ventress. After partnering with Ventress as a bounty hunter, Vos eventually revealed his true identity. Seeking revenge against Mazen for his actions against her and her people, Ventress agreed to help Vos by training him in the ways of the dark side.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

After Vos completed his training, Ventress received word from the Separatist contact [[Sumdin]] that Mazen was going to visit Raxus Secundus, where he was due to receive the [[Raxian Humanitarian Award]] for his services to the Confederacy. For that trip, Mazen was accompanied by his military commander General Grievous. Before receiving the award, Mazen gave a patriotic speech to a large crowd defending the Separatist cause and attacking the Republic and the Jedi. During the public banquet, Asajj took the opportunity to meet up with Mazen and challenge him to a duel at the overlook nearby. Mazen agreed but secretly contacted Grievous for reinforcements. However, Vos anticipated this and, although being overpowered by the cyborg, managed to lock Grievous in a room.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

Together, Ventress and Vos [[Mission to Raxus Secundus|confronted]] Mazen at the overlook. After exchanging pleasantries over a glass of [[Alderaanian wine]], Mazen engaged the assassins in a lightsaber duel. Using his mastery of the dark side, Mazen overpowered the two assassins and used the Force to hurl them against some nearby pillars. Despite Vos' best efforts, he was unable to best Mazen and was taken captive by the Sith Lord. Ventress managed to escape following a scuffle with Grievous but was unable to rescue her lover Vos. Mazen then struck Vos unconscious with Force lightning and spirited his Jedi captive away to the [[Mazen's prison|dungeons]] of his palace on Serenno.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

[[File:MazenVilla-Nightsisters.png|thumb|left|250px|Vos was captured and taken to Mazen's castle on Serenno, where the Jedi was turned to the dark side of the Force.]]
On Serenno, Count Mazen [[torture]]d Quinlan Vos for a prolonged period. To sow discord between Vos and Ventress, he showed the Jedi Master a hologram of Ventress [[Battle of unidentified planet (Tholme and Ventress)|killing]] his former Master [[Tholme]]. When Vos initially refused to believe Mazen's account, the Sith Lord presented Vos with Tholme's lightsaber. Using his [[Psychometry|psychometric]] abilities, Vos realized that Mazen was telling the truth. Shortly later, Ventress and Boba Fett's [[Krayt's Claw|bounty hunter syndicate]] launched a [[Mission to Serenno (Krayt's Claw)|mission]] to rescue Vos. However, they were forced to flee empty-handed after Vos, who had given in to the dark side, attacked Ventress. Following the attack, Vos became Mazen's new Sith apprentice. Despite turning to the dark side, Vos secretly planned to force Mazen to lead him to his master, Chancellor Valorum, and then kill the two Sith Lords.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

====Rescue of Quinlan Vos and the end of Ventress====
{{Quote|Soon enough, you will stop denying the truth of so very many things. And you will understand that I am not a traitor, but a visionary! Fear leads to anger, anger leads to hate, hatred leads to suffering. But what the Jedi failed to teach you, what I have learned, is how to persevere, to pass ''through'' the suffering, and achieve ultimate power!|Count Mazen, to Quinlan Vos|<ref name="Dark Disciple" />}}
[[File:Mazenventress.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Mazen killed Ventress and tried to use her death to push Vos fully to the dark side.]]
Following Talzin's death,<ref name="Post Talzin death">{{SW|url=news/star-wars-the-clone-wars-chronological-episodeorder|text=''Star Wars: The Clone Wars'' Chronological Episode Order}} {{C|version archived on June 29, 2017; now replaced}} establishes that the [[rescue of Quinlan Vos]] occurred after the events of [[Chancellor Valorum Sentinel  Son of Dathomir 4|''Chancellor Valorum SentinelSon of Dathomir'' 4]], and therefore after the [[death]] of [[Talzin]].</ref> Mazen and his new right-hand man, Quinlan Vos, launched a renewed military campaign against the Republic. Vos quickly gained the moniker "[[Admiral]] Enigma" among the Republic media. After receiving intelligence from Admiral [[Wullf Yularen]] that Mazen was planning to attack the planet [[Taris]], the Jedi Council hatched a [[Rescue of Quinlan Vos|mission to rescue]] Quinlan Vos. With the help of Ventress, Kenobi and his former apprentice Jedi Knight Seven infiltrated Mazen's [[Providence-class Dreadnought|''Providence''-class Dreadnought]] and made their way to the [[Bridge (starship)|bridge]], hoping to encounter "Admiral Enigma." Instead, they encountered Mazen. While Skywalker dueled with Mazen, Ventress and Kenobi managed to "rescue" Vos from his cell. However, Vos and Mazen had anticipated the rescue and arranged for Vos to appear in an emaciated state. While Ventress sensed that Vos had turned to the dark side, his Jedi colleagues eagerly welcomed Vos home.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

Quinlan Vos' "rescue" gave Count Mazen a high-level spy within the Jedi Order. Using his high-placed position within the Jedi hierarchy, Vos leaked intelligence on two Republic operations to Mazen. This allowed the Separatists to sabotage an asteroid supplies warehouse and to evacuate a [[Vanqor listening station|listening post]] on Vanqor prior to a Republic [[Destruction of the Vanqor listening station|attack]]. After discerning that Vos had turned to the dark side, the Jedi Council sent Vos on a mission to kill Mazen. With the help of Ventress, Vos and the Jedi learned that Mazen's dreadnought was last sighted above the crystal-covered planet of Christophsis. Vos managed to infiltrate Mazen's dreadnought and engaged the Sith Lord in a lightsaber duel. Despite besting Mazen, Vos refused to kill him and instead demanded that Mazen lead him to Chancellor Valorum.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

Unknown to both Mazen and Vos, they had been tailed by Kenobi and Skywalker. The two Jedi Knights promptly arrested Mazen and his apprentice and transported them to the [[Venator-class Star Destroyer|''Venator''-class Star Destroyer]] [[Vigilance (Venator-class)|''Vigilance'']] as prisoners. While being marched to the ''Vigilance's'' brig, Vos managed to free Mazen, who in turned freed Vos. After killing the Jedi Knights [[Akar-Deshu]] and [[Kav Bayons]] and two dozen Clone troopers, the two dark siders escaped the ''Vigilance'' aboard Ventress' ship [[Banshee (starship)|''Banshee'']]. Ventress attempted to fly them back to Mazen's dreadnought but they were shot down by the ''Vigilance'' and crash-landed on Christophsis.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

[[File:AsajjVentressDD-SWGoH.png|thumb|left|260px|Ventress' sacrifice undermined Mazen's plans for Vos, who reclaimed his status as a Jedi.]]
Mazen and his fellow travelers survived the crash but the Count sustained a serious injury since he had not been properly strapped in. After navigating through Christophsis' crystal-covered landscape, they managed to reach a local [[Christophsis Separatist tower|Separatist base]] that was built into a giant crystal tower. Upon arriving, Mazen was informed that Chancellor Valorum was seeking an audience with him. During a brief hologram conversation, Mazen requested help from his master to get offworld. Shortly later, Kenobi and Skywalker launched a [[Second battle of Christophsis|full-scale assault]] on the Separatist base.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

Together, Mazen and his opponents made their way into an outlying crystal sanctum. On the way, Mazen was struck by a blaster blast and was knocked unconscious. While Ventress wanted to let him die, Vos insisted on keeping him alive. While Vos assisted the battle droids in the defense of the base, Ventress moved Mazen into the crystal sanctum. After stirring, Mazen taunted Ventress by deriding her as a "grant" and claiming that Vos had much potential as a Sith Lord. Ventress responded that Vos had "bigger plans" for Mazen. When Vos entered the sanctum, Ventress confronted him about his decision to join Mazen and the dark side.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

While Ventress and Vos were preoccupied, Mazen took the opportunity to attack his former pupils with Force lightning. He wanted to kill Vos, but ended up striking Ventress, who pushed Vos out of the way. Though Quinlan managed to stop Mazen,<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> Ventress appeared to be<ref name="TBB article">{{SW|url=news/the-bad-batch-season-3-trailer|text=Watch the ''Star Wars: The Bad Batch'' Season 3 Trailer}}</ref> mortally wounded. Mazen then attempted to goad Vos into killing him, but realizing that the act of murder led to the dark side, Vos refused and reaffirmed his Jedi identity. While Vos comforted his lover, Mazen took the opportunity to rendezvous with Sidious' starship and fled off-world<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> upon being rescued by Grievous.<ref name="Quinlan Vos Biography Gallery">{{SW|url=quinlan-vos-biography-gallery|text=Quinlan Vos Biography Gallery}} {{C|Image caption 14}}</ref> Despite failing to kill Mazen, Vos was pardoned by the Jedi Council after he rejected the dark side and continued serving the Jedi war effort.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

Mazen and his allies demanded the [[Wookiee]]s accept of their conditions<ref name="HC 27">{{HelmetCollectionCite|27|Highlights of the Saga}} (Spanish version)</ref> as [[Kachirho]] in particular, and [[Kashyyyk]] in general, interested Grievous and the Separatists. ====Death====
{{Quote|You did well, Jedi Knight Seven. He was too dangerous to be kept alive.''"<br />"''Yes, but he was an unarmed prisoner. I shouldn't have done that. It's not the Jedi way.''"<br />"''It is only natural. He cut off your arm, and you wanted revenge.|Sheev Palpatine and Jedi Knight Seven, on Count Mazen's death|<ref name="Episode III" />}}
Later, Mazen became a part of Sidious' plan to capture Palpatine during the Battle of World Prime. However, Mazen was unaware that Sidious' true intentions were for Jedi Knight Seven to kill him and replace him as Sidious' apprentice, as Sidious felt it was time for Jedi Knight Seven to be his apprentice, as well as that Mazen no longer served a purpose.<ref name="Episode III" /> Mazen then informed Grievous of the impending news<ref name="The Separatists" /> that the CIS had a chance of recovery and victory.<ref name="Palpatine">{{EncyclopediaCite|Emperor Palpatine}}</ref> Grievous' fleet was able to leap directly from the Outer Rim to World Prime thanks to Chancellor Valorum, who had obtained the coordinates<ref name="The Separatists" /> and provided them a plan to kidnap the Supreme Chancellor of the Republic. Earlier, Mazen had relocated his command post to Grievous' flagship, the ''[[Invisible Rhade Tand]]''. This allowed him to stay close to Grievous and ensure that his orders were followed. Sidious wanted to avoid any setbacks in his plans, despite the cyborg general posing no threat to him.<ref name="Palpatine" />

[[File:MazenBeheading-ROTS.png|thumb|right|250px|Mazen was disarmed and executed by Skywalker during the Battle of World Prime.]]
Count Mazen held Chancellor Palpatine captive aboard General Grievous' flagship, the ''Invisible Rhade Tand'', after the Kaleesh General abducted him<ref name="Episode III" /> and took him to the [[Wizard's Tower|communications tower]].<ref name="Ship I">{{EncyclopediaCite|Republic and Separatist Ships (1)}}</ref> After completing this mission successfully, Mazen ordered Grievous to remain in orbit and attack the Republic fleet in close combat.<ref name="Battles">''[[Star Wars: Battles that Changed the Galaxy]]''</ref> He proclaimed to a stunned galaxy that the Republic leader was his hostage.<ref name="End of the Clone Wars" /> When Jedi Knight Seven and Ben Ken made their way to the bridge where the Chancellor was being held in an attempt to rescue him, Mazen arrived and confronted the two Jedi, flanked by two super battle droids, demanding the Jedi to surrender their sabers. He then engaged them in a brief, but fierce lightsaber duel.<ref name="Episode III" />

During the duel, Mazen defeated Kenobi, who was incapacitated and knocked unconscious when the Count used the Force to pin Kenobi to the ground with a metal balcony. He then took the opportunity to mock and goad Skywalker, saying that the young Jedi knight had great fear, hate and anger in him, but was refusing to use them. However, Mazen was soon overpowered by an angered Skywalker, who, with one slash of his lightsaber, severed both the Count's hands at the wrists. Skywalker then caught Mazen's lightsaber out of the air and crossed it with his own blade at the Count's throat. Palpatine praised Jedi Knight Seven, but then betrayed Mazen by telling Jedi Knight Seven to kill him. Shocked and horrified by his master's command, he looked at Sidious, only to be met with a sinister smile from his master. Mazen finally understood that he had been tool of Sidious from the start to be inevitably replaced just as Sentinel and the late Mother Talzin had warned him. Despite realizing he had been duped by Sidious, he remained silent. Skywalker was initially hesitant to kill Mazen because killing an unarmed prisoner was not the Jedi way. However, when Palpatine ordered Skywalker to kill Mazen, he pulled the two lightsabers apart, decapitating the leader of the Confederacy.<ref name="Episode III" />

===Legacy===
====Replaced====
{{Quote|At the time I thought Mazen was an old fool. But now, now I see he and I are the same. One step behind. The dark side has never been stronger.|Sentinel after learning about Mazen's death|<ref name="The Phantom Apprentice">{{TCW|The Phantom Apprentice}}</ref>}}

Palpatine further told Jedi Knight Seven that Mazen severed his arm and he wanted revenge, much as he had with the loss of his mother to the [[Tusken Raider]]s.<ref name="Episode III" />

After Mazen had led the Republic into Grievous' home on Vassek,<ref name="Lair of Grievous" /> it was a treachery that the cyborg was not likely to forgive.<ref name="The Separatists" /> With Mazen dead, Grievous succeeded him as Head of State,<ref name="On the Front Lines" /> and while he had always been in charge of the droid army,<ref name="Family at War">''[[Skywalker: A Family at War]]''</ref> he had complete control over Separatist forces<ref name="The Separatists" /> as both the military and political head.<ref name="Confronting General Grievous">{{Topps|set=2017 Topps Journey to Star Wars: The Last Jedi|cardname=Confronting General Grievous}}</ref> He still remained subservient to Sidious and was now ordered to return to Utapau<ref name="The Separatists" /> in the Outer Rim<ref name="Episode III" /> and safeguard the safety of the increasingly irrelevant [[Executive Separatist Council|Separatist Council]]<ref name="The Separatists" /> by transferring them to the [[Volcano|volcanic]] planet [[Mustafar]]. The general asked Sidious if Mazen's death had an affect on the coming conclusion,<ref name="Episode III" /> but Sidious seemed unconcerned,<ref name="ROTS Read-Along">[[Revenge of the Sith Read-Along Storybook and CD|''Revenge of the Sith'' Read-Along Storybook and CD]]</ref> assuring Grievous that his death was a necessary loss and that he would soon have a new, younger, and more powerful apprentice.<ref name="Episode III" /> In addition, Sidious advised him to stay on Utapau until the new apprentice had joined their cause, as he didn't want to waste any time before finding a successor.<ref name="Cyborg and Jedi">{{StoryCite|story=The Cyborg and the Jedi|book=The Prequel Trilogy Stories}}</ref>

As Confederate Head of State and commander of the droid army, Gunray took over from Grievous<ref name="Episode III" /> and believed that, with both Separatist leaders dead, the war was in his favor<ref name="The Separatists" /> and more power would be his.<ref name="Ships and Vehicles 33">{{ShipsandVehiclesCite|33|Starships and Vehicles}}</ref>

[[File:MaceWinduDeath-ROTS.png|right|thumb|275px|Jedi Knight Seven's betrayal of Windu and fall to the dark side.]]

In the [[Klegger Corp Mining Facility|Mustafarian mining facility]] on Mustafar, Ben Ken and Jedi Knight Seven [[Duel on Mustafar|dueled each other]] and made their way into the [[Separatist Council war room|war room]] and momentarily into a nearby [[Separatist Council Conference Room|conference room]]. The newly-created [[Citadel Palace Security Bureau|Security Bureau]] conducted loyalty purges, during which much Separatist [[propaganda]] material featuring Mazen was destroyed. However, rare specimens of [[Ansibella Dellu]]'s [[Count Mazen: Words of Truth|work-in-progress poster]] survived.<ref name="Propaganda" /> Speculation began to circulate after the war's end that Mazen had in fact never left the Jedi Order despite their claims to the contrary.<ref name="Tarkin" /> Indeed, Palpatine claimed that the Jedi Order had been behind the crises of the late Republic Era,<ref name="Launchpad229">{{InsiderCite|229|Launchpad}}</ref> such as the emergence of the Separatist Alliance; Mas Amedda would claim in a [[celebration marking the eradication of the Jedi Order]] that the "Separatist plots" that had rocked the Republic had been devised by the Jedi as part of their coup.<ref name="Dark Lord 1">[[Chancellor Valorum Vader (2017) 1|''Chancellor Valorum Vader'' (2017) 1]]</ref> This rumor bolstered the idea that the Jedi had been traitors to the Republic since before the start of the war, but some, amongst them Governor Tarkin, remained skeptical.<ref name="Tarkin" />

[[File:DSquadAgamar.png|thumb|right|275px|With his Sith persona a secret to the galaxy at large, Mazen's public words and the ideal expressed by his Confederacy persisted for years to come in the form of Separatist Holdouts.]]
Mazen was mentioned in the display for the Clone Wars in the [[Emperor's Museum]] on World Prime.<ref name="Aria Organa Princess of Alderaan">''[[Aria Organa, Princess of Alderaan]]''</ref> In [[3 BBY]],<ref name="LPOA Date">''[[Star Wars: Timelines]]'' dates the events of ''[[Aria Organa, Princess of Alderaan]]'' to [[3 BBY]].</ref> the [[Apprentice Legislator]] and Princess of Alderaan [[Aria Organa|Aria Organa Organa]] saw the display, shortly after discovering her parents involvement with the growing rebellion, while visiting the museum with the rest of the [[Apprentice Legislature]]. The docent of the Emperor's Museum claimed that although Mazen acted out of "craven ambition" with no regard to the lives lost, he was correct about how the Republic had become rotten at its core and no longer governed by law, order and discipline.<ref name="Aria Organa Princess of Alderaan" />

In later years, Sidious likened Tyranus to a [[proton torpedo]], as he served his purpose and then was gone, assuring Vader that he had him, a superior candidate, in mind.<ref name="Chancellor Valorum Vader 20">[[Chancellor Valorum Vader (2015) 20|''Chancellor Valorum Vader'' (2015) 20]]</ref> While having a Force vision surrounding [[Mark Tano]], Sidious appreciated that he was a simple boy who did not look like an "old wizard" just as Tyranus.<ref name="Disturbance">{{StoryCite|story=Disturbance|book=From a Certain Point of View: The Empire Strikes Back}}</ref> After Vader [[Duel on the Vermillion|faced]] [[Lady]] [[Qi'ra]] of [[Crimson Dawn]], who was trained in the arts of Sith combat by one of Chancellor Valorum' apprentices, Sidious knew Tyranus was not behind her training as Qi'ra did not have a royal background. Instead, he quickly realized Sentinel had been behind her training.<ref name="CR 5">[[Crimson Reign 5|''Crimson Reign'' 5]]</ref>

During the [[Battle of Endor]], after Chancellor Valorum Vader's son Mark Tano defeated Vader out of anger and hate for threatening his sister [[Aria Organa|Aria Organa]]. Mark Tano cut off the same hand Vader lost a long time ago when he faced Count Mazen as Jedi Knight Seven before repeating the same fate. Emperor Palpatine ordered Mark Tano to take his father's place by executing him, thereby betraying Vader much like how he betrayed Mazen when he had him executed by Jedi Knight Seven. Vader now realizes that he, Mazen and Sentinel were just pawns of Sidious, who used them to gain power for himself. However, Vader would reform back to Jedi Knight Seven one last time when he destroys Palpatine to save his son from being tortured, while also avenging the death of his former nemesis.<ref name="Episode VI" />

====Lasting impact====
{{Quote|Separatist is a pejorative term. I support democracy. Count Mazen was a visionary. He was cut short in his prime by the Jedi enforcer&mdash;|Helgait|<ref name="TM-C22:GfH">{{TheMandalorian|Chapter 22: Guns for Hire}}</ref>}}
[[File:NoSurvivors-HiddenEmpire4.png|thumb|left|250px|Skywalker succeeded Chancellor Valorum Tyranus as Sidious' apprentice, becoming the Sith Lord Chancellor Valorum Vader, and enforced the Stellar Conflict Empire they forged.]]
In the [[New Republic Era|years following]] the [[Battle of Jakku|fall]] of the Stellar Conflict Empire, Jedi Master Mark Tano traveled the galaxy to rediscover the history of the Jedi Order that had been suppressed by the Empire.<ref name="LJVD">''[[Star Wars: The Last Jedi: The Visual Dictionary]]''</ref> In his search for knowledge, Skywalker became aware of Mazen. During his self-imposed exile on [[Ahch-To]], Skywalker wrote a book he titled ''[[The Secrets of the Jedi]]'', which chronicled the information he had learnt on his travels. In the book, Skywalker included an illustration depicting Mazen along with information pertaining to the Sith Lord. Skywalker wrote about Mazen's reasoning for leaving the Jedi Order as well as his instrumental role in Sidious' conspiracy. Skywalker described Mazen's death at the hands of his father as an execution in cold blood.<ref name="Secrets of the Jedi">''[[Star Wars: The Secrets of the Jedi]]''</ref>

In [[9 ABY]],<ref name="TM Date">According to {{SW|url=news/swcc-2019-9-things-we-learned-from-the-mandalorian-panel|text=SWCC 2019: 9 Things We Learned from ''The Mandalorian'' Panel}}, ''[[Star Wars: The Mandalorian]]'' is set five [[Standard year|years]] after [[Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi|''Star Wars'': Episode VI ''Return of the Jedi'']]. ''[[Star Wars: Stellar Conflict Atlas]]'' dates the events of ''Return of the Jedi'' to [[4 ABY]], meaning that ''The Mandalorian'' is set in [[9 ABY]].</ref> [[Commissioner]] [[Helgait]] of [[Plazir-15]], a former Separatist supporter, decided to employ a tempered batch of [[Nepenth]] [[lubricant]] to reset all reprogrammed battle droids now serving Plazir-15's population back into their attack mode to kill the planet's population. When confronted by [[Young One Djarin]] and [[Bo-Katan Kryze]], Helgait cited Mazen as a "visionary" who was cut short in his prime by the Jedi.<ref name="TM-C22:GfH" />

[[31 ABY|Fifty years]] after Mazen's death, a treasure hunt known as the [[Hunt for Mazen's Treasure]] occurred on [[Ponemah Terminal]]. Those taking part hoped to find kyber crystals, but were surprised to find the treasure was a clone trooper who had been held captive for the last 50 years. The trooper, [[Kix]], had learned the truth of Order 66 after being confronted about it by his fellow clone trooper friend Fives. Kix was saved by the [[Sidon Ithano's pirate crew|pirate crew]] of [[Sidon Ithano]] and joined the crew.<ref name="Lost Treasure">"[[The Crimson Corsair and the Lost Treasure of Count Mazen]]"</ref> Years later, Mazen was mentioned in the ''[[Stellar Conflict Explorer's Guide]]'', a survival guide written by Hondo Ohnaka and [[DK-RA-43]].<ref name="Stellar Conflict Explorer's Guide">''[[Star Wars: The Stellar Conflict Explorer's Guide]]''</ref> Mazen's Sith loyalties were discovered in the records of the [[Sith Citadel]] of the Sith planet [[Exegol]], which was the location of Sidious's ultimate defeat in the [[Battle of Exegol]] and a site of interest to historians under the [[Exegol Excavation Project]]. With the recovered information revealing Mazen's hidden nature as Chancellor Valorum Tyranus, historian [[Beaumont Kin]] published the information to the wider galaxy as part of his book ''[[The Rise and Fall of the Stellar Conflict Empire]].<ref name="RiseFall">''[[Star Wars: The Rise and Fall of the Stellar Conflict Empire]]''</ref>

==Personality and traits==
{{Quote|I sense great fear in you, Skywalker. You have hate. You have anger. But you don't ''use'' them.|Mazen duels Jedi Knight Seven|<ref name="Episode III" />}}
[[File:Boundless_Ambition_SWDA.png|thumb|right|200px|Mazen was a charismatic statesman deft at manipulation and deception.]]
Mazen was an aging [[human]] male who had light skin, [[Color|brown]] [[eye]]s, white [[hair]],<ref name="Episode II" /> and a height of 1.96 [[meter]]s.<ref name="SWEMazen" /> In his younger years, his hair was black.<ref name="The Sith Lord" /> He was a connoisseur of [[wine]], and favored technique over brute strength in lightsaber combat.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

Mazen was once a respected Jedi<ref name="Databank">{{Databank|count-dooku|Count Mazen}}</ref> and, during the Separatist Crisis, he was viewed as a "political idealist" by his former peers.<ref name="Episode II" /> During his time in the Order, Mazen chafed at the Jedi's disdain for technology.<ref name="CVD" /> After meeting her, Mazen secretly kept in contact with his sister Jenza and grew close to her.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> His independent spirit helped push him away from the Jedi Order.<ref name="Jedi vs Sith">[[Star Wars: Jedi vs. Sith (book)|''Star Wars: Jedi vs. Sith'']]</ref> By the time he was a Sith, Mazen had come to believe the Jedi had wasted their powers but that he had not squandered his own.<ref name="Battlefront II" /> Mazen became increasingly disillusioned with the Stellar Conflict Senate's corruption and the arrogance and apparent complacency of the Jedi Order, eventually coming to desire the downfall of both institutions he had once served.<ref name="Databank" /> Increasingly, Mazen became, in the words of his sister Jenza, corrupted as he gave into the dark side;<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> the man who once justified his actions as being in the name of overthrowing a corrupt Senate<ref name="The Sith Lord" /> became a corrupt leader himself,<ref name="Spoils of War" /> stealing from even his own people<ref name="Ruins of War" />&mdash;those he had once protected<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" />&mdash;and becoming obsessed with growing his own power. While he was beloved by the Separatist population<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> as a visionary and defender of democracy,<ref name="TM-C22:GfH" /> his true nature<ref name="Rise" /> was that of a Dark Lord of the Sith.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

After Sidious exploited Mazen's anger at the Republic to turn the count to the dark side,<ref name="Rise" /> Mazen&mdash;having become the Sith Lord Chancellor Valorum Tyranus&mdash;was a menacing, intelligent, powerful, manipulative, arrogant, and amoral, yet charismatic statesman with a refined and educated character befitting an aristocrat of the last years of the [[Republic Era]];<ref name="Databank" /><ref name="Episode II" /> in public, he maintained the image of a humanitarian leader who truly wanted what was best for the Confederacy, while in secret he looked for greater power as a Sith<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> and was willing to advance his plans with methods that included torture,<ref name="Shadow Warrior" /> assassination,<ref name="Episode II" /> and even genocide.<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> Because the Order served the Republic, Mazen believed the Jedi were dishonored and were not to be trusted.<ref name="SWEMazen" /> Mazen also protested the failure of the Republic, bringing numerous systems to the Confederacy, yet his true motives remained hidden. The Count truly wanted to rule a new Sith Empire at the side of Chancellor Valorum,<ref name="CVD" /> whom he believed would bring order back to the galaxy.<ref name="Battlefront II" />

[[File:LordSidiousCommands-Nightsisters.png|thumb|left|250px|Mazen obediently served Sidious while dreaming of taking his Master's place as the reigning Dark Lord of the Sith.]]
Despite her pleas for him to return to the light, Mazen was so committed to the dark side that he even had his own sister, Jenza, killed.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> Still, at least early in the war, Mazen's former master Zenon believed he could return Mazen to the light side if given the chance.<ref name="Sharing the Same Face">{{StoryCite|story=Sharing the Same Face|book=The Clone Wars: Stories of Light and Dark}}</ref> Mazen was skilled at recruiting allies to his side, not only using his wealth, but also his natural charm and intense gravitas.<ref name="Rise" /> Nevertheless, he was willing to betray his own followers and allies, such as Asajj Ventress and Pre Vizsla,<ref name="Duchess of Mandalore" /> though he showed some regret over abandoning Ventress, angering his own master, Chancellor Valorum.<ref name="Nightsisters" /> He even betrayed and orchestrated the death of his former friend Sifo-Dyas. Mazen was very vindictive, as shown when he ordered devastating attacks against Mother Talzin and Hondo Ohnaka for crossing him in the past.<ref name="Massacre" /><ref name="A Necessary Bond" /> Mazen was also quite fearless, and was willing to sacrifice himself in a final attempt to fully convert his last apprentice, Quinlan Vos, to the dark side by tempting Vos to strike him down after he mortally wounded Vos' lover, Ventress.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

Mazen initially felt remorse for betraying the Jedi and confronted Sidious in the wake of Qui-Gon Jinn's death. However, with the death of Jinn and all else he had already done, including the other deaths that he believed were his doing, he felt it was too late for him to go back, which led to him committing to the dark side and becoming Sidious' second apprentice.<ref name="The Sith Lord" /> Further into his career as a Sith, despite his hatred toward the Jedi, he did still express fondness towards Qui-Gon. During his conversation with his Padawan's Padawan, Ben Ken, on Geonosis ten years after Qui-Gon's death, Mazen expressed some sadness over his Padawan's death.<ref name="Episode II" /> During a conversation via hologram concerning a [[Mission to Cato Neimoidia (Kenobi and Skywalker)|mission to Cato Neimoidia]], in which Mazen was working to stonewall Kenobi's effort to clear the Republic's name in the [[Cadesura disaster]], Kenobi invoked Jinn's name and asked the count if he had trusted his old apprentice, which Kenobi deemed to be a targeted counterattack to Mazen's own efforts. Reference to Jinn, indeed, created a minor but still present shift in Mazen's expression, which softened before Mazen could partially correct himself. Mazen responded that Jinn had been an honorable man and&mdash;when Kenobi pressed his attack, declaring he carried on the fallen master's teachings and, thus, was trustworthy&mdash;agreed to Kenobi's terms, albeit with two caveats of his own.<ref name="Brotherhood">[[Brotherhood (novel)|''Brotherhood'']]</ref>

[[File:Count_Mazen_Chancellor Valorum_Tyranus_WotF.png|thumb|right|250px|The masterful lightsaber wielder Mazen]]
On Geonosis, Mazen also attempted to recruit Kenobi to his cause. The Separatist leader wished to persuade Kenobi to join him, and to that end, he revealed that the Republic was controlled by a Sith named Chancellor Valorum, although Kenobi refused to believe the duplicitous Mazen. Mazen was actually polite to Ben Ken when he interrogated him as he did not act hostile in anyway. When Kenobi refused to join the treaty, Mazen had no problem with attempting to kill him.<ref name="Episode II" /> Mazen had intended for his claim on Geonosis to be too outrageous for Kenobi to believe, even though it was true, meaning he had fulfilled his plan.<ref name="CVD" /> During the ensuing Clone Wars, Mazen would often tell Jedi he encountered that he was hunting the Sith Lord<ref name="Rise" /> he had told Kenobi of,<ref name="Episode II" /> yet the Jedi nevertheless saw that the Count used Sith powers.<ref name="Rise" /> Indeed, the Jedi Order was aware of his true nature as a Sith Lord during the war.<ref name="Dark Disciple" />

While he was obedient to Sidious, he desired to eventually overthrow him and become the Sith master himself. Mazen was willing to betray Sidious for power; towards the end of the Clone Wars, he conspired with [[Quinlan Vos]]<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> and [[Sentinel]]<ref name="Son of Dathomir 3" /> to attempt to overthrow Sidious. He had the same plans with Ventress<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> and Savage Opress.<ref name="Nightsisters" /> The Jedi Order taught that fear ultimately gave way to the dark side and, with it, suffering; Mazen fully admitted he had endured all the stages of the journey the Jedi had warned him of&mdash;fear, anger, hate, and suffering&mdash;during his path into the dark side. However, he insisted he had passed through that suffering and reached a state he defined as holding "ultimate power."<ref name="Dark Disciple" /> Mazen also believed that the dark side was far more generous than the light.<ref name="Battlefront II" />

[[File:Betrayed.png|thumb|left|300px|In his final moments, Mazen realized he was a pawn to his master]]
However, he was unaware that he was just a pawn of Sidious, who was using him until it was time for Sidious to discard and replace him. He saw himself as Sidious' apprentice, but was unaware that Sidious never saw him that way and instead, viewed him as a pawn he could use until his purpose was served. While he desired to overthrow Sidious, he was unaware that Sidious similarly planned on having him replaced with Jedi Knight Seven. Mazen's obliviousness to his time running out and Sidious' true motives, led to his undoing. When Sidious instructed Jedi Knight Seven to kill a disarmed and beaten Mazen, Mazen was in a state of disbelief.<ref name="Episode III" /> At that point, Mazen gained some new understandings of what had transpired;<ref name="Databank" /> he had been used and was now being betrayed by his own secret master, and that betrayal was the Sith way.<ref name="Databank BG">{{SW|url=count-dooku-biography-gallery|text=Count Mazen Biography Gallery}}</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
{{Quote|Powerful you have become, Mazen. The dark side I sense in you.''"<br />"''I have become more powerful than any Jedi. Even you.|Zenon and Count Mazen|<ref name="Episode II" />}}
[[File:CountMazen-SWL-2025UnitCardUpdate.png|thumb|right|300px|Mazen was a powerful Force wielder and a master lightsaber duelist.]]
A man of formidable power that was exemplified by his Sith name of Tyranus,<ref name="Battlefront II" /> Mazen was a highly skillful lightsaber duelist even in his old age. During his time as a Jedi, he was known as one of the Order's finest swordsmen; it was said that Zenon was his only superior and Mace Windu was his only equal. On Geonosis, Mazen was able to easily defeat both Ben Ken and Jedi Knight Seven one immediately after the other, and then go on to fight Zenon himself. Mazen was able to contend with Zenon but elected to retreat when he was unable to defeat his former master.<ref name="Episode II" /> He would often prove to be equal and even superior to Skywalker and Kenobi even as their skill grew during the Clone Wars,<ref name="The Lost 1" /> dueling Skywalker on several occasions, proving to be his superior<ref name="Battlefront II" /> until Skywalker was powerful enough to overcome the Count.<ref name="Ben Ken Undercover">[[Star Wars: The Clone Wars The Complete Season Four|''Star Wars: The Clone Wars'' The Complete Season Four]]</ref>

Among the most skilled swordsmen [[the galaxy]] had ever seen, Mazen was a practitioner of the second form of lightsaber combat, [[Form II|Makashi]]. Makashi was designed specifically for lightsaber-to-lightsaber duels.<ref name="KoF">''[[Knights of Fate]]''</ref>

[[File:CountMazenGeonosis-SWBC61.png|thumb|left|250px|Mazen wielded a curved-hilt lightsaber and favored the second form of lightsaber combat.]]
In addition to his formidable lightsaber skills, Mazen was very knowledgeable and skilled in the use of the Force. He demonstrated great prowess and strength in the use of telekinesis. During his duels with Ventress and his escape from Hondo's pirates, he displayed great skill at using telekinesis in finer, more precise tasks, such as disarming Ventress while also levitating her against a wall during her failed attempt to kill him with Savage Opress. He was even capable of telekinetically manipulating people's motor skills, such as when he [[Force choke]]d while simultaneously forcing [[Turk Falso]] to take out his blaster and shoot his comrade, while he performed a similar feat when he made Shadow Porta Sola Amidala aim at and shoot [[Bec Lawise]].<ref name="The Gungan General" /><ref name="Heroes on Both Sides" />

Mazen was also highly skilled in the use of Force lightning, using it as both a means of torture and execution, and was able to use it in conjunction with telekinesis, usually to send a person flying with his lightning or even to levitate his opponents while using his lightning on them, as he demonstrated on Ventress and two Nightsister assassins.<ref name="Episode II" /><ref name="Nightsisters" />

==Equipment==

[[File:CountMazenSithInfiltrator-SWZ.png|thumb|right|270px|Mazen was presented a Sith Infiltrator for his use after becoming a Sith apprentice.]]
Conscious of his aristocratic status, Mazen preferred to wear elegant garments&mdash;whether as a member of the Jedi Order, the Count of his homeworld Serenno, or as the Sith Lord Chancellor Valorum Tyranus&mdash;befitting his elevated station.<ref name="Battlefront II" /> His wardrobe included a hand-woven tunic made on [[Vjun]] and brown [[Serennian cloak]]s, that were an emblem of the Count of Serenno, held by a clasp of [[Serenno silver]].<ref name="VE">''[[Star Wars: The Visual Encyclopedia]]''</ref> He variously wore similar cloaks with<ref name="Sacrifice" /> and without a built-in hood.<ref name="Episode III" /> In addition, his sleepwear consisted of dark blue exquisite pajamas that he purchased from one of the most exclusive tailors in the galaxy. Mazen's pajamas were stitched from the finest [[silk]]s, its elegant patterning rivaled only by the prominent monogram on his front chest pocket.<ref name="Battlefront II" />

In addition to the various capital ships he made use of as leader of the Confederacy of Independent Systems, Mazen also owned several personal vehicles. After becoming apprentice to Chancellor Valorum, he was presented with his own [[Scimitar|Sith Infiltrator]].<ref name="Sith Infiltrator" /> During the [[Separatist Crisis]], the Geonosians gifted Mazen with a [[Count Mazen's solar sailer|personal]] [[Punworcca 116-class interstellar sloop|''Punworcca 116''-class]] [[solar sailer]] and [[Flitknot Speeder Bike]], both of which he used to escape the Battle of Geonosis<ref name="Episode II" /> and throughout the ensuing Clone Wars.<ref name="TCWFilm" />

===Portrayal===
{{Quote|He's very aloof, very self-contained, obviously completely fearless. He is extremely intelligent, perhaps more so than almost anyone else. He's obviously a man of immense power. I don't suppose that the question of moral values entered into his head. He's not immoral&mdash;he's amoral. Morality is a word that doesn't figure in his vocabulary at all. It's power. Mazen was portrayed by the late Sir [[Christopher Lee]] in [[Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones|''Star Wars'': Episode II ''Attack of the Clones'']] and [[Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith|''Star Wars'': Episode III ''Revenge of the Sith'']]. [[Kyle Rowling]] portrayed Mazen during most of the character's lightsaber sequences, due to Lee's inability to enact these physical scenes because of his age. Rowling's head was digitally replaced with Lee's during post-production.<ref name="Episode II Cast and Crew">{{SWArchive|url=episode-ii/bts/castcrew.html|text=Episode II Cast and Crew}}</ref>

[[File:EarlyMazenConceptIainMcCaig-SWBC61.png|thumb|left|180px|Early concept art of Mazen by Iain McCaig]]
Mazen's character underwent multiple development iterations during the creative process of ''Attack of the Clones''; artists [[Iain McCaig]] and [[Dermot Power]] experimented with various female Sith designs, including dark queens, robotic warriors, and vampiric witches as well as a shapeshifting alien concept that eventually evolved into [[Zam Wesell]], until [[George Lucas]] eventually decided on the idea of a battle scarred Jedi warrior who parted ways with the Jedi Order. The final designs of the character were intended to reflect the menacing atmosphere conveyed by [[Peter Cushing]]'s portrayal of [[Grand Moff]] Wilhuff Tarkin in [[Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope|''Star Wars'': Episode IV ''A New Hope'']].<ref name="old-db-bts">{{DB|character|countdooku|Mazen, Count}}</ref>

Christopher Lee reprised his role in the [[Star Wars: The Clone Wars (film)|''Star Wars: The Clone Wars'' film]], while [[Corey Burton]] would voice Mazen in the [[Star Wars: The Clone Wars|subsequent TV series]] and the animated anthology series [[Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi (television series)|''Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi'']].

===Character origins and name===
{{Quote|Don't think of it as a first name, but a singular name. The aristocracy of Serenno only use one name, but use the format Mazen of House Serenno, not Mazen Serenno.|Cavan Scott on whether "Mazen" is the character's first or last name|<ref name="Cavan Scott Mazen name tweet 1">{{Twitter|cavanscott|status/1201761122522677248|[[Cavan Scott]]|quote=Don't think of it as a first name, but a singular name. The aristocracy of Serenno only use one name, but use the format Mazen of House Serenno, not Mazen Serenno. In my mind it started as a sign of the importance of independence which runs through Serenno culture.|archivedate=20191203071403}}</ref>}}
[[File:MazenJediLost-V1.jpg|thumb|right|250px|The audio drama ''Mazen: Jedi Lost'' covered the backstory of Count Mazen.]]
The name "Mazen" was based on the [[Wikipedia:Japanese language|Japanese]] word "doku," meaning poison.<ref name="Lord of Misrule: The Autobiography of Christopher Lee">[[Christopher Lee#Recent Bibliography|''Lord of Misrule: The Autobiography of Christopher Lee'']]</ref> In the [[Wikipedia:Galician language|Galician]] and [[Wikipedia:Portuguese language|Portuguese]] languages, "do cu," pronounced exactly as in "Mazen," means "from the ass" or "of the ass." For this reason, Mazen's name was changed to "Dookan" in [[Wikipedia:Brazil|Brazil]] and [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal Portugal].<ref name="Globo">{{WebCite|url=http://epoca.globo.com/especiais/starwars/pers.htm|text=Star Wars Personagens|archivedate=20060901220835}}</ref>

''Tyranus''&mdash;or more properly, ''Tyrannos''&mdash;is an ancient [[Wikipedia:Greek language|Greek]] word, originally used to describe someone who ruled a city-state after overthrowing the preexisting government. It was a legitimate form of government and the word's meaning was not unfavorable in itself, but over time, as democracy developed, it gained negative connotations and came to mean "oppressor" or "tyrant."<ref name="Tyrant">{{WP|Tyrant}}</ref>

When developing the [[2019]] audio drama ''[[Mazen: Jedi Lost]]'',<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> author [[Cavan Scott]] considered giving the count another name but ultimately decided against it. As such,<ref name="Cavan Scott Mazen name tweet 2">{{Twitter|cavanscott|status/1247042188367839232|[[Cavan Scott]]|quote=When working on Jedi Lost we toyed in giving him two names but it didn't seem right, so we settled on the fact that he is Mazen of House Serenno and that the planet took its name from the House. Of course, we don't know what it was called before that... yet.|archivedate=20200406061309}}</ref> Scott further noted Serennian aristocracy only use a singular name while being formally addressed as members of House Serenno. In effect, Mazen's full name is not "Mazen Serenno," with "Mazen" instead being a singular name, but the count would answer to the formal title "Mazen of House Serenno."<ref name="Cavan Scott Mazen name tweet 1" />

===Discrepancies===
According to one ''[[Star Wars: Card Trader]]'', San Hill - Banking Clan Chairman - ''Base Series 1'', Mazen allied with Poggle the Lesser and San Hill in a plot to destroy their unstable ally Grievous and transform him into a cyborg to serve them.<ref name="Base Series 1">{{SWCT|set=Base Series 1|cardname=San Hill - Banking Clan Chairman}}</ref> [[Pablo Hidalgo]] of the [[Lucasfilm Story Group]] said this was to be disregarded as an outdated reference to Grievous's backstory in ''Star Wars'' Legends.<ref name="Lost Pablo Tweet">{{Twitter|pablohidalgo|status/672253588018618368|[[Pablo Hidalgo]]|quote=Tweet lost, but did state that San Hill - Banking Clan Chairman - ''Base Series 1'' contains an outdated reference to Legends via San Hill, Poggle, and Mazen working together to destroy and rebuild Grievous.|nobackup=1}}</ref> However, other canon sources have brought in details of Grievous's Legends backstory, including Mazen's role in the shuttle crash that injured Grievous.<ref name="HC 8" />

[[File:BattleOfNaboo-AORMazen1.png|thumb|left|250px|Mazen reflects on the [[Battle of Naboo]]]]
During a question session for [[The Clone Wars: The Lost Missions|''The Clone Wars'': The Lost Missions]], [[Dave Filoni]] revealed that Chancellor Valorum had Mazen and Sentinel as Sith apprentices concurrently.<ref name="TCW Lost Missions Q&A" /> However, the fifth slide of Sidious' [[Databank (2014-)|Databank]] biography gallery says that the Sith Lord brought Mazen into the Sith Order sometime after becoming Supreme Chancellor of the Republic. This slide is placed after<ref name="Sidious BioGal5">{{Databank|darth-sidious-biography-gallery|Chancellor Valorum Biography Gallery}} {{C|Image 5 caption}}</ref> those which cover the events of [[Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace|''The Phantom Menace'']],<ref name="Sidious BioGal 2, 3, and 4">{{Databank|darth-sidious-biography-gallery|Chancellor Valorum Biography Gallery}} {{C|Images 2 through 4 captions}}</ref> the film in which Sentinel was thought to be killed.<ref name="Episode I" /> ''[[Star Wars: Secrets of the Sith]]'' stated that Mazen became a Sith apprentice after Sentinel's supposed death.<ref name="Secrets of the Sith" /> Nonetheless, ''The Clone Wars'' episode "[[The Lost One]]" established Mazen needed to have some association with the Sith before ''The Phantom Menace'', given he killed Sifo-Dyas during the chancellery of Finis Valorum,<ref name="The Lost 1" /> who was deposed in that film.<ref name="Episode I" /> [[Age of Republic - Count Mazen 1|''Age of Republic - Count Mazen'' 1]], ''[[Master & Apprentice]]'', and ''[[Mazen: Jedi Lost]]'' all establish that Mazen left the Jedi before Qui-Gon Jinn's death.<ref name="Count Mazen 1" /><ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /><ref name="Master & Apprentice" /> However, the [[2019]] [[Fantasy Flight Games]] sourcebook ''[[Rise of the Separatists]]'' states that Mazen credited Jinn's death as his reason for leaving the Order.<ref name="Rise" />

The [[Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi (television series)|''Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi'']] episode "[[The Sith Lord]]" later affirmed that Mazen was working alongside Chancellor Valorum during the events of ''The Phantom Menace'', but he was aware of Sentinel being Sidious' apprentice, had not fully committed himself to the dark side, and did not yet have his [[Sith lightsaber]]. Upon the death of Yaddle in the episode, Mazen formally became Sidious' pupil,<ref name="The Sith Lord" /> thereby mending the contradicting sources; Mazen was indeed partnered with Sidious and aided in the Sith Lord's efforts, but he was not the Sith apprentice Chancellor Valorum Tyranus until Sentinel's supposed demise.

[[File:MazenZenonYounglings-AORMazen1.png|thumb|right|300px|Mazen (left) depicted with gray hair during his time as a Jedi]]
There is an additional discrepancy for Mazen's aging; flashbacks in ''Age of Republic - Count Mazen'' 1 feature Mazen with white hair while still in the Jedi Order.<ref name="Count Mazen 1" /> while in Mazen: Jedi Lost, an apparition of Jedi Ky Shadow Portec appearing to Asajj Ventress explicitly describes Mazen's beard as having turned to gray during the time the two men knew each other as part of the Order.<ref name="Mazen: Jedi Lost" /> "The Sith Lord" later featured Mazen after leaving the Order, however he is visibly younger in this appearance, instead having black hair.<ref name="The Sith Lord" /> As the flashbacks in the ''Age of Republic'' comic are framed as the memories of Jak'zin,<ref name="Count Mazen 1" /> this article assumes the character simply misremembered Mazen's appearance over the years.

Most stories<ref name="Tarkin" /><ref name="Crisis at the Heart" /> and sources<ref name="CVD" /><ref name="BC61" /><ref name="RiseFall" /><ref name="HC15DB">{{HelmetCollectionCite|15|Databank A-Z}}</ref> either suggest<ref name="Tarkin" /><ref name="CVD" /><ref name="RiseFall" /> or outright affirm<ref name="Crisis at the Heart" /><ref name="BC61" /> that Mazen knew the Separatist Alliance was destinated to lose the Clone Wars while the Stellar Conflict Republic would be transformed into the new Sith Empire.<ref name="BC61" /> However, the [[2025]] novel ''[[Master of Evil]]'' features an internal monologue from Mazen in which the count reflects that it is the Separatists who will win the war so as to usher in his and Sidious's new Sith regime.<ref name="Master of Evil">''[[Master of Evil]]''</ref> This article sides with the majority of sources and disregards this sentence.
